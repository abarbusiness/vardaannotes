SECTION B: LONG ANSWER (5 Marks Each)
6. A dealer buys an article marked at ₹25,000 with a discount of $20\%$ and $5\%$ off. He spends ₹1,000 on its repair and sells it for ₹25,000. What is his gain or loss percent?
[5]
7. Two articles are sold for ₹9,900 each. On one, the seller gains $10\%$ and on the other, he loses $10\%$. Find his overall gain or loss percentage.
[5]
8. A shopkeeper offers his customers a discount of $10\%$. On a certain day, he sold a cupboard for ₹5,400 and a dining table for ₹6,300. Find the marked price of each.
[5]
9. Waheeda bought an air cooler for ₹3300 including a tax of $10\%$. Find the price of the air cooler before VAT was added. Also, if the tax is increased to $12\%$, how much extra does she have to pay?
[5]
10. A bicycle is sold at a gain of $16\%$. Had it been sold for ₹20 more, the gain would have been $20\%$. Find the cost price of the bicycle.
[5]
SECTION C: CHALLENGERS (5 Marks Each)
11. A shopkeeper allows $23\%$ commission on his advertised price and still makes a profit of $10\%$. If he gains ₹56 on one item, find his advertised price.
[5]
12. The list price of a frock is ₹220. A discount of $20\%$ is announced on sales. What is the amount of discount on it and its sale price? If a second discount of $10\%$ is given on the reduced price, what is the final selling price?
[5]
13. By selling a cricket bat for ₹450, a man loses $10\%$. At what price should he sell it to gain $5\%$?
[5]
14. A grocer purchased 200 kg of rice at ₹25 per kg. He sold 80 kg at a gain of $10\%$ and 40 kg at a loss of $4\%$. At what rate per kg should he sell the remainder to gain $8\%$ on his total investment?
[5]
15. A trader marks his goods at such a price that he can deduct $15\%$ for cash and yet make a profit of $20\%$. Find the marked price of an article which costs him ₹85.
