Air is everywhere ‚Äî inside buildings, in water, in soil, in our lungs. Although we cannot see it, air is a mixture of gases that surrounds the Earth. This blanket of air around the Earth is called the atmosphere.

Key facts about air:
  • Air has NO colour, NO smell, NO taste
  • Air occupies space (fill a balloon!)
  • Air has weight/mass ‚Äî it exerts pressure (atmospheric pressure)
  • Air can be compressed (pressed into smaller space) ‚Äî e.g., bicycle tyres, scuba tanks
  • Air also exists in soil, water, and within living organisms

üî• 3. Role of Air in Burning (Combustion)

Oxygen in air is necessary for burning. Without oxygen, nothing can burn.

Activity proof: Cover a burning candle with a glass jar ‚Üí it goes out within a few seconds. Why? The candle uses up all the oxygen inside the jar. Once oxygen is used up, combustion stops ‚Üí flame extinguishes.

Fire triangle: For anything to burn, you need:
(1) Fuel (wood, paper, petrol) + (2) Oxygen (from air) + (3) Heat (ignition) ‚Üí Fire!
Remove any ONE of these ‚Üí fire goes out!

üåø 4. Role of Air in Respiration and Photosynthesis

Respiration (All living things):
  • Animals and plants take in Oxygen (O‚ÇÇ) and use it to break down glucose ‚Üí release energy
  • They release Carbon Dioxide (CO‚ÇÇ) as waste

Photosynthesis (Green plants only):
  • Plants absorb CO‚ÇÇ from air through stomata
  • Using sunlight energy, they combine CO‚ÇÇ + water ‚Üí glucose + Oxygen (O‚ÇÇ)
  • The oxygen released by plants is what we breathe! This is why plants are called the "lungs of the Earth"
üîÑ The Oxygen-CO‚ÇÇ Balance Animals ‚Üí breathe O‚ÇÇ, release CO‚ÇÇ. Plants ‚Üí absorb CO‚ÇÇ (day), release O‚ÇÇ. This is nature's perfect balance! Cutting forests destroys this balance ‚Äî less CO‚ÇÇ absorption, more global warming, less O‚ÇÇ.

üêü 5. Air in Water and Soil

Air dissolved in water: Fish and other aquatic animals breathe using gills that extract dissolved oxygen from water. This oxygen entered the water by dissolving from the air above. When you boil water, you see bubbles ‚Äî these are dissolved air escaping!
Air in soil: Soil contains air in tiny pores (gaps) between soil particles. Earthworms and other soil organisms breathe this air. When it rains heavily, these pores fill with water ‚Üí earthworms come out to the surface to breathe!

üåç 6. The Atmosphere ‚Äî Air's Protective Role

  • The atmosphere protects Earth from harmful ultraviolet (UV) rays from the Sun (via the ozone layer)
  • The atmosphere keeps Earth warm enough for life (greenhouse effect ‚Äî in moderate amounts)
  • Air resistance (drag) slows down falling objects and burns up meteors before they hit Earth
  • Clouds (made of water vapour in air) cause rainfall ‚Üí essential for life

Air Pollution

Causes: Vehicle exhaust (CO, CO‚ÇÇ, NOx), industrial smoke, burning of garbage, construction dust, burning of fossil fuels, crop burning
Effects: Smog, respiratory diseases (asthma, lung cancer), global warming, acid rain, ozone layer depletion
Solutions: Use public transport, plant trees, use CNG/electric vehicles, avoid burning garbage, use cleaner fuels
