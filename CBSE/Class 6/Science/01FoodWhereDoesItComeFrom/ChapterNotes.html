Every living being ‚Äî humans, animals, birds, and even tiny insects ‚Äî needs food to survive. Food gives us energy to work, play, and think. It also helps our body grow, repair itself when injured, and fight diseases.

Food provides us:
  • ‚ö° Energy ‚Äî to do all activities (walking, running, studying)

üåø 2. Food Variety ‚Äî What Do People Eat?

People across India and the world eat very different types of food. For example:

  • In Punjab ‚Äî people eat a lot of roti (wheat bread) and dal (lentils)
  • In West Bengal and Odisha ‚Äî people eat lots of rice and fish
  • In Rajasthan ‚Äî bajra (pearl millet) roti with ker-sangri (desert vegetables) is common
  • In Kerala ‚Äî rice, coconut, and seafood are staples

This variety happens because of differences in climate, geography, culture, and crop availability.

üåæ 3. Where Does Our Food Come From?

All food we eat comes from either plants or animals. There is no food that comes from both or from somewhere else!

üå± A. Food from Plants

Plants are the most important source of food. We eat different parts of plants:

Part of Plant Examples of Food
Root Carrot, radish (mooli), beetroot, sweet potato, turnip (shalgam)
Stem Potato (underground stem), sugarcane, ginger (rhizome), lotus stem (nadru)
Leaf Spinach (palak), cabbage, lettuce, coriander, fenugreek (methi)
Flower Cauliflower (gobi), banana flower, broccoli, pumpkin flower
Fruit Mango, apple, tomato, cucumber, pea (in pod)
Seed Wheat, rice, maize (corn), chickpea (chana), mustard

Tomato is a fruit (botanically), not a vegetable.
Ginger is a rhizome (modified underground stem).
Broccoli and Cauliflower ‚Äî we eat the flower of the plant!

üåæ 5. Ingredients and Sources of Common Foods

When we cook food, we use different ingredients. An ingredient is anything that goes into making a dish. Each ingredient comes from either a plant or an animal.

Example: Dal (Lentil Soup)
  • Lentils (dal) ‚Üí seeds of the lentil plant
  • Oil ‚Üí from mustard, sunflower, or groundnut plant
  • Tomato ‚Üí fruit of the tomato plant
  • Salt ‚Üí mineral (not from plant or animal)
  • Spices (cumin, turmeric) ‚Üí seeds, roots of plants
Example: Kheer (Rice Pudding)
  • Rice ‚Üí seed of the rice plant
  • Milk ‚Üí from cow or buffalo (animal)
  • Sugar ‚Üí from sugarcane (plant stem)
  • Cardamom ‚Üí from cardamom plant (seed/pod)
üêù Special Case: Honey Honey is made by honeybees from the nectar of flowers. But the nectar originally comes from plants (flowers)! So honey comes from an animal (bee) but the bee uses a plant source (flower nectar). Honey is considered an animal product.

üîë 6. Key Terms to Remember

  • Ingredient ‚Äî A substance used to make food (e.g., wheat, milk, oil)
  • Herbivore ‚Äî An animal that eats only plants
  • Carnivore ‚Äî An animal that eats only other animals
  • Omnivore ‚Äî An animal that eats both plants and animals
  • Nectar ‚Äî The sweet liquid found in flowers, collected by bees to make honey
  • Sprouts ‚Äî Seeds (like moong dal) that germinate (begin to grow) and are eaten as food
  • Edible ‚Äî Fit to be eaten as food (e.g., "edible parts of a plant")

üìù 7. Quick Revision ‚Äî Important Points

  1. All food comes from plants or animals.
  2. We eat different parts of plants ‚Äî roots, stems, leaves, flowers, fruits, and seeds.
  3. Animals provide us food like milk, eggs, honey, and meat.
  4. Herbivores eat only plants. (Cow, horse, rabbit)
  5. Carnivores eat only animals. (Lion, eagle, tiger)
  6. Omnivores eat both plants and animals. (Humans, dogs, bears)
  7. Honey is an animal product made by honeybees from flower nectar.
  8. Potato is a modified underground stem (tuber), NOT a root.
  9. Food gives us energy, helps in growth, repair, and protection from diseases.
