🍽️ 1. Introduction — Why Do We Need Different Foods?

We eat many different kinds of food every day. But have you ever wondered why we need so many different foods? Why can't we just eat rice all day? The reason is that our body needs many different nutrients to stay healthy, and no single food provides ALL the nutrients we need.

The things that our food is made of — which our body needs — are called components of food or nutrients.

The Main Components (Nutrients) of Food:
  1. Carbohydrates
  2. Proteins
  3. Fats
  4. Vitamins
  5. Minerals
  6. Dietary Fibre (Roughage)
  7. Water

⚡ 2. Carbohydrates — The Energy Givers

Carbohydrates are the main source of energy for our body. They provide the fuel needed to do all activities — running, playing, thinking, and even breathing!

Two Types of Carbohydrates:

1. Starch — A complex carbohydrate found in grains, roots, and some vegetables.
   Examples: Rice, wheat, potato, maize (corn), sweet potato, bread

2. Sugar — A simple carbohydrate that tastes sweet.
   Examples: Sugar (from sugarcane), honey, jaggery (gur), fruits (natural sugars)

🧪 NCERT Activity: Iodine Test for Starch

Method: Put a drop of iodine solution on a food item.
Result:
  • If iodine turns blue-black → Starch is PRESENT
  • If no colour change (stays orange-brown) → Starch is ABSENT
Foods that test positive for starch: Rice, potato, bread, cooked rice
Foods that test negative: Apple, cucumber (mostly water and sugar, not starch)

💪 3. Proteins — The Body Builders

Proteins are called the "body builders" because they help in growth, repair, and maintenance of the body. Every cell in your body contains protein. Children especially need a lot of protein because their bodies are growing rapidly.

Sources of Protein:
  • Animal sources (best quality): Milk, eggs, meat, fish, chicken
  • Plant sources: Dal (lentils), rajma (kidney beans), soyabean, chana (chickpea), peas, nuts (groundnuts, almonds)
* Dal + rice together form a complete protein — that's why this combination is so popular in India!

🧪 NCERT Activity: Biuret Test for Protein

Method: Crush the food into a paste. Add caustic soda (NaOH) solution, then add a drop of copper sulphate (CuSO₄) solution.
Result:
  • If the mixture turns violet/purple → Protein is PRESENT
  • No colour change → Protein is ABSENT

🫒 4. Fats — The Energy Stores

Fats are also an energy source, but they provide more than double the energy compared to the same amount of carbohydrate! Fats are stored in the body and used when extra energy is needed. They also help keep us warm and protect our organs.

Sources of Fat:
  • Animal fats: Butter, ghee, cream, milk, egg yolk, meat, fish liver oil
  • Plant fats/oils: Groundnut oil, sunflower oil, coconut oil, mustard oil, soyabean oil, olive oil, nuts, seeds

🧪 NCERT Activity: Paper Test for Fat

Method: Rub the food on a piece of paper, let it dry, then hold it against light.
Result:
  • If the paper shows a greasy/oily patch (translucent) → Fat is PRESENT
  • If only a water stain (which dries away) → Fat is ABSENT
When you rub a groundnut on paper and dry it, the paper becomes translucent (see-through). This is proof of fat!

⚠️ Important Difference: Carbohydrates vs Fats Both carbohydrates and fats give energy.
But 1 gram of fat = ~9 kcal (more energy than carbohydrates).
1 gram of carbohydrate = ~4 kcal
Too much fat stored → can cause obesity and heart problems.

🌈 5. Vitamins — The Protective Foods

Vitamins are needed in very small amounts, but they are extremely important for protecting the body from diseases and keeping it healthy. They are called "protective foods".

There are two groups of vitamins: Fat-soluble (dissolve in fat) and Water-soluble (dissolve in water).

Vitamin Other Name Sources Function Deficiency Disease
Vitamin A Retinol Carrot, mango, milk, egg, liver, fish oil Keeps eyes and skin healthy Night Blindness (cannot see in dim light)
Vitamin B (B1, B2, B12...) Whole grains, dal, meat, eggs, yeast Helps nervous system and energy release Beri-beri (weak muscles, nerve damage); Anaemia
Vitamin C Ascorbic acid Amla, orange, lemon, guava, tomato Heals wounds, keeps gums healthy, boosts immunity Scurvy (gums bleed, teeth fall out)
Vitamin D Calciferol Sunlight (skin makes it!), fish liver oil, eggs, milk Helps body absorb calcium for strong bones and teeth Rickets (soft, bent bones in children)
Vitamin K — Green leafy vegetables, liver Helps in blood clotting Excessive bleeding from wounds
🎯 Memory Trick for Vitamins Fat-soluble vitamins: A, D, E, K → "All Dogs Eat Kibble"
Water-soluble vitamins: B and C

For deficiency diseases:
A → Night blindness (can't see at night → "A" for "Absence of sight")
C → Scurvy (C = "Citrus" prevents it → amla, lemon)
D → Rickets (D = "Dense bones needed")
B1 → Beri-Beri

🦴 6. Minerals — Essential for Body Functions

Like vitamins, minerals are needed in small amounts but are vital for health. Minerals keep our bones, teeth, and blood healthy.

Mineral Sources Function Deficiency
Calcium Milk, curd, paneer, cheese, ragi, fish Strong bones and teeth, muscle contraction Weak bones, tooth decay
Iron Spinach, dal, jaggery, meat, liver, eggs Makes haemoglobin in blood (carries oxygen) Anaemia (tiredness, pale skin)
Iodine Iodised salt, seafood, seaweed Production of thyroid hormones (controls growth) Goitre (swelling of neck/thyroid gland)
Phosphorus Milk, eggs, fish, nuts Bone and teeth formation, energy Weak bones
🩸 Anaemia vs Goitre — Common Exam Question Anaemia = deficiency of Iron → less haemoglobin → less oxygen in blood → tiredness, weakness, pale skin, dizziness
Goitre = deficiency of Iodine → thyroid gland swells → visible lump in neck

🌿 7. Dietary Fibre (Roughage)

Roughage is the part of plant food that our body cannot digest. It has no nutritional value, but it is extremely important for healthy digestion.

What does Roughage do?
  • Helps food move smoothly through the digestive system
  • Prevents constipation (difficulty passing stool)
  • Helps clean our intestines
  • Reduces risk of colon cancer
Sources: Whole wheat, oats, fruits with skin, vegetables (especially leafy greens), bran, sprouts, pulses

💧 8. Water — The Essential Liquid

About 70% of our body weight is water! Water is NOT a nutrient, but it is absolutely essential for life. Without water, no life is possible.

Functions of Water:
  • Dissolves food and carries nutrients to all body parts (through blood)
  • Removes waste from the body (through urine and sweat)
  • Regulates body temperature (through sweating)
  • Lubricates joints
  • Needed for all chemical reactions in the body
We should drink 8–10 glasses of water per day. We also get water from fruits, vegetables, and other liquids.

🥗 9. Balanced Diet — Eating the Right Amount of Everything

A balanced diet is a diet that contains all the nutrients in the right amounts — not too much, not too little. It gives the body everything it needs for energy, growth, repair, and protection.

A balanced meal plate includes:
  • 🟡 Grains/Carbs — Roti/rice/bread (majority)
  • 🔴 Proteins — Dal, eggs, pulses, milk, fish
  • 🟢 Vegetables — Especially greens (vitamins and minerals)
  • 🟠 Fruits — Fresh seasonal fruits (vitamins, water, fibre)
  • 🟤 Fats — Small amounts of oil, ghee, nuts
  • 💧 Water — 8–10 glasses a day

🏥 10. Deficiency Diseases

When the body does not get enough of a particular nutrient for a long time, it leads to a deficiency disease.

Night Blindness

Deficiency of Vitamin A. Person cannot see properly in dim light or darkness. Eating carrots, milk, and green leafy vegetables helps prevent this.

Scurvy

Deficiency of Vitamin C. Gums bleed easily, teeth may fall out, wounds don't heal properly. Prevented by eating citrus fruits (lemon, amla, orange).

Rickets

Deficiency of Vitamin D in children. Bones become soft and bend. Legs may become bow-shaped. Sunlight exposure and fish liver oil help prevent it.

Beri-Beri

Deficiency of Vitamin B1 (Thiamine). Causes weakness of muscles, swelling, and nerve damage. Found in people who eat only polished (white) rice.

Anaemia

Deficiency of Iron. Body cannot make enough haemoglobin → less oxygen in blood → extreme tiredness, weakness, pale skin. Eat spinach, jaggery, meat.

Goitre

Deficiency of Iodine. Thyroid gland in the neck swells up, causing a visible bulge on the neck. Using iodised salt in cooking prevents goitre.

Kwashiorkor

Severe deficiency of Protein (mostly in young children). Symptoms: swollen belly, stunted growth, loss of hair colour, weakness. Common in developing countries.

📝 11. Quick Revision

  1. Food has 7 main components: Carbohydrates, Proteins, Fats, Vitamins, Minerals, Roughage, Water.
  2. Carbohydrates and fats → provide energy. Carbs are tested with iodine (turns blue-black for starch).
  3. Proteins → growth and repair. Tested with biuret test (turns violet).
  4. Fats → energy store. Tested with paper test (greasy patch).
  5. Vitamins and Minerals → protection. Needed in small amounts but critical for health.
  6. Roughage → helps in digestion, prevents constipation.
  7. Water → 70% of body; essential for all body functions.
  8. A balanced diet contains all nutrients in the right proportions.
  9. Lack of nutrients → Deficiency diseases (Night blindness, Scurvy, Rickets, Goitre, Anaemia).
