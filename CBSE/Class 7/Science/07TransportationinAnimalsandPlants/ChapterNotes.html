The circulatory system is the body's transport network. It carries oxygen, nutrients, hormones, and waste products through the blood to all body cells and removes waste products.

Main Components of the Circulatory System:

  • ü´Ä Heart ‚Äî the pump (about the size of your fist, located in the chest cavity)
  • üî¥ Blood ‚Äî the transport fluid (5‚Äì6 litres in an adult human)
  • ü©∏ Blood vessels:
    • Arteries ‚Äî carry blood away from heart; thick walls, high pressure, carry oxygenated blood (except pulmonary artery)
    • Veins ‚Äî carry blood to heart; thin walls, low pressure, carry deoxygenated blood (except pulmonary vein)
    • Capillaries ‚Äî microscopic vessels; connect arteries to veins; one cell thick ‚Üí allow exchange of O‚ÇÇ, nutrients, CO‚ÇÇ, waste between blood and cells

üåø 3. Transportation in Plants

Plants also have a transport system but it uses two separate tubes instead of a pump:

XYLEM üíß
Carries water + minerals from roots
Direction: upward only (roots ‚Üí leaves)
Force: root pressure + transpiration pull
One-way transport (no pump needed)
PHLOEM üç¨
Carries food (sucrose/glucose)
Direction: both up and down (leaves ‚Üí all plant parts)
From leaves ‚Üí roots, fruits, seeds, growing buds
This transport is called translocation

Transpiration ‚Äî Water Loss from Leaves

Transpiration = evaporation of water from leaves through stomata. This water loss creates a "pull" (negative pressure) that draws water upward through the xylem from roots. This is called transpiration pull ‚Äî the main force that moves water up tall trees!

Advantages of Transpiration: Cools the plant; helps absorb minerals from soil; creates water circulation in plants

üíß 4. Excretion in Humans ‚Äî Waste Removal

  • The kidneys filter blood and remove waste + excess water as urine (contains urea, uric acid, salts, water)
  • Urine flows through ureters ‚Üí urinary bladder (stored) ‚Üí excreted through urethra
  • Sweat glands in skin also remove some waste (urea, salts) through sweat
  • Lungs remove CO‚ÇÇ via exhalation
  • Liver converts toxic ammonia into less-toxic urea (which kidneys then remove)

üìù 5. Quick Revision

  1. Arteries: carry blood away from heart (oxygenated). Veins: carry blood to heart (deoxygenated). Capillaries: exchange site
  2. Heart rate = ~72 beats/minute. Stethoscope hears "lub-dub" sounds
  3. Blood = RBCs (O‚ÇÇ transport, haemoglobin) + WBCs (fight infection) + Platelets (clotting) + Plasma (nutrients)
  4. Xylem = water + minerals, upward only. Phloem = food (sucrose), upward + downward (translocation)
  5. Transpiration = water evaporation from stomata ‚Üí creates pull ‚Üí water moves upward through xylem
  6. Kidneys filter blood ‚Üí urine (water + urea + salts). Lungs excrete CO‚ÇÇ. Skin excretes sweat
