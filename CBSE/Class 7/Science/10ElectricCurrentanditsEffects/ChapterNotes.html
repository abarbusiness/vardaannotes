üß≤ 3. Electromagnet ‚Äî How It Works

An electromagnet is a temporary magnet made by passing electric current through a coil of wire wound around an iron core.

How to make an electromagnet:
  1. Take an iron nail (the core)
  2. Wind insulated copper wire (many turns/coils) around it ‚Äî this is called a solenoid
  3. Connect the ends of the wire to a battery through a switch
  4. When switch is closed ‚Üí current flows ‚Üí nail becomes a temporary magnet
  5. When switch is opened ‚Üí current stops ‚Üí nail loses magnetism immediately

Strength of electromagnet increases when you:
  • Increase the number of turns/coils of wire
  • Increase the current (use more cells/batteries)
  • Use an iron core (instead of air core)

üõ°Ô∏è 5. Fuse ‚Äî Safety Device

A fuse is a short piece of thin wire (made of a metal with a low melting point, e.g. copper-tin alloy) connected in series in a circuit. If too much current flows (short circuit, overloading) ‚Üí fuse wire gets very hot ‚Üí melts and breaks ‚Üí circuit opens ‚Üí appliances are protected from damage.

Modern alternative: MCB (Miniature Circuit Breaker) ‚Äî automatically switches off when current exceeds safe limit; can be reset (reusable), unlike fuse wire (must be replaced each time it blows).

üìù 6. Quick Revision

  1. A circuit needs: Cell, Switch, Bulb/Resistor, Connecting Wires in a closed loop
  2. Heating effect: Current generates heat in resistance wire. E.g. electric iron, bulb filament, fuse
  3. Magnetic effect: Current through a coil creates a magnetic field ‚Üí Electromagnet
  4. Chemical effect: Electrolysis ‚Äî current through a solution causes chemical change ‚Üí Electroplating
  5. Electromagnet: iron nail + wire coil + current ‚Üí temporary magnet; stronger with more coils, more current
  6. Electric bell: electromagnet repeatedly attracts and releases armature ‚Üí continuous ringing
  7. Fuse: thin wire with low melting point; melts if excess current flows ‚Üí protects circuit
  8. MCB = modern replaceable fuse; can be reset without replacing wire
