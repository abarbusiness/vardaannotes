‚öñÔ∏è 1. What is an Equation?

An equation is a mathematical statement that says two expressions are equal. It always has an equals (=) sign.

Expression Equation Difference
2x + 3 2x + 3 = 9 Expression has no = sign; Equation has = sign
3y ‚àí 5 3y ‚àí 5 = 10 An equation has both LHS and RHS
Parts of an Equation:
‚Ä¢ Variable (unknown): the letter to find (x, y, n, etc.)
‚Ä¢ LHS (Left-Hand Side): expression before the = sign
‚Ä¢ RHS (Right-Hand Side): expression after the = sign
‚Ä¢ Solution: the value of the variable that makes LHS = RHS (also called ROOT of the equation)

‚öñÔ∏è 2. The Balance Method ‚Äî Core Concept

Think of an equation as a balance scale. Both sides must stay balanced. Whatever you do to one side, do the SAME to the other side!

üîë 3. Methods to Solve Equations

Method 1: Transposition (Moving terms across = sign)

üîë Golden Rule of Transposition When you move a term from LHS to RHS (or vice versa), its sign is reversed:
‚Ä¢ Addition ‚Üî Subtraction when crossing the = sign
‚Ä¢ Multiplication ‚Üî Division when crossing the = sign
‚úèÔ∏è Solve: 2x + 5 = 13
2x + 5 = 13
2x = 13 ‚àí 5     (transposing +5 from LHS ‚Üí becomes ‚àí5 on RHS)
2x = 8
x = 8 √∑ 2     (transposing √ó2 from LHS ‚Üí becomes √∑2 on RHS)
x = 4
‚úÖ Check: 2(4)+5 = 8+5 = 13 ‚úì
‚úèÔ∏è Solve: 3(y ‚àí 2) = 12
3(y ‚àí 2) = 12
y ‚àí 2 = 12 √∑ 3 = 4
y = 4 + 2
y = 6
‚úÖ Check: 3(6‚àí2) = 3(4) = 12 ‚úì
‚úèÔ∏è Solve: (n+3)/5 = 4
(n+3)/5 = 4
n + 3 = 4 √ó 5 = 20     (multiply both sides by 5)
n = 20 ‚àí 3
n = 17
‚úÖ Check: (17+3)/5 = 20/5 = 4 ‚úì

Method 2: Trial and Error (for simple equations)

Example: Solve 5x ‚àí 3 = 12. Try different values of x:
x=2: 5(2)‚àí3 = 7 ‚â† 12
x=3: 5(3)‚àí3 = 12 ‚úì ‚Üí Solution: x = 3
(Better to use transposition for complex equations!)

üìù 4. Setting Up Equations from Word Problems

Steps:
  1. Identify the unknown ‚Äî give it a variable (e.g., x)
  2. Translate the words into an equation ‚Äî "is" ‚Üí "=", "sum" ‚Üí "+", etc.
  3. Solve the equation
  4. Verify the answer in the original question

üìù Word Problem: "The sum of three consecutive odd numbers is 51. Find the numbers."

Numbers: 15, 17, 19
‚úÖ Check: 15+17+19 = 51 ‚úì

üìù Quick Revision

  1. An equation: Mathematical statement with LHS = RHS containing an unknown variable
  2. Solution = value that makes LHS = RHS. Always VERIFY your solution!
  3. Transposition: Move term across = sign with reversed sign (+ becomes ‚àí, √ó becomes √∑)
  4. Whatever you do to one side, do the SAME to the other side (balance method)

  6. Consecutive numbers: n, n+1, n+2. Consecutive even/odd: n, n+2, n+4
