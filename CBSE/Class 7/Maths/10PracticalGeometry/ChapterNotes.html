A unique triangle can be constructed if we are given enough information. The following conditions are sufficient:

Conditions for unique triangle construction:
  • SSS ‚Äî All 3 sides given
  • SAS ‚Äî 2 sides and included angle given
  • ASA ‚Äî 2 angles and included side given
  • AAS ‚Äî 2 angles and a non-included side
  • RHS ‚Äî Right angle, hypotenuse, and one side (for right triangles)
Note: AAA does not give unique triangle (infinite triangles possible). SSA is ambiguous (0, 1 or 2 triangles possible).

Construction 1 ‚Äî SSS (All three sides given)

üìê Construct ‚ñ≥ABC with AB=5cm, BC=6cm, AC=4cm
  1. Draw base BC = 6 cm using ruler.
  2. With B as centre and radius = 5 cm (=AB), draw an arc above BC.
  3. With C as centre and radius = 4 cm (=AC), draw another arc cutting the previous arc at A.
  4. Join AB and AC. ‚ñ≥ABC is ready.

Construction 2 ‚Äî SAS (Two sides + included angle)

üìê Construct ‚ñ≥PQR with PQ=4cm, ‚à†Q=60¬∞, QR=5cm

  2. At Q, draw ‚à†Q = 60¬∞ using protractor. Draw ray QX.
  3. On ray QX, mark P at 4 cm from Q (PQ = 4cm).
  4. Join PR. ‚ñ≥PQR is ready.

Construction 3 ‚Äî ASA (Two angles + included side)

üìê Construct ‚ñ≥LMN with ‚à†L=60¬∞, LM=5cm, ‚à†M=75¬∞
  1. Draw base LM = 5 cm.
  2. At L, draw angle = 60¬∞ (draw ray LX).
  3. At M, draw angle = 75¬∞ (draw ray MY).
  4. The two rays LX and MY intersect at N. ‚ñ≥LMN is ready.
  5. Note: ‚à†N = 180¬∞ ‚àí 60¬∞ ‚àí 75¬∞ = 45¬∞.

üìù Quick Revision

  1. Tools: Ruler (length), Compass (arcs), Protractor (angles), Set-square (right angles/parallels)
  2. 5 construction criteria: SSS, SAS, ASA, AAS, RHS ‚Üí each gives a unique triangle
  3. AAA ‚Üí NOT unique (similar triangles). SSA ‚Üí ambiguous (0, 1, or 2 triangles possible)

  5. Triangle possible only if sum of any two sides > third side
