Ratio: Comparison of two quantities of the same type by division. Ratio of a to b = a:b = a/b

Proportion: Two ratios are equal: a:b = c:d ‚Üí a√ód = b√óc (cross-multiplication)
E.g., Is 3:4 = 9:12? ‚Üí 3√ó12 = 36 and 4√ó9 = 36 ‚úì Yes, they are in proportion.

üìà 3. Profit and Loss

Term Formula When?
Profit Profit = SP ‚àí CP (when SP > CP) Selling price more than cost price
Loss Loss = CP ‚àí SP (when CP > SP) Cost price more than selling price
Profit % (Profit/CP) √ó 100 Always calculated on CP
Loss % (Loss/CP) √ó 100 Always calculated on CP
SP (if profit % known) SP = CP √ó (100 + Profit%)/100 Finding selling price
SP (if loss % known) SP = CP √ó (100 ‚àí Loss%)/100 Finding selling price
CP (if SP and Profit% known) CP = SP √ó 100/(100 + Profit%) Finding cost price

üè¶ 4. Simple Interest (SI)

SI = (P √ó R √ó T) / 100    |    Amount = P + SI
Symbol Meaning Common units
P Principal (original sum borrowed/invested) Rupees (‚Çπ)
R Rate of Interest per annum % per year (p.a.)
T Time period Years (convert months: T = months/12)
SI Simple Interest earned/paid Rupees (‚Çπ)
A Amount = Principal + SI Rupees (‚Çπ)
Find P
P = (SI √ó 100) / (R √ó T)
Find R
R = (SI √ó 100) / (P √ó T)
Find T
T = (SI √ó 100) / (P √ó R)
Find A
A = P + SI = P(1 + RT/100)
Examples:
1. P=‚Çπ5000, R=8% p.a., T=3 yrs. SI = (5000√ó8√ó3)/100 = ‚Çπ1200. Amount = 5000+1200 = ‚Çπ6200
2. SI=‚Çπ600, P=‚Çπ4000, T=2 yrs. R = (600√ó100)/(4000√ó2) = 7.5% p.a.
3. A=‚Çπ7000, P=‚Çπ5000, R=10% p.a. SI=2000. T = (2000√ó100)/(5000√ó10) = 4 years

üìù Quick Revision

  1. Ratio a:b = a/b. In proportion a:b = c:d, ad = bc (cross multiply)
  2. Fraction to % ‚Üí multiply by 100. % to fraction ‚Üí divide by 100
  3. Profit/Loss % always calculated on COST PRICE (CP)
  4. SP = CP √ó (100 ¬± P or L%) / 100. CP = SP √ó 100 / (100 ¬± P or L%)
