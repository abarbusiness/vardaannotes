üîé 1. What is Congruence?

Two figures are congruent (‚âÖ) if they have the same shape AND same size. When superimposed (placed on top of each other), they match exactly.

Congruent vs. Similar:
‚Ä¢ Congruent (‚âÖ): Same shape + SAME size. Example: Two identical stamps.
‚Ä¢ Similar (~): Same shape, DIFFERENT size (covered in higher classes). Example: A photo and its enlargement.
Corresponding Parts: When two triangles are congruent, their CPCT hold true:
CPCT = Corresponding Parts of Congruent Triangles (are equal)
If ‚ñ≥ABC ‚âÖ ‚ñ≥PQR, then: AB=PQ, BC=QR, AC=PR and ‚à†A=‚à†P, ‚à†B=‚à†Q, ‚à†C=‚à†R

üìã 2. Criteria for Congruence of Triangles

SSS
Side-Side-Side

All three sides of one triangle equal to corresponding three sides of the other triangle. No angle needed!

SAS
Side-Angle-Side

Two sides AND the included angle (the angle between the two sides) are equal. Angle must be BETWEEN the two sides!

ASA
Angle-Side-Angle

Two angles AND the included side (side between the two angles) are equal.

AAS
Angle-Angle-Side

Two angles and a non-included side are equal. (Since 3rd angle can be derived, this works!)

RHS
Right Angle-Hypotenuse-Side

Only for RIGHT triangles: Right angle + Hypotenuse + one leg are equal.

‚ö†Ô∏è What does NOT prove congruence ‚Ä¢ AAA (Angle-Angle-Angle) ‚Äî proves SIMILARITY only, not congruence! (Triangles could be different sizes)
‚Ä¢ SSA (Side-Side-Angle) ‚Äî DOES NOT work! The angle must be included (SAS) or hypotenuse (RHS) for right triangles.

‚úèÔ∏è 3. Worked Examples

Example 1: In ‚ñ≥ABC and ‚ñ≥DEF: AB=DE=5cm, BC=EF=7cm, AC=DF=9cm. Are they congruent?
All three sides are equal. So by SSS criterion, ‚ñ≥ABC ‚âÖ ‚ñ≥DEF. By CPCT, ‚à†A=‚à†D, ‚à†B=‚à†E, ‚à†C=‚à†F.

Example 2: In ‚ñ≥PQR and ‚ñ≥XYZ: PQ=XY=6cm, ‚à†P=‚à†X=40¬∞, PR=XZ=8cm. Are they congruent?
Two sides and the included angle (‚à†P is between PQ and PR). By SAS criterion, ‚ñ≥PQR ‚âÖ ‚ñ≥XYZ.

Example 3: In ‚ñ≥ABC, AB=AC (isosceles) and AD bisects ‚à†A. Prove ‚ñ≥ABD ‚âÖ ‚ñ≥ACD.
In ‚ñ≥ABD and ‚ñ≥ACD: AB=AC (given), ‚à†BAD=‚à†CAD (AD bisects ‚à†A), AD=AD (common side).
‚Üí By SAS, ‚ñ≥ABD ‚âÖ ‚ñ≥ACD. So BD=CD (CPCT) ‚Äî AD bisects BC! (Median and angle bisector coincide in isosceles triangle!)

üìù Quick Revision

  1. Congruent figures: same shape AND same size (‚âÖ symbol); CPCT ‚Äî corresponding parts of congruent triangles are equal
  2. SSS: 3 sides equal | SAS: 2 sides + included (between them) angle equal
  3. ASA: 2 angles + included (between them) side equal | AAS: 2 angles + any side
  4. RHS: Right angle + Hypotenuse + one Side (for right triangles only)
  5. ‚ùå AAA proves similarity, NOT congruence | ‚ùå SSA is not a congruence criterion
  6. In proof: State given, state what we need to prove, write "In ‚ñ≥___ and ‚ñ≥___:", list equal parts, state which criterion, conclude congruence, then use CPCT if needed
