Writing Skills (Format & Tips)

1. Descriptive Paragraph (Person/Place/Event)

Goal: Describe a person, place, or event vividly using sensory details.
Word Limit: 100-120 words.

Title (Optional but good) Introduction: Introduce the person/place/event. Who/Where/What is it? Body: - Person: Physical appearance (eyes, height, clothes), Personality (kind, rude, helpful), Habits. - Place: Location, Sights, Sounds, Atmosphere/Vibe. - Event: Occasion, Decorations, Activities, Feelings. Conclusion: Your personal opinion or final thought. Why is this person/place/event special to you?

2. Diary Entry

Tone: Informal, personal, and emotional. Write as if talking to a friend.
Tense: Mostly Past Tense (describing what happened) + Future for hopes (e.g., "I hope to...").

Date: 20th February 2025 Day: Thursday Time: 9:00 PM (Usually night) Dear Diary, Opening: (Express emotion) - "I am so happy/sad/shocked today because..." or "What a terrible day it was!" Body: Describe the event. Who did what? How did you feel? Be honest. Use "I", "My". Conclusion: (Future hope/Final thought) - "I hope tomorrow will be better." or "Good night now, I am sleepy." (Signature/Name) Ankit

3. Story Writing

Structure: Beginning -> Middle (Climax/Problem) -> End (Resolution).
Cues: Always include the given starting line or hints.

TITLE (Catchy & Relevant, e.g., "The Hidden Treasure") **(Start with the given cue if any)** Once upon a time... (Introduction of characters & setting). Plot/Action: Introduce a problem or twist. The character faces a challenge. Build suspense. Climax: The most exciting part where the problem is at its peak. Conclusion: The problem is solved. Happy/Sad ending. Moral: (Optional, write only if asked or fits well) - "Greed is a curse."
