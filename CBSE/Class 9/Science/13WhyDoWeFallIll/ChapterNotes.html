Chapter Overview:

"Health is wealth." This chapter distinguishes between 'Healthy' and 'Disease-free', classifies diseases (Acute/Chronic, Infectious/Non-infectious), explains their causes (Pathogens), modes of transmission, and principles of Treatment and Prevention (Immunization).

1. Health and Disease

Health (WHO): A state of complete physical, mental, and social well-being.

Disease-Free: Absence of discomfort or derangement of body function.

Difference:

  • A person may be disease-free but not healthy (e.g., a musician who cannot breathe enough to play flute is not 'healthy' though he has no disease).

2. Types of Diseases

Based on Duration:

  • Acute Diseases: Last for very short time. No long term effect on health. (e.g., Common Cold, Typhoid).
  • Chronic Diseases: Last for long time (even lifetime). Drastic long term effects (e.g., Diabetes, Cancer, Elephantiasis).

Based on Cause:

  • Infectious (Communicable): Caused by microbes (Pathogens). Spread from infected to healthy person. (e.g., Malaria, COVID-19).
  • Non-Infectious (Non-communicable): Not caused by microbes. Do not spread. (e.g., High BP, Cancer).

3. Infectious Agents (Pathogens)

Agent Diseases Caused
Bacteria Typhoid, Cholera, Tuberculosis (TB), Anthrax.
Virus Common Cold, Influenza, Dengue, AIDS, COVID-19.
Protozoa Malaria (Plasmodium), Kala-azar (Leishmania).
Fungi Skin infections (Ringworm).
Worms Elephantiasis, Ascariasis.

4. Means of Spread

  • Air: Coughing/Sneezing releases droplets. (e.g., TB, Pneumonia).
  • Water: Excreta mixes with drinking water. (e.g., Cholera).
  • Vectors: Animals carry pathogen. (e.g., Mosquitoes - Anopheles for Malaria).
  • Sexual Contact: Syphilis, AIDS.

5. Principles of Treatment

  1. Reduce the effects (Symptomatic): Medicines to bring down fever, reduce pain. Bed rest to conserve energy.
  2. Kill the cause: Using specific medicines (Antibiotics for bacteria, Antivirals for viruses) to kill the microbe.
    Note: Antibiotics block bacterial cell wall synthesis. They do not work on viruses.

6. Principles of Prevention

"Prevention is better than Cure".

General Ways:

  • Safe drinking water.
  • Proper nutrition (Immune system needs food).
  • Public hygiene (Clean environment).

Specific Ways (Immunization):

Vaccination: Introducing a weak/dead microbe into body to fool the immune system into producing antibodies and memory cells. Next time actual microbe enters, body kills it immediately.

Edward Jenner invented vaccine (Smallpox).

Practice Zone

Q1: Why are antibiotics not effective against viral infections?

Ans: Viruses do not have their own biochemical pathways (like cell wall synthesis) which antibiotics block in bacteria. Viruses use host machinery.

Q2: Name one acute and one chronic disease.

Ans: Acute: Common Cold. Chronic: Tuberculosis (or Diabetes).
