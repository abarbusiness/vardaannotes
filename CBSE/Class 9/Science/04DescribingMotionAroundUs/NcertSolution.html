NCERT Solutions: Chapter 4

1. My father went to a shop from home which is located at a distance of 250 m on a straight road. On reaching there, he discovered that he forgot to carry a cloth bag. He came home to take it, went to the shop again, bought provisions and came back home. How much was the total distance travelled by him? What was his displacement from home?
Solution:

Distance Travelled: Path taken is Home → Shop (250m) → Home (250m) → Shop (250m) → Home (250m). Total distance = 250 + 250 + 250 + 250 = 1000 m.

Displacement: Since his initial position was home and his final position is also home, the net change in position is zero. Displacement = 0 m.

2. A student runs from the ground floor to the fourth floor of a school building to collect a book and then comes down to their classroom on the second floor. If the height of each floor is 3 m, find: (i) the total vertical distance travelled, and (ii) their displacement from the starting point.
Solution:

(i) Total vertical distance: Ground to 4th floor means 4 floors up (4 × 3m = 12m). From 4th floor to 2nd floor means 2 floors down (2 × 3m = 6m). Total vertical distance = 12m + 6m = 18 m.

(ii) Displacement: The student starts at Ground (0m) and ends at the 2nd floor (2 × 3m = 6m). Displacement = 6 m upwards.

3. A girl is riding her scooter and finds that its speedometer reading is constant. Is it possible for her scooter to be accelerating and if so, how?
Solution:

Yes, it is possible. A constant speedometer reading means the speed is constant. However, acceleration is the rate of change of velocity (which includes both speed and direction). If the girl is turning her scooter or riding in a circular path, the direction of her velocity is continuously changing. Therefore, her scooter would be accelerating despite having a constant speed.

4. A car starts from rest and its velocity reaches 24 m s⁻¹ in 6 s. Find the average acceleration and the distance travelled in these 6 s.
Solution:

Given: Initial velocity ($u$) = 0 m/s, Final velocity ($v$) = 24 m/s, Time ($t$) = 6 s.

Acceleration ($a$):
$a = (v - u) / t = (24 - 0) / 6 = 4$ m s⁻².

Distance ($s$): Using the kinematic equation $s = ut + \frac{1}{2}at^2$
$s = (0 \times 6) + \frac{1}{2} \times 4 \times (6)^2 = 0 + 2 \times 36 = 72$ m.

5. A motorbike moving with initial velocity 28 m s⁻¹ and constant acceleration stops after travelling 98 m. Find the acceleration of the motorbike and the time taken to come to a stop.
Solution:

Given: Initial velocity ($u$) = 28 m/s, Final velocity ($v$) = 0 m/s (it stops), Displacement ($s$) = 98 m.

Acceleration ($a$): Using $v^2 = u^2 + 2as$
$0^2 = (28)^2 + 2 \times a \times 98$
$0 = 784 + 196a$
$a = -784 / 196 = -4$ m s⁻².
(The negative sign indicates retardation/braking).

Time ($t$): Using $v = u + at$
$0 = 28 + (-4)t$
$4t = 28 \implies t = 7$ s.

6. Fig. 4.27 shows a position-time graph of two objects A and B that are moving along the parallel tracks in the same direction. Do objects A and B ever have equal velocity? Justify your answer.
Solution:

No, objects A and B never have equal velocity. In a position-time graph, the velocity is given by the slope of the line. Because the lines for A and B are straight lines with different constant slopes (A's line is steeper than B's line), their velocities are constant but different from each other throughout the motion. They intersect at a point, which means they are at the same position at that instant, but their velocities are not equal.

7. A graph in Fig. 4.28 shows the change in position with time for two objects A and B moving in a straight line from 0 to 10 seconds. Choose the correct option(s).
(i) The average velocity of both over the 10 s time interval is equal since they have the same initial and final positions.
(ii) The average speeds of both over the 10 s time interval are equal since both cover equal distance in equal time.
(iii) The average speed of A over the 10 s time interval is lower than that of B since it covers a shorter distance than B in 10 seconds.
(iv) The average speed of A over the 10 s time interval is greater than that of B since B’s speed is lower than A’s in some segments.
Solution:

Based on the graph, both objects A and B start at the same origin at $t=0$ and reach the exact same position at $t=10$ seconds. Since they are moving in a straight line without reversing direction, their displacement and total distance are equal.

  • (i) is correct: Average velocity is displacement/time. Since both displacement and time (10s) are equal for A and B, their average velocities are equal.
  • (ii) is correct: Average speed is distance/time. Since motion is strictly forward, distance equals displacement. Thus, their total distance covered in 10s is identical, making their average speeds equal.

Therefore, options (i) and (ii) are the correct choices.

8. A truck driver driving at the speed of 54 km h⁻¹ notices a road sign with a speed limit of 40 km h⁻¹ for trucks. He slows down to 36 km h⁻¹ in 36 s. What was the distance travelled by him during this time? Assume the acceleration to be constant while slowing down.
Solution:

First, convert velocities to SI units (m/s) by multiplying by $\frac{5}{18}$:
Initial velocity ($u$) = $54 \times \frac{5}{18} = 15$ m/s.
Final velocity ($v$) = $36 \times \frac{5}{18} = 10$ m/s.
Time ($t$) = 36 s.

Acceleration ($a$):
$a = (v - u) / t = (10 - 15) / 36 = -5 / 36$ m s⁻².

Distance ($s$): Using $s = ut + \frac{1}{2}at^2$
$s = (15 \times 36) + \frac{1}{2} \times \left(-\frac{5}{36}\right) \times (36)^2$
$s = 540 + \frac{1}{2} \times (-5) \times 36$
$s = 540 - 90 = 450$ m.
(Alternatively, using $s = \frac{(u+v)}{2} \times t$, $s = \frac{15+10}{2} \times 36 = 12.5 \times 36 = 450$ m).

9. A car starts from rest and accelerates uniformly to 20 m s⁻¹ in 5 seconds. It then travels at 20 m s⁻¹ for 10 seconds and finally applies the brake (with uniform acceleration) to stop in 6 seconds. Find the total distance travelled.
Solution:

The motion consists of three phases. We calculate the distance for each phase:

Phase 1 (Acceleration):
$u_1 = 0$ m/s, $v_1 = 20$ m/s, $t_1 = 5$ s.
$a_1 = (20 - 0) / 5 = 4$ m s⁻².
$s_1 = u_1t_1 + \frac{1}{2}a_1t_1^2 = 0 + \frac{1}{2}(4)(5)^2 = 2 \times 25 = 50$ m.

Phase 2 (Constant Velocity):
$v_2 = 20$ m/s, $t_2 = 10$ s.
$s_2 = v_2 \times t_2 = 20 \times 10 = 200$ m.

Phase 3 (Deceleration to Stop):
$u_3 = 20$ m/s, $v_3 = 0$ m/s, $t_3 = 6$ s.
$a_3 = (0 - 20) / 6 = -10/3$ m s⁻².
$s_3 = u_3t_3 + \frac{1}{2}a_3t_3^2 = (20 \times 6) + \frac{1}{2}\left(-\frac{10}{3}\right)(36) = 120 - 60 = 60$ m.

Total Distance ($s$): $s = s_1 + s_2 + s_3 = 50 + 200 + 60 = 310$ m.

10. A bus is travelling at 36 km h⁻¹ when the driver sees an obstacle 30 m ahead. The driver takes 0.5 seconds to react before pressing the brake. Once the brake is applied, the velocity of the bus reduces with constant acceleration of 2.5 m s⁻². Will the bus be able to stop before reaching the obstacle?
Solution:

Initial velocity ($u$) = $36 \times \frac{5}{18} = 10$ m/s.

Reaction Phase: During the 0.5s reaction time, the bus continues at 10 m/s.
Distance covered ($s_1$) = $10 \times 0.5 = 5$ m.

Braking Phase: The bus decelerates at $a = -2.5$ m s⁻² until it stops ($v = 0$).
Using $v^2 = u^2 + 2as_2$:
$0 = (10)^2 + 2(-2.5)s_2$
$0 = 100 - 5s_2 \implies 5s_2 = 100 \implies s_2 = 20$ m.

Total stopping distance: $s_1 + s_2 = 5 + 20 = 25$ m.
Since the obstacle is 30 m ahead and the bus completely stops in 25 m, yes, the bus will safely stop 5 meters before reaching the obstacle.

11. A student said, “The Earth moves around the Sun”. In this context, discuss whether an object kept on the Earth can be considered to be at rest.
Solution:

Motion and rest are relative concepts, meaning they depend on the chosen reference point (observer).

  • If the reference point is the Earth's surface (like the floor of a room), an object kept on a table is at rest because its position relative to the room does not change with time.
  • However, if the reference point is the Sun or an astronaut in space, the object on Earth is in motion because it is moving along with the Earth at a very high speed.

Therefore, an object on Earth can be considered at rest relative to the Earth, but in motion relative to the Sun.

12. The velocity-time graph from 0 s to 120 s for a cyclist is shown. Calculate the displacement and average acceleration in the 120 s time interval.
Solution:

Based on the standard velocity-time graph provided in the textbook:
- From $t=0$ to $20s$: Velocity increases from 0 to 3 m/s.
- From $t=20$ to $100s$: Velocity is constant at 3 m/s.
- From $t=100$ to $120s$: Velocity decreases from 3 m/s to 2 m/s.

Displacement is the area under the v-t graph.
Area 1 (Triangle, 0-20s) = $\frac{1}{2} \times base \times height = \frac{1}{2} \times 20 \times 3 = 30$ m.
Area 2 (Rectangle, 20-100s) = $length \times width = 80 \times 3 = 240$ m.
Area 3 (Trapezium, 100-120s) = $\frac{1}{2} \times (\text{sum of parallel sides}) \times height = \frac{1}{2} \times (3 + 2) \times 20 = 50$ m.
Total Displacement: $30 + 240 + 50 = 320$ m.

Average Acceleration:
$a_{av} = \frac{\text{Final Velocity} - \text{Initial Velocity}}{\text{Total Time}} = \frac{2 - 0}{120} = \frac{1}{60}$ m s⁻².

14. On entering a state highway, a car continues to move with a constant velocity of 6 m s⁻¹ for 2 minutes and then accelerates with a constant acceleration 1 m s⁻² for 6 seconds. Find the displacement of the car on the state highway in the 2 min 6 s time interval.
Solution:

The motion occurs in two parts.

Part 1 (Constant Velocity):
Velocity ($v$) = 6 m/s, Time ($t_1$) = 2 minutes = 120 s.
Displacement ($s_1$) = $v \times t_1 = 6 \times 120 = 720$ m.

Part 2 (Constant Acceleration):
Initial velocity for this part ($u$) = 6 m/s, Acceleration ($a$) = 1 m s⁻², Time ($t_2$) = 6 s.
Displacement ($s_2$) = $ut_2 + \frac{1}{2}at_2^2$
$s_2 = (6 \times 6) + \frac{1}{2}(1)(6)^2 = 36 + \frac{1}{2}(36) = 36 + 18 = 54$ m.

Total Displacement: $s_1 + s_2 = 720 + 54 = 774$ m.

16. Rohan studies science from 6 PM to 7:30 PM at home. Consider the tip of the minute’s hand of the wall clock. During the given time interval, what is its: (i) distance travelled, (ii) displacement, (iii) speed, and (iv) velocity. The length of the minute’s hand is 7 cm.
Solution:

Time interval = 6:00 PM to 7:30 PM = 1 hour 30 mins = 1.5 hours.
In 1.5 hours, the minute hand completes exactly 1.5 revolutions. The radius ($r$) is 7 cm.

(i) Distance Travelled:
Distance = 1.5 $\times$ Circumference = $1.5 \times (2\pi r) = 1.5 \times 2 \times \frac{22}{7} \times 7 = 66$ cm.

(ii) Displacement:
Since it completes 1 full revolution (back to 12) plus another half revolution, the tip ends up at the '6' mark on the clock (exactly opposite its starting point at '12'). The displacement is a straight line across the circle, which is the diameter.
Displacement = $2 \times r = 2 \times 7 = 14$ cm.

(iii) Speed:
Total time in seconds = 90 mins $\times$ 60 = 5400 s.
Speed = Distance / Time = $\frac{66}{5400} \approx 0.0122$ cm/s.

(iv) Velocity (Magnitude):
Velocity = Displacement / Time = $\frac{14}{5400} \approx 0.0026$ cm/s.
