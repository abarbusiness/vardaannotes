Test 03
Class: Diploma Program in EE/EEE Subject: Applied Physics Time: 2 Hours Max. Marks: 50
General Instructions:
  • All questions are compulsory.
  • The paper is divided into three sections: A, B, and C.
  • Assume standard values for physical constants where necessary.
  • Write your answers neatly and legibly.
SECTION A (10 Marks)(Attempt all questions - 2 Marks Each)
Q.1
Define absolute error and relative error. How is percentage error calculated?
Q.2
State Hooke's Law and write the definition of Young's Modulus.
Q.3
Briefly explain the effect of impurities and temperature on the surface tension of a liquid.
Q.4
Define critical velocity. State the significance of Reynolds's number in fluid dynamics.
Q.5
State Boyle's law and Charles's law for gases.
SECTION B (15 Marks)(Short Answer Type - 3 Marks Each)
Q.6
The resistance R of a wire is given by R = V/I, where V = (100 ± 5) V and I = (10 ± 0.2) A. Find the percentage error in the measurement of resistance.
Q.7
Draw a neat stress-strain diagram to represent the behavior of a metal wire under a continuously increasing load. Clearly mark the elastic limit, yield point, and breaking stress on it.
Q.8
Differentiate between cohesive and adhesive forces. Use these concepts to explain why the meniscus of water is concave, whereas the meniscus of mercury is convex in a glass tube.
Q.9
Write the law of thermal conductivity. Define the coefficient of thermal conductivity and state its S.I. unit.
Q.10
A given mass of gas occupies a volume of 400 cm3 at 27°C and 760 mm of Hg pressure. Calculate its volume at Standard Temperature and Pressure (STP) using the general gas equation.
SECTION C (25 Marks)(Long Answer Type - 5 Marks Each)
Q.11
(a) State Stokes' law and write its mathematical formula.
(b) Discuss the free fall of a spherical body through a viscous medium. Explain the various forces acting on it and describe how it eventually attains terminal velocity.
Q.12
(a) Define linear, aerial, and cubical expansion of solids. Write the relation between their coefficients without derivation.
(b) A metal rod is exactly 2 m long at 20°C. Find its length at 100°C if the coefficient of linear expansion of the metal is 1.8 × 10-5 /°C.
Q.13
A uniform steel wire of length 4 m and diameter 2 mm is stretched by applying a load of 10 kg. Calculate the stress, strain, and the elongation produced in the wire.
(Given: Young's modulus of steel = 2.0 × 1011 N/m2, g = 9.8 m/s2)
Q.14
Water rises to a height of 5 cm in a capillary tube of radius 0.3 mm. Calculate the surface tension of water.
(Given: density of water = 103 kg/m3, angle of contact = 0°, g = 9.8 m/s2)
Q.15
(a) What is the need for measurement in engineering? Briefly explain the requirements of a standard unit.
(b) State Gay-Lussac's law. What are the two specific heats of a gas? Differentiate between isothermal and adiabatic expansion of a gas.
