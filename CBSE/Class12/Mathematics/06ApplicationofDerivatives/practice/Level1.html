6.1 Rate of Change of Quantities
1.
Find the rate of change of the area of a circle with respect to its radius $r$ when $r = 5\text{ cm}$.
2.
Find the rate of change of the area of a circle with respect to its radius $r$ when $r = 12\text{ cm}$.
3.
The radius of a circle is increasing uniformly at the rate of $3\text{ cm/s}$. Find the rate at which the area of the circle is increasing when the radius is $10\text{ cm}$.
4.
An edge of a variable cube is increasing at the rate of $3\text{ cm/s}$. How fast is the volume of the cube increasing when the edge is $10\text{ cm}$ long?
5.
A stone is dropped into a quiet lake and waves move in circles at a speed of $4\text{ cm/s}$. At the instant when the radius of the circular wave is $10\text{ cm}$, how fast is the enclosed area increasing?
6.
The length $x$ of a rectangle is decreasing at the rate of $3\text{ cm/min}$ and the width $y$ is increasing at the rate of $2\text{ cm/min}$. When $x = 10\text{ cm}$ and $y = 6\text{ cm}$, find the rate of change of the perimeter.
7.
Using the data from the previous question (Q6), find the rate of change of the area of the rectangle.
8.
A balloon, which always remains spherical, has a variable diameter $\frac{3}{2}(2x + 1)$. Find the rate of change of its volume with respect to $x$.
9.
A spherical bubble is expanding so that its radius is increasing at the rate of $0.02\text{ cm/s}$. Find the rate of increase of its surface area when the radius is $5\text{ cm}$.
10.
The total cost $C(x)$ in Rupees associated with the production of $x$ units of an item is given by $C(x) = 0.005x^3 - 0.02x^2 + 30x + 5000$. Find the marginal cost when 3 units are produced.
11.
The total revenue in Rupees received from the sale of $x$ units of a product is given by $R(x) = 13x^2 + 26x + 15$. Find the marginal revenue when $x = 7$.
12.
A particle moves along the curve $y = \frac{2}{3}x^3 + 1$. Find the points on the curve at which the y-coordinate is changing twice as fast as the x-coordinate.
13.
A ladder $5\text{ m}$ long is leaning against a wall. The bottom of the ladder is pulled along the ground, away from the wall, at the rate of $2\text{ cm/s}$. How fast is its height on the wall decreasing when the foot of the ladder is $4\text{ m}$ away from the wall?
14.
The surface area of a spherical balloon is increasing at the rate of $2\text{ cm}^2\text{/s}$. Find the rate of change of its volume when the radius is $6\text{ cm}$.
15.
Sand is pouring from a pipe at the rate of $12\text{ cm}^3\text{/s}$. The falling sand forms a cone on the ground in such a way that the height of the cone is always one-sixth of the radius of the base. How fast is the height of the sand cone increasing when the height is $4\text{ cm}$?
6.2 Increasing and Decreasing Functions

18.

19.
Find the intervals in which the function $f(x) = x^2 - 4x + 6$ is strictly decreasing.
20.
Find the intervals in which the function $f(x) = 2x^2 - 3x$ is strictly increasing or decreasing.
21.
Determine the intervals where $f(x) = 2x^3 - 3x^2 - 36x + 7$ is strictly increasing.
22.
Determine the intervals where $f(x) = 2x^3 - 3x^2 - 36x + 7$ is strictly decreasing.
23.
Find the intervals in which $f(x) = 4x^3 - 6x^2 - 72x + 30$ is strictly increasing.
24.
Find the intervals in which $f(x) = -2x^3 - 9x^2 - 12x + 1$ is strictly decreasing.
25.
Prove that the function given by $f(x) = \cos x$ is strictly decreasing in $(0, \pi)$.
26.
Prove that the function given by $f(x) = \cos x$ is strictly increasing in $(\pi, 2\pi)$.
27.
Prove that $f(x) = x - \sin x$ is strictly increasing for all real $x$.
28.
Find the intervals in which the function $f(x) = \sin x + \cos x$, $0 \le x \le 2\pi$ is strictly increasing.
29.
Find the intervals in which the function $f(x) = \sin 3x$, $x \in [0, \pi/2]$ is strictly increasing or decreasing.
30.

31.

6.3 Tangents and Normals
32.

33.
Find the slope of the tangent to the curve $y = \frac{x-1}{x-2}$ at $x = 10$.
34.
Find the slope of the normal to the curve $y = x^3 - 3x + 2$ at the point whose x-coordinate is 3.
35.
Find the point at which the tangent to the curve $y = \sqrt{4x-3} - 1$ has its slope equal to $\frac{2}{3}$.
36.
Find points on the curve $\frac{x^2}{9} + \frac{y^2}{16} = 1$ at which the tangents are parallel to the x-axis.
37.
Find points on the curve $\frac{x^2}{9} + \frac{y^2}{16} = 1$ at which the tangents are parallel to the y-axis.
38.
Find the equation of all lines having slope $-1$ that are tangents to the curve $y = \frac{1}{x-1}$.
39.
Find the equation of the tangent to the curve $y = x^4 - 6x^3 + 13x^2 - 10x + 5$ at $(0, 5)$.
40.
Find the equation of the normal to the curve $y = x^4 - 6x^3 + 13x^2 - 10x + 5$ at $(0, 5)$.
41.
Find the equation of the tangent to the curve $y = \sin^2 x$ at $x = \pi/4$.
42.
Find the equation of the tangent to the curve $y = x^3 - 3x + 2$ which is parallel to the line $y = 9x - 5$.
43.
Find the angle of intersection of the curves $y = x^2$ and $x^2 = y$ at $(1, 1)$.
44.
Show that the curves $x = y^2$ and $xy = k$ cut at right angles if $8k^2 = 1$.
45.
Find the equation of the normal to the curve $x^2 = 4y$ which passes through the point $(1, 2)$.
46.
Prove that the curve $\left(\frac{x}{a}\right)^n + \left(\frac{y}{b}\right)^n = 2$ touches the straight line $\frac{x}{a} + \frac{y}{b} = 2$ at the point $(a, b)$, whatever be the value of $n$.

55.

56.

57.

58.

6.6 Applied Optimization
77.

78.

79.

80.
A wire of length $28\text{ m}$ is to be cut into two pieces. One of the pieces is to be made into a square and the other into a circle. What should be the lengths of the two pieces so that the combined area of the square and the circle is minimum?
81.
Prove that of all rectangles with a given perimeter, the square has the maximum area.
82.

83.

84.
A rectangular sheet of tin $45\text{ cm}$ by $24\text{ cm}$ is to be made into a box without top, by cutting off squares from each corner and folding up the flaps. What should be the side of the square to be cut off so that the volume of the box is maximum?
85.

86.

87.

88.

89.
Show that a right circular cylinder which is open at the top, and has a given surface area and maximum volume, is such that its height is equal to the radius of the base.
90.
