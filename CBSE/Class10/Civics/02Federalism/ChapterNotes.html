Federalism

Class 10 Civics • Chapter 02

1. What is Federalism?

A system of government in which power is divided between a central authority and various constituent units (states) of the country.

Key Features

  1. There are two or more levels (tiers) of government.
  2. Different tiers govern the same citizens, but each has its own jurisdiction (legislation, taxation, administration).
  3. Constitution guarantees existence of each tier.
  4. Fundamental provisions cannot be changed unilaterally by one level. Needs consent of both.
  5. Courts (Judiciary) act as umpire if disputes arise.
  6. Sources of revenue are specified (Financial Autonomy).
  7. Dual Objective: Safeguard unity + Accommodate diversity.

Routes to Federalism

  • Coming Together federation: Independent states coming together to form a bigger unit. Pools sovereignty. (USA, Switzerland, Australia).
  • Holding Together federation: Large country divides power between constituent states and national govt. (India, Spain, Belgium).

2. What makes India a Federal Country?

Constitution declared India as a 'Union of States'. (Word 'Federation' not used, but principle is federal).
Three Fold Distribution of Legislative Powers:

1. Union List (97 Subjects)

  • Subjects of national importance. Need uniform policy.
  • Defense, Banking, Currency, Foreign Affairs, Communication.
  • Only Union Govt can make laws.

2. State List (66 Subjects)

  • Subjects of local importance.
  • Police, Trade, Commerce, Agriculture, Irrigation.
  • Only State Govt can make laws.

3. Concurrent List (47 Subjects)

  • Common interest.
  • Education, Forest, Marriage, Adoption, Succession.
  • Both can make laws. If conflict arises, Union law prevails.

Residuary Subjects: Subjects not in any list (e.g., Computer Software). Union Govt has power.

3. How is Federalism Practised?

A. Linguistic States

Creation of linguistic states was the first major test.
Fear: Leaders feared it would lead to disintegration.
Reality: It made country more united and administration easier.

B. Language Policy

No language is given status of 'National Language'. Hindi is 'Official Language'.
Safeguards: 21 other Scheduled Languages. States have their own official languages. Centre agrees to use English along with Hindi for official purposes (to satisfy non-Hindi speakers like TN).

C. Centre-State Relations

Earlier, same party ruled Centre and States. State rights were ignored.
Post-1990: Rise of regional parties. Era of Coalition Governments. Supreme Court judgment made it difficult for Centre to dismiss State govt (Article 356 abuse reduced).

4. Decentralization in India (1992 Amendment)

Power taken from Centre and State and given to Local Govt.
Why? Problems are best settled at local level. People have better knowledge of local issues. Direct democracy.

Features of 1992 Constitutional Amendment (73rd & 74th)

  • Mandatory to hold regular elections (State Election Commission created).
  • Reservation for SC/ST/OBC.
  • 1/3rd seats reserved for Women.
  • State Finance Commission to share revenue.

Structure of Panchayati Raj (Rural)

  1. Gram Panchayat: Village level. Decisions making body. Head: Sarpanch.
  2. Gram Sabha: General body of all voters. Reviews Panchayat work. Meets 2-3 times/year.
  3. Panchayat Samiti (Block): Group of Gram Panchayats. Head: BDO/Chairman.
  4. Zila Parishad (District): Top level. Members: MPs, MLAs. Head: Zila Parishad Chairperson.

Urban Local Bodies

  • Municipalities: Towns. Head: Municipal Chairperson.
  • Municipal Corporations: Big cities. Head: Mayor.
