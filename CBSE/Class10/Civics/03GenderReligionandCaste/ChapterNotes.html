Gender, Religion and Caste

Class 10 Civics • Chapter 03

1. Gender and Politics

Sexual Division of Labour: A system where work inside home is done by women and work outside by men. It is not biological but social.

Feminist Movement:

Agitations demanding equal rights/opportunities (Voting, Education, Career). Political expression of gender division has helped improve women's role in public life.

Discrimination against women in India

  • Literacy Rate: 76% for men vs 54% for women.
  • Unpaid Work: On average, Indian woman works 1 hour more than men daily but gets no payment/recognition.
  • Sex Ratio: 940 (varies). Female foeticide (preference for son).
  • Representation:
    • Lok Sabha: Less than 12% (Earlier even lower).
    • State Assemblies: Less than 5%.
    • Exception: 1/3rd seats reserved in Panchayats (1992).

Women's Reservation Bill: Long pending demand to reserve 33% seats in Lok Sabha/Assemblies.

2. Religion, Communalism and Politics

Gandhi's View: "Religion can never be separated from politics". He meant moral values of religion, not sectarianism.

Human Rights Groups: Victims of communal riots are largely religious minorities.

Communalism

When religion is seen as the basis of the nation.
Problem: When one religion acts against another. Belief that people of different religions cannot live as equal citizens.

Forms of Communalism

  • Everyday beliefs: Stereotypes, belief in superiority of one's religion.
  • Political Dominance: Majority tries to dominate minority.
  • Political Mobilization: Use of religious symbols, leaders, fear in elections.
  • Communal Violence: Riots, Massacre. (Partition of India/Pakistan).

Secular State (India)

  1. No official religion. (Unlike Sri Lanka-Buddhism, Pakistan-Islam).
  2. Fundamental Right to profess any religion (Article 25).
  3. State can intervene in religious matters to ensure equality (e.g., Ban on Untouchability).

3. Caste and Politics

Caste system is extreme form of social division. Hereditary occupational division, sanctioned by rituals.

A. Caste in Politics

When parties choose candidates, they keep in mind caste composition of electorate.

  • Political parties appeal to caste sentiments.
  • "One person, one vote" has compelled leaders to mobilize support.
REALITY CHECK
  • No PC (Parliamentary Constituency) has a clear majority of one single caste.
  • No party wins votes of ALL voters of a caste.
  • Ruling MPs frequently lose elections (implies caste arithmetic is not rigid).

B. Politics in Caste

Politics also influences caste system.

  • Castes try to get bigger by incorporating sub-castes ('Sanskritization').
  • Calculated alliances (Coalitions between castes).
  • New groups: "Backward" and "Forward" caste groups.
