1. Electric Current and Circuit

Electric Current (I): This is the rate of flow of electric charge through a conductor. In simple terms, it's how much charge is passing a point in a circuit every second.

Formula: I = Q/t
Where I is current, Q is net charge, and t is time.

SI Unit: The SI unit of current is the Ampere (A).

Definition of 1 Ampere: One ampere is defined as the flow of one coulomb of charge per second. (1 A = 1 C/s)

Direction of Current:

  • Conventional Current: Direction of flow of positive charge (from positive to negative terminal of battery).
  • Electron Flow: Electrons (negative charge) flow from the negative to the positive terminal.

Electric Circuit: A continuous and closed path along which an electric current can flow.

Ammeter: An instrument used to measure electric current. It is always connected in series.

2. Electric Potential and Potential Difference

Electric Potential Difference (V): Often called voltage, it is the work done to move a unit charge from one point to another in an electric circuit.

Formula: V = W/Q
Where V is potential difference, W is work done, and Q is charge.

SI Unit: The SI unit is the Volt (V).

Definition of 1 Volt: One volt is the potential difference between two points when 1 joule of work is done to move a charge of 1 coulomb. (1 V = 1 J/C)

Source: A cell or battery creates the potential difference.

Voltmeter: An instrument used to measure potential difference. It is always connected in parallel.
