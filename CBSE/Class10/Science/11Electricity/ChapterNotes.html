2. Ohm's Law & Resistance

OHM'S LAW $$ V = I R $$

V-I Graph: A straight line passing through origin. The SLOPE of V-I graph gives Resistance ($R$).

ADVANCED TRICK: STRETCHING A WIRE If a wire of resistance $R$ is stretched to 'n' times its original length:
  • New Length $L' = nL$
  • New Area $A' = A/n$ (Volume remains constant)
  • New Resistance $R' = \rho \frac{nL}{A/n} = n^2 (\rho \frac{L}{A}) = n^2 R$.
Example: Stretch 2x length $\to$ Resistance becomes $2^2 = 4$ times.

3. Combinations

  • Series: $R_s = R_1 + R_2$. Current Same. Voltage divides. High Total Resistance.
  • Parallel: $1/R_p = 1/R_1 + 1/R_2$. Voltage Same. Current divides. Low Total Resistance.

4. Heating & Power

ENERGY FORMULAS $$ H = I^2 R t \quad (\text{Joule's Law}) $$ $$ P = VI = I^2 R = \frac{V^2}{R} $$ Commercial Unit: $1 \text{ kWh (Unit)} = 3.6 \times 10^6 \text{ Joules}$.

Applications

  • Fuse: Lead-Tin alloy. Low Melting Point. Connected in Series (Live Wire).
  • Heater Element: Nichrome (Alloy). High Resistivity. High Melting Point. Doesn't oxidise.
  • Bulb Filament: Tungsten. Very High Melting Point ($3380^\circ$C).
