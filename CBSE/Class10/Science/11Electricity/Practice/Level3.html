Section A: Assertion-Reasoning

Directions: In the following questions, a statement of assertion (A) is followed by a statement of reason (R). Mark the correct choice as:

(a) Both A and R are true and R is the correct explanation of A.
(b) Both A and R are true but R is not the correct explanation of A.
(c) A is true but R is false.
(d) A is false but R is true.

  1. Assertion: The resistivity of a semi-conductor decreases with increase of temperature.
    Reason: At higher temperatures, more electrons are free to move in semiconductors.
  2. Assertion: Fuse wire must have high resistance and low melting point.
    Reason: Fuse is used for small current flow only.
  3. Assertion: When a wire is stretched to three times its length, its resistance becomes 9 times.
    Reason: Resistance is directly proportional to the square of length when volume is constant.
  4. Assertion: Current is a scalar quantity.
    Reason: Electric current does not follow vector laws of addition.
  5. Assertion: 40 W bulb glows brighter than 100 W bulb when connected in series.
    Reason: In series combination, power dissipated is $I^2R$ and resistance of 40 W bulb is greater.
  6. Assertion: A bird perches on a high power line and nothing happens to the bird.
    Reason: The level of bird is very high from the ground.
  7. Assertion: It is advantageous to connect electric appliances in parallel.
    Reason: In parallel, if one device fails, others continue to work.
  8. Assertion: Bending a wire does not affect its electrical resistance.
    Reason: Resistance of a wire is proportional to resistivity of material.
  9. Assertion: Alloys are commonly used in electrical heating devices.
    Reason: Alloys do not oxidize (burn) readily at high temperatures.
  10. Assertion: Two unequal resistors are connected in series. The potential drop across the larger resistance is more.
    Reason: In series, $V=IR$ and $I$ is constant.
Section B: Case-Study Based Questions

I (Ampere) 0.5 1.0 2.0 3.0 4.0
V (Volts) 1.6 3.4 6.7 10.2 13.2
  1. Plot a graph between \( V \) and \( I \) and calculate the resistance of that resistor.
  2. What is the nature of the graph obtained?
  3. Is the resistor obeying Ohm's Law? Justify.
  4. If the length of this wire is doubled, what will be the new resistance?

Case II: The Electric Bill. A household uses the following electric appliances:
(i) Refrigerator of rating 400 W for 10 hours each day.
(ii) Two electric fans of rating 80 W each for 12 hours each day.
(iii) Six electric tubes of rating 18 W each for 6 hours each day.
The electricity bill for the month of June is to be calculated.

  1. Calculate the total energy consumed by the refrigerator in June (30 days).
  2. Calculate the total energy consumed by fans and tubes in one day.
  3. If the cost per unit is Rs 3.00, what is the total electricity bill for June?
  4. How can the family reduce this bill? Suggest one scientific method.
