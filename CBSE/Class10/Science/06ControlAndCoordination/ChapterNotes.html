1. Nervous System

Neuron: Dendrite (Receive) $\to$ Cell Body (Process) $\to$ Axon (Transmit) $\to$ Nerve End.

ELECTRICAL VS CHEMICAL IMPULSE
  • Electrical (Nerve): Fast, but can only reach cells connected by nerves. Cannot trigger continuously (rest time needed).
  • Chemical (Hormone): Slower, diffuse to all cells via blood. Continuous effect possible.
REFLEX ARC $$ Stimulus \to Receptor \to Sensory \: Neuron \to Spinal \: Cord \to Motor \: Neuron \to Effector \to Response $$ Brain is NOT involved immediately but is informed later.

2. Human Brain (CNS)

Part Sub-part Function
Forebrain Cerebrum "Thinking Part". Memory, Logic, Voluntary Actions, Sensory Interpretation.
Midbrain - Reflexes of eyes (pupil size) and ears.

3. Plant Coordination

Nastic vs Tropic

  • Nastic: Non-directional. Growth Independent. Fast. (e.g. Mimosa pudica leaves drooping due to turgor change).
  • Tropic: Directional. Growth Dependent. Slow. (e.g. Phototropism).

Auxin Mechanism (Phototropism)

Auxin is made at shoot tip. When light comes from one side, Auxin diffuses to the shady side. High auxin conc stimulates cell elongation on the shady side. Uneven growth causes the stem to Bend towards light.

4. Hormones (Endocrine)

HYPOTHALAMUS Link between Nervous & Endocrine.
Releases "Releasing Hormones" that stimulate the Pituitary gland to release its hormones.
Gland Function/Hormone
Pituitary (Master). GH (Growth). Excess: Gigantism. Low: Dwarfism.
Thyroid Thyroxin. Regulates Carb/Fat/Protein metabolism. Needs Iodine. Deficiency $\to$ Goitre.
Pancreas Insulin. Lowers blood sugar. Deficiency $\to$ Diabetes.
Adrenal Adrenaline. "Fight or Flight". \(\uparrow\) Heartbeat, \(\uparrow\) O$_2$ supply.

Feedback Mechanism

Regulates timing/amount of hormone.
Sugar Rise $\to$ Pancreas Detects $\to$ Insulin Released $\to$ Sugar Falls $\to$ Insulin Stopped.
