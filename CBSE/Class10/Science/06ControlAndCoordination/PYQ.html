2 Marks 31/S · Q26
Q26. (a) Explain how tendrils help a pea plant climb up other plants or fences.
OR
(b) List two major limitations of the use of electrical impulses as a means of communication between the cells in most multicellular animals.
(a) Tendrils:
- Tendrils are sensitive to touch (Thigmotropism).
- When a tendril touches a support, the part of the tendril away from the support grows faster than the part in contact.
- This uneven growth causes the tendril to circle around the object and cling to it.
(b) Limitations of Electrical Impulses:
1. They reach only those cells that are connected by nervous tissue (not every cell).
2. Once an impulse is generated and transmitted, the cell takes some time to reset for the next impulse (Refractory period), so continuous transmission is limited.

3 Marks 31/2 · Q29
Q29. (a) Define hormone.
(b) “Hormones should be secreted in precise quantities. We have a feedback mechanism through which this is done.” With the help of an example justify the statement.
(a) Hormone:
Hormones are chemical messengers secreted by endocrine glands directly into the blood to act on specific target organs to regulate various body functions.
(b) Feedback Mechanism:
The timing and amount of hormone released are regulated by feedback mechanisms.
Example (Insulin):
- If blood sugar levels rise (e.g., after a meal), they are detected by cells of the pancreas which respond by producing more insulin.
- As blood sugar levels fall, insulin secretion is reduced.
- This ensures precise control of blood sugar.
1 Mark 31/1 · Q11
Q11. The plant hormone whose concentration stimulates the cells to grow longer on the side of the shoot which is away from light is :
  • (A) Cytokinins
  • (B) Gibberellins
  • (C) Adrenaline
  • (D) Auxins
Auxins:
When a growing plant detects light, a hormone called Auxin, synthesized at the shoot tip, helps the cells to grow longer.
When light is coming from one side of the plant, auxin diffuses towards the shady side of the shoot. This concentration of auxin stimulates the cells to grow longer on the side of the shoot which is away from light.
Correct Option: (D)

  • (A) Hormonal effect
  • (B) Thermal effect
  • (C) Change in amount of water in cells

2 Marks 31/2 · Q23
Q23. A person is making a list to purchase few things from a nearby market. Explain how the fore-brain plays an important role in performing this activity.
Role of Fore-brain:
- The fore-brain (Cerebrum) is the main thinking part of the brain.
- It has separate areas for receiving sensory impulses (hearing, sight, etc.), association areas (for thinking, memory, interpreting information), and motor areas (for voluntary movements).

3 Marks 31/2 · Q29
Q29. (a) Define hormone.
(b) “Hormones should be secreted in precise quantities. We have a feedback mechanism through which this is done.” With the help of an example justify the statement.
(a) Hormone:
Hormones are chemical messengers secreted in trace amounts by endocrine glands directly into the blood, which act on specific target organs to regulate physiological processes.
(b) Feedback Mechanism:
The timing and amount of hormone released are regulated by feedback mechanisms to maintain balance (Homeostasis).
Example (Insulin):
- If blood sugar levels rise (e.g., after a meal), they are detected by the cells of the pancreas, which respond by producing more insulin to lower the sugar level.
- As the blood sugar level falls, insulin secretion is reduced automatically.
This ensures that hormones are secreted only when needed and in precise quantities.

1 Mark 31/3 · Q19
Q19. Assertion (A): In our actions of writing or talking, our nervous system communicates with the muscles.
Reason (R): Cranial nerves and spinal nerves form the peripheral nervous system.
  • (A) Both Assertion (A) and Reason (R) are true and Reason (R) is the correct explanation of Assertion (A).
  • (B) Both Assertion (A) and Reason (R) are true, but Reason (R) is not the correct explanation of Assertion (A).
  • (C) Assertion (A) is true, but Reason (R) is false.
  • (D) Assertion (A) is false, but Reason (R) is true.
Analysis:
Assertion (A): True. Voluntary actions like writing/talking involve the CNS sending signals to muscles via nerves.
Reason (R): True. PNS consists of Cranial and Spinal nerves.
Relation: The Reason defines PNS but does not explain how communication happens (electrical impulses, synapses, neuromuscular junctions) or why it happens for writing/talking.
Thus, both are true but R is not the correct explanation of A.
Correct Option: (B)
5 Marks 31/3 · Q39
Q39. A person while climbing up a rocky hill comes into a panic state and fear. His body starts reacting in a “flight-or-flight” condition to adjust to the dangerous and stressful situation.
Based on the above facts, answer the questions that follow.
(a) (i) Name the hormone secreted in the blood of the person in this situation.
OR
(a) (ii) Name the source gland of the hormone secreted in this condition.
(b) State any two responses in the body of the person as a result of the secretion of this hormone.
(c) How does the action of the chemical signal in terms of hormones differ from the electrical impulses via nerve cells ?
(a) (i) Hormone: Adrenaline.
(a) (ii) Source Gland: Adrenal Gland (Adrenal Medulla).
(b) Responses to Adrenaline:
1. Heart beats faster: To supply more oxygen to muscles.
2. Breathing rate increases: Due to contractions of diaphragm and rib muscles.
3. Blood flow to digestive system and skin is reduced (arteries constrict) and diverted to skeletal muscles.
(c) Difference (Hormonal vs Nervous):
- Signal Nature: Hormones are chemical signals (slow, spread via blood). Nerve impulses are electrical (fast, via neurons).
- Reach: Hormones reach all cells of the body (wide-acting). Nerve impulses reach only connected cells (specific).
- Duration: Hormonal effects are long-lasting. Nervous effects are short-lived.
3 Marks 31/4 · Q33
Q33. State two limitations of electrical impulses in multicellular organisms. Why is chemical communication better than electrical impulses as a means of communication between cells in multicellular organisms?
Limitations:
1. They reach only those cells connected by nervous tissue (not all cells).
2. Once generated, a cell needs time to reset before generating another impulse (cannot be continuous).
Chemical Communication Advantage:
Hormones (chemical messengers) can diffuse to all cells of the body and allow for steady, persistent changes rather than short pulses.
4 Marks 31/4 · Q39
Q39. In life there are certain changes in the environment called 'stimuli' to which we respond appropriately. Touching a flame suddenly is a dangerous situation for us. One way is to think consciously about the possibility of burning and then moving the hand. But our body has been designed in such a way that we save ourself from such situations immediately.

(i) Name the action by which we protect ourself in the situation mentioned above and define it.
(ii) Write the role of (a) motor and (b) relay neuron.
(iii) (A) What are the two types of nervous system in human body? Name the components of each of them.
OR
(iii) (B) Which part of the human brain is responsible for :
(a) thinking
(b) picking up a pencil
(c) controlling blood pressure
(d) controlling hunger
(i): Reflex Action. It is a sudden, involuntary, and automatic response to a stimulus.
(ii): (a) Motor Neuron: Carries impulse from spinal cord to effector (muscle). (b) Relay Neuron: Connects sensory and motor neurons within the spinal cord.
(iii) (A):
1. Central Nervous System (CNS): Brain and Spinal Cord.
2. Peripheral Nervous System (PNS): Cranial nerves and Spinal nerves.
(iii) (B):
(a) Thinking: Forebrain (Cerebrum).
(b) Picking pencil: Cerebellum (Hindbrain).
(c) Blood pressure: Medulla (Hindbrain).
(d) Hunger: Forebrain (Hypothalamus/Cerebrum).

4 Marks 31/6 · Q38
Q38. Case Study: The growth movements of plant parts in which the direction of the stimulus determines the direction of the response is known as tropic movements or tropism. Plants also have non-directional movements which may not be growth dependent.

(I) Name the movement which causes 'X' and 'Y' to grow downwards and upwards respectively.
(II) Write the name of a hormone that plays a major role in (i) falling of leaves (ii) rapid cell division.
(III) (a) Leaves of the sensitive plant move very quickly in response to 'touch'. How is this stimulus of touch communicated and explain how the movement takes place.
OR
(III) (b) Name the plant hormone which is synthesized at the shoot tip. How does this hormone helps the plant to bend towards light ?
(I) Geotropism (Gravisensitivity). X (Root) - Positive Geotropism. Y (Shoot) - Negative Geotropism.
(II) (i) Falling leaves: Abscisic Acid. (ii) Cell division: Cytokinins.
(III) (a) Communication: Electrochemical signals. Movement: By changing the amount of water (turgor pressure) in the cells (pulvinus). Cells swell or shrink, causing folding.
(III) (b) Hormone: Auxin.
Mechanism: Auxin diffuses to the shady side. More auxin causes cells on shady side to elongate more than the illuminated side, causing bending acting towards light (Phototropism).
