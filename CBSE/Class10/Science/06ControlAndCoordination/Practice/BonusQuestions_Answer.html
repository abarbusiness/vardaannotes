SECTION A: OBJECTIVE TYPE ANSWERS
1. Gap between Neurons
Ans: (b) Synapse
The small gap between two neurons where the nerve impulse passes is called synapse.
2. Cell Division Hormone
Ans: (c) Cytokinin
Cytokinins promote cell division.
3. Abscisic Acid Function
Ans: (c) inhibit growth
It is a stress hormone responsible for wilting of leaves.
4. Voluntary Action
Ans: (d) Chewing
Chewing is a voluntary action controlled by our will, unlike the others which are involuntary.
5. Iodine Necessity
Ans: (b) Thyroxin
Iodine is essential for the synthesis of thyroxin by the thyroid gland.
6. Sunflower Movement
Ans: (a) Phototropism
Growth movement in response to light is phototropism.
7. Spinal Cord Origin
Ans: (b) Medulla
Spinal cord is the downward continuation of the Medulla Oblongata.
8. Endocrine and Exocrine
Ans: (c) Pancreas
Pancreas secretes enzymes (exocrine) and hormones like insulin (endocrine).
9. Taste Receptors
Ans: (b) Gustatory receptors
Gustatory receptors detect taste; Olfactory detect smell.
10. Brain Protection
Ans: (b) Cranium
The brain is protected by the skull or cranium.
11. Assertion: CNS
Ans: (d) A is false but R is true.
A is false because CNS includes Brain AND Spinal Cord. R is true.
12. Assertion: Nerve Impulse Direction
Ans: (a) Both A and R are true and R is the correct explanation of A.
Synapses ensure one-way transmission because neurotransmitters are only released from the axon ending.
13. Assertion: Insulin
Ans: (b) Both A and R are true but R is not the correct explanation of A.
Insulin regulates blood sugar by signaling cells to absorb glucose. Diabetes is the result of failure, not the reason for regulation.
14. Assertion: Plant Nervous System
Ans: (a) Both A and R are true and R is the correct explanation of A.
Plants lack nerve cells but use electro-chemical signals to communicate triggers for movement.
SECTION B: SHORT ANSWER ANSWERS
15. Neuron Structure
(i) (Diagram required: Cell Body/Soma, Axon (long tail), Dendrites (branching from soma)).
(ii) Dendrite: Acquires the information (impulse) from the previous neuron/receptor.
16. Reflex Arc
Reflex Arc: The pathway taken by a nerve impulse during a reflex action.
Stimulus $\rightarrow$ Receptor $\rightarrow$ Sensory Neuron $\rightarrow$ Spinal Cord (Relay Neuron) $\rightarrow$ Motor Neuron $\rightarrow$ Effector (Muscle) $\rightarrow$ Response.
17. Tendril Growth
When a tendril touches a support, auxin diffuses to the side away from the support. This causes cells on the other side to grow faster than the side in contact, causing the tendril to bend around the support (Thigmotropism).
18. Tropic vs Nastic Movements
Tropic: Directional movement (Growth related). e.g., Phototropism (shoot bends to light).
Nastic: Non-directional movement (Not growth related). e.g., Thigmonasty (Touch-me-not plant folding leaves).
19. Phototropism
Process: When light falls on one side of shoot, auxin diffuses to the shady side. High auxin conc. stimulates faster cell growth on shady side, causing shoot to bend towards light.
(Diagram showing bending towards sun).
20. Adrenaline Function
Hormone: Adrenaline (Adrenal gland).
Function (Emergency): Increases heart beat, diverts blood to muscles, increases breathing rate to supply more oxygen for energy (Fight or Flight response).
SECTION C: LONG ANSWER ANSWERS
21. Human Brain
(i) (Diagram required: Forebrain (Cerebrum), Midbrain, Hindbrain (Pons, Medulla, Cerebellum)).
(ii) (a) Cerebellum: Posture, balance, precision of voluntary actions.
(b) Medulla: Involuntary actions like BP, salivation, vomiting.
(c) Cerebrum: Thinking, memory, sensory interpretation, voluntary control.
22. Hormones and Glads
(i) Thyroid: Thyroxin (Metabolism of carbs, fats, proteins).
(ii) Pituitary: Growth Hormone (Regulates growth and development).
(iii) Pancreas: Insulin (Regulates blood sugar level).
(iv) Adrenal: Adrenaline (Fight or flight response).
(v) Testes: Testosterone (Male secondary sexual characters).
SECTION D: CASE STUDY ANSWERS
23. Case Study: Diabetes
(i) Insulin.
(ii) Pancreas.
(iii) Feedback Mechanism: When blood sugar rises, pancreatic cells detect it and secrete more insulin. As sugar level falls, insulin secretion is reduced. This ensures precise regulation.
24. Case Study: Reflex Action
(i) Sudden, involuntary response to a stimulus.
(ii) Receptors in sense organs (e.g., skin).
(iii) Reflex arcs evolved because the thinking process of brain is not fast enough to respond to dangerous stimuli to prevent injury.
