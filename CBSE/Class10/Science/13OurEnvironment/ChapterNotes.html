1. Ecosystem

Decomposers (Cleaners): Break down complex organic substances (dead plants/animals) into simple inorganic substances that go into the soil and are used up once more by the plants. (e.g., Fungi, Bacteria). Without them, nutrients would not recycle.

Types of Ecosystem

  • Natural: Forest, Pond, Lake. (Self-sustaining).
  • Artificial: Crop fields, Garden, Aquarium. (Man-made, require cleaning, feeding).

2. Energy Flow (10% Law)

Unidirectional: Energy flows from Sun $\to$ Producers $\to$ Consumers. It never reverts back to solar input or previous level.
Loss: At each step, 90% energy is lost as HEAT to environment.

BIOMAGNIFICATION Pesticides (DDT) run off into water bodies $\to$ Aquatic plants take it up $\to$ Small fish eat plants $\to$ Large fish eat small fish $\to$ Birds/Humans eat large fish.
Concentration increases at each level.
Tip: Humans get the highest dose being at the top.

3. Garbage Management (3 R's)

THE 3 Rs
  1. Reduce: Use less. (Switch off lights, repair leaking taps). *Best method*.
  2. Reuse: Use again. (Plastic bottles for storing dal, Envelopes). *Better than recycle (energy saving)*.
  3. Recycle: Process waste to make new things. (Paper, Glass, Metal).

Case Study: Disposable Cups

  • Kulhads (Clay Cups): Resulted in loss of fertile top-soil. Discontinued.
  • Paper Cups: Biodegradable but cutting trees is bad.
  • Plastic Cups: Non-biodegradable, pollution.
  • Current Best: Paper cups (controlled forests) or Reusable glass/steel cups.
