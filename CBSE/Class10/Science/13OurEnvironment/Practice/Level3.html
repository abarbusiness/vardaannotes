Section A: High Order Thinking Skills (HOTS)
  1. Biomagnification Limit: Why is biological magnification highest in top carnivores? (Accumulation at each step). Can it be reversed naturally? (Very slow process/No).
  2. Energy Paradox: If energy decreases at each level, how do top predators (like tigers) get enough energy to be so powerful/strong? (They consume larger quantities of biomass; requires large territory).
  3. Waste Analysis: Disposable plastic cups were replaced by clay cups (kulhads) on trains, but then replaced by disposable paper cups. Why were kulhads discontinued? (Top soil depletion). Why are paper cups better than plastic? (Biodegradable).
  4. Pesticide Trace: A sample of wheat was found to contain trace amounts of pesticide. Suggest path of entry. (Soil/Water $\rightarrow$ Plants).
Section B: Case Study Based Questions

Case I: The Ozone Shield. Ozone is a molecule formed by three atoms of oxygen. While O2 is essential, O3 is deadly poison. At higher levels, it shields the surface of earth from UV radiation.

  1. Write the reaction involved in formation of Ozone. ($O_2 \rightarrow O+O; O+O_2 \rightarrow O_3$).
  2. In year 1980s, the amount of ozone dropped sharply. Identify the synthetic chemical responsible. (CFCs).
  3. How does UV radiation affect humans? (Skin cancer, cataract, immune system damage).

Case II: Waste Disposal. Improvements in lifestyle have resulted in greater amounts of waste material generation. Changes in packaging have resulted in much of our waste becoming non-biodegradable.

  1. Differentiate between composting and incineration. (Composting: Biological degradation. Incineration: Burning at high temp).
  2. Suggest one method to reduce non-biodegradable waste at home. (Reduce use of single-use plastics).
  3. Why are bacteria and fungi called decomposers? (They break down complex organic matter).
