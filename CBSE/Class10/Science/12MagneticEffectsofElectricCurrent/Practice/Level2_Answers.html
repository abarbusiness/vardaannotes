Section C: Numericals & Safety (31-40)
  1. $P = 2000 W, V = 220 V$. Current $I = P/V = 2000/220 \approx 9.09 A$. Since rating is 5A, the fuse will blow or the circuit will trip. It cannot be operated.
  2. $I = P/V = 1000/220 \approx 4.54 A$. Ideal fuse rating should be slightly higher, i.e., 5 A.
  3. In parallel, Voltage is same. $P = VI \Rightarrow I = P/V$. Current is directly proportional to Power. So 100W bulb draws more current. Ratio = 100:60 = 5:3.
  4. Total Power = $10 \times 40 = 400 W = 0.4 kW$. Time = 5 h. Energy = $P \times t = 0.4 \times 5 = 2 kWh$ (or 2 Units).
  5. $I = 1500/220 \approx 6.8 A$. The current (6.8A) > Fuse Rating (5A). The fuse will melt immediately. It will NOT work.
  6. $R = V^2/P = (220 \times 220)/100 = 48400/100 = 484 \Omega$.
  7. Heat $H = V^2/R \times t$. If V becomes half (110 vs 220), Heat becomes $(1/2)^2 = 1/4$ times. Heat produced reduces to one-fourth.
  8. For single turn radius $R$, $B_1 \propto I/R$.
    For double loop (2 turns), length $2\pi r \times 2 = 2\pi R \Rightarrow r = R/2$.
    $B_2 = N \times (\text{Field for } r) \propto 2 \times I/(R/2)$ $= 4 I/R$.
    So field becomes 4 times.
  9. $P = V \times I = 220 \times 4.5 = 990 W$.
  10. Total Power $P_{total} = 1000 + 500 = 1500 W$. $I = 1500/220 \approx 6.81 A$.
Section D: Scientific Reasoning (41-50)
  1. To offer minimum resistance. In case of a fault, the huge leakage current should flow through the earth wire to ground instantly to blow the fuse/MCB, rather than through the user or appliance. Thicker wire = Less Resistance.
  2. Because the magnetic field lines inside are parallel and equidistant, indicating that the magnetic field strength is the same at all points inside the solenoid.
  3. (1) Voltage division: Appliances won't get rated 220V. (2) Reliability: If one fails, circuit breaks and all fail.
  4. Physics of Protection: Metallic body is connected to Earth. If live wire touches the body, potential of body becomes zero (Earth potential) and current flows to Earth (low resistance path). This prevents shock to the user touching the metal.
  5. The tangent to a magnetic field line gives the direction of the field. If lines intersect, there would be two tangents at one point, implying two directions of the field at the same point, which is physically impossible.
  6. Soft iron has low retentivity (loses magnetism easily), making it suitable for temporary magnets. Steel has high retentivity (retains magnetism), making it a permanent magnet (cannot be switched off).
  7. A current carrying wire produces a magnetic field around it. This magnetic field exerts a magnetic force/torque on the compass needle (which is a small magnet), causing it to deflect.
  8. The force $F = BIl \sin(\theta)$. When conductor is perpendicular, $\theta = 90^\circ$, $\sin(90) = 1$ (Maximum). When parallel, $\sin(0) = 0$ (Zero force).
  9. Because they are good conductors of electricity (low resistivity), minimizing energy loss as heat ($I^2R$) during transmission.
  10. AC can be stepped up (voltage increased, current decreased) using transformers. Transmission at high voltage/low current reduces $I^2R$ heating loss significantly. DC cannot be stepped up/down easily by transformers.
