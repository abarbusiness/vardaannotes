Life Processes

Class 10 Science • Chapter 05 (Deep Detail)

1. Nutrition

PHOTOSYNTHESIS $$ 6CO_2 + 12H_2O \xrightarrow{\text{Sunlight/Chlorophyll}} C_6H_{12}O_6 + 6O_2 + 6H_2O $$ Stomata Mechanism: Water enters Guard cells $\to$ Swell/Turgid $\to$ Pore Opens.
Water exits $\to$ Shrink/Flaccid $\to$ Pore Closes.

Human Digestion

Villi: Finger-like projections in Small Intestine. Result in:

  • Increased Surface Area for absorption.
  • Rich blood supply to take food to cells.

2. Respiration

  • Aerobic: in Mitochondria. Oxygen utilized. End products: $CO_2, H_2O, Energy$ (High).
  • Anaerobic (Muscle): Lack of $O_2$. Products: Lactic Acid (Cramps).
  • Anaerobic (Yeast): Absence of $O_2$. Products: Ethanol, $CO_2$.
ATP & ALVEOLI ATP: Energy Currency. $ADP + P \xrightarrow{\text{Energy}} ATP$.
Alveoli: Balloon-like structures in lungs. Provide surface for exchange of gases ($O_2/CO_2$). Contain residual volume of air.

3. Transportation

Human Heart (Double Circulation)

Pulmonary Circ: Heart $\to$ Lungs $\to$ Heart.

Systemic Circ: Heart $\to$ Body $\to$ Heart.

Lymph (Tissue Fluid): Similar to plasma but colorless and less protein. Carries digested fat from intestine. Drains excess fluid back to blood.

Plants

  • Xylem: Water/Minerals. Upwards. Transpiration Pull (Suction).
  • Phloem: Food. Both directions. Translocation (Uses ATP).

4. Excretion

Nephron (Filtration Unit)

  1. Glomerulus: Filters blood under pressure.
  2. Bowman's Capsule: Collects filtrate.
  3. Tubule: Selective reabsorption (Glucose, Amino acids, Salts, Water).
  4. Collecting Duct: Collects urine.
ARTIFICIAL KIDNEY (HEMODIALYSIS) Device to remove Nitrogenous waste from blood due to kidney failure. Difference from Kidney: No Reabsorption occurs in dialysis.
