Section A: Identify 'X', 'Y', 'Z' Puzzles

Solve these identification problems:

  1. An organic compound A is essentially a constituent of wine and beer. On heating with alkaline Potassium Permanganate, it gives another organic compound B which keeps freezing in winter. Compound A and B react in the presence of sulphuric acid to form a sweet smelling compound C.
    (a) Identify A, B and C.
    (b) Write the chemical equation for the formation of C.
  2. A neutral organic compound X of molecular formula $C_2H_6O$ on heating with excess of concentrated $H_2SO_4$ at 443 K gives a compound Y. Compound Y on hydrogenation in the presence of Nickel catalyst gives Z.
    (a) Identify X, Y and Z.
    (b) Write the equation for the formation of Y.
    (c) What is the role of Sulphuric Acid here?
  3. An organic compound A is widely used as a preservative in pickles and has a molecular formula $C_2H_4O_2$. This compound reacts with ethanol to form a sweet smelling compound B.
    (a) Identify the compound A.
    (b) Write the chemical equation for its reaction with ethanol to form B.
    (c) Name the process.
  4. Compound X with molecular formula $C_2H_6O$ burns with a blue flame. It is also used as a fuel in cars along with petrol. Identify X and write the combustion equation.
  5. A mixture of oxygen and ethyne is burnt for welding. Can you explain why a mixture of ethyne and air is not used?
Section B: Structural Analysis & Isomerism
  1. Draw the possible structural isomers for Pentane ($C_5H_{12}$). Name them.
  2. How many structural isomers can you draw for Butane ($C_4H_{10}$)? Draw them.
  3. A cyclic carbon compound has formula $C_6H_{12}$. Draw its structure. How many single bonds are present in it?
  4. Draw the electron dot structure for Ethyne ($C_2H_2$). How many covalent bonds does it have?
  5. What is a Homologous Series? Explain with an example. state two characteristics of a homologous series.
Section C: Differentiate & Reason
  1. Differentiate between Ethanol and Ethanoic acid on the basis of:
    (a) Physical state
    (b) Reaction with Sodium Carbonate
  2. Differentiate between Diamond and Graphite on the basis of:
    (a) Structure
    (b) Electrical conductivity
  3. What is Saponification? Write the reaction for the preparation of soap.
  4. What forms the "hard" part of hard water? Why does soap fail in hard water?
  5. Why is the conversion of Vegetable Oil to Vegetable Ghee considered a Hydrogenation reaction? Name the catalyst used.
