Section A: High Order Thinking Skills (HOTS)
  1. Survival of Variations: A population of bacteria living in temperate water. If the water temperature increases due to global warming, most of these bacteria might die, but a few heat-resistant variants might survive. Explain the significance of variation here.
  2. DNA Accuracy: We say "Reproduction is the creation of a blueprint". If the copying of DNA is not 100% accurate, is it a flaw or an advantage? Justify. (Advantage --> Evolution).
  3. Contraception Logic: Why does the use of Copper-T not protect against sexually transmitted diseases? (Mechanism is uterine, not barrier).
  4. Placental Permeability: Why do we say that the embryo gets nutrition from the mother's blood, but their blood never mixes? (Diffusion across placenta).
  5. Flower Modification: Why do wind-pollinated flowers produce huge amounts of pollen compared to insect-pollinated ones? (Wastage compensation).
Section B: Experimental Competency (Case Studies)

Case I: Menstrual Cycle. The following graph shows the level of hormones (LH and FSH) and the thickness of the endometrial lining during a 28-day cycle.

  1. On which day is the level of LH maximum? What does this cause? (Day 14, Ovulation).
  2. Why does the lining thickness decrease from Day 1 to Day 5? (Menstruation).
  3. If fertilization occurs on Day 16, how will the lining thickness change after Day 28? (It will persist).
  4. Which hormone is responsible for the maintenance of the lining? (Progesterone).

Case II: Reproductive Parts. A student observes a slide of binary fission in Amoeba and budding in Yeast.

  1. In Amoeba, what divides first: Nucleus or Cytoplasm? (Nucleus - Karyokinesis).
  2. In Yeast, does the bud separate immediately? (No, can form chains).
  3. Draw the sequence of stages for Amoeba.
Section C: Assertion & Reasoning

Directions: Read Assertion (A) and Reason (R) and select the correct option.

  1. Assertion (A): Amoeba reproduces by binary fission.
    Reason (R): All unicellular organisms reproduce by binary fission.
    (A is True, R is False - e.g. Plasmodium).
  2. Assertion (A): A flower is legally defined as a modified shoot.
    Reason (R): It carries out sexual reproduction in plants.
    (Both true but R is not explanation of 'modified shoot').
  3. Assertion (A): Testes are situated outside the abdominal cavity.
    Reason (R): Sperm formation requires a temperature lower than body temperature.
    (Both true and R explains A).
  4. Assertion (A): Warts is a sexually transmitted disease.
    Reason (R): It is caused by bacteria.
    (A is True, R is False - Virus).
