1. Physical vs Chemical Changes

Physical Change: No new substance, reversible (e.g., Melting Ice).

Chemical Change: New substance formed, irreversible (e.g., Cooking Food).

Signs of Chemical Reaction:

  • Change in State (H$_2$(g) + O$_2$(g) $\to$ H$_2$O(l))
  • Change in Colour (Iron $\to$ Rust)
  • Evolution of Gas (Zinc + Acid $\to$ H$_2$)
  • Change in Temperature (Exothermic/Endothermic)
  • Formation of Precipitate (PbI$_2$ Yellow Ppt)
ACTIVITY 1.1: BURNING MAGNESIUM Mg Ribbon burns with a dazzling white flame and changes into a white powder (Magnesium Oxide).
$$ 2Mg(s) + O_2(g) \longrightarrow 2MgO(s) $$ Tip: Ribbon is cleaned with sandpaper before burning to remove protective Oxide layer.
ACTIVITY 1.2: YELLOW PRECIPITATE Mix Lead Nitrate and Potassium Iodide solutions.
$$ Pb(NO_3)_2(aq) + 2KI(aq) \longrightarrow PbI_2(s) \downarrow + 2KNO_3(aq) $$ Result: Yellow Precipitate of Lead Iodide ($PbI_2$).

2. Methods of Balancing

Law of Conservation of Mass: Total mass of elements in reactants = Total mass in products.

Hit & Trial Method (Step-by-Step)

Eq: $Fe + H_2O \rightarrow Fe_3O_4 + H_2$

  1. Start with compound having max atoms ($Fe_3O_4$). It has 4 Oxygen.
  2. Balance O: Add 4 to water $\rightarrow 4H_2O$.
  3. Balance H: Now we have 8H on LHS. Add 4 to H$_2$ on RHS $\rightarrow 4H_2$.
  4. Balance Fe: RHS has 3Fe. Add 3 to Fe on LHS $\rightarrow 3Fe$.
  5. Final: $3Fe(s) + 4H_2O(g) \rightarrow Fe_3O_4(s) + 4H_2(g)$.

(Note: Iron reacts with Steam, hence $g$ state for water)

3. Types of Reactions

A. Combination Reaction

Two reactants $\to$ One Product. (Generally Exothermic).

Quick Lime + Water: $CaO + H_2O \to Ca(OH)_2 + Heat$.

Whitewashing: $Ca(OH)_2$ reacts with $CO_2$ to form $CaCO_3$ (Calcium Carbonate) giving shiny finish.

B. Decomposition Reaction

One Reactant $\to$ Multiple Products. (Requires Energy: Endothermic).

  • Thermal (Heat): $2FeSO_4 \xrightarrow{\Delta} Fe_2O_3 (\text{Red}) + SO_2 + SO_3$. (Green crystals turn brown, smell of burning sulphur).
  • Thermal (Heat): $2Pb(NO_3)_2 \xrightarrow{\Delta} 2PbO + 4NO_2 + O_2$. (Emission of Brown Fumes of Nitrogen Dioxide).
  • Electrolytic (Electricity): $2H_2O \to 2H_2 + O_2$. ($H_2$ at Cathode, $O_2$ at Anode. Vol of H$_2$ is double).
  • Photolytic (Light): $2AgCl \to 2Ag + Cl_2$. (White powder turns Grey). Used in B&W photography.

C. Displacement Reaction

More reactive element displaces less reactive.

Iron in Copper Sulphate: $Fe(s) + CuSO_4(aq) \to FeSO_4(aq) + Cu(s)$.

Blue solution turns Light Green. Reddish-brown copper deposits on nail.

D. Double Displacement Reaction

Exchange of ions. (Precipitation).

$Na_2SO_4 + BaCl_2 \to BaSO_4(\text{White Ppt}) + 2NaCl$.

4. Redox Reactions

Oxidation: Gain of Oxygen / Loss of Hydrogen.

Reduction: Loss of Oxygen / Gain of Hydrogen.

Example: $MnO_2 + 4HCl \to MnCl_2 + Cl_2 + 2H_2O$.

  • $MnO_2 \to MnCl_2$: Oxygen lost (Reduction). Oxidizing Agent = $MnO_2$.
  • $HCl \to Cl_2$: Hydrogen lost (Oxidation). Reducing Agent = $HCl$.

5. Everyday Chemistry

CORROSION LIST
  • Rusting (Iron): $Fe_2O_3 \cdot xH_2O$ (Reddish Brown).
  • Silver Tarnish: $Ag_2S$ (Black Silver Sulphide).
  • Copper Corrosion: $CuCO_3 \cdot Cu(OH)_2$ (Basic Copper Carbonate - Green).
RESPIRATION

Respiration is an Exothermic reaction (Energy released).

$$ C_6H_{12}O_6(aq) + 6O_2(aq) \longrightarrow 6CO_2(aq) + 6H_2O(l) + Energy $$
