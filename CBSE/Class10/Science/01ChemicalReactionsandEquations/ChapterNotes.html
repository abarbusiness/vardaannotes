Chemical Reactions & Equations

Class 10 Science • Chapter 01 (Deep Detail)

1. Physical Change vs Chemical Reaction

Physical Change: No new substance formed; usually reversible (melting of ice, boiling of water).

Chemical Reaction: New substance(s) formed with new properties; often irreversible.

Standard observations that indicate a chemical reaction:

  • Change in state
  • Change in colour
  • Evolution of gas
  • Change in temperature (heat/light produced or absorbed)
  • Formation of a precipitate (insoluble solid)

2. Writing Chemical Equations (NCERT Style)

Word Equation $\rightarrow$ Skeletal Equation $\rightarrow$ Balanced Equation

Example: Magnesium + Oxygen $\rightarrow$ Magnesium oxide

Skeletal: $Mg + O_2 \rightarrow MgO$

Balanced: $2Mg + O_2 \rightarrow 2MgO$

State symbols: $(s)$ solid, $(l)$ liquid, $(g)$ gas, $(aq)$ aqueous solution

Special symbols: $\uparrow$ gas evolved, $\downarrow$ precipitate, $\Delta$ heating

3. Balancing Chemical Equations (Hit and Trial Method)

Law of Conservation of Mass: Number of atoms of each element remains same on both sides.

Equation: $Fe + H_2O \rightarrow Fe_3O_4 + H_2$

  1. Choose the compound with maximum atoms first ($Fe_3O_4$).
  2. Balance oxygen by putting 4 before water: $Fe + 4H_2O \rightarrow Fe_3O_4 + H_2$
  3. Balance hydrogen by putting 4 before hydrogen gas: $Fe + 4H_2O \rightarrow Fe_3O_4 + 4H_2$
  4. Balance iron by putting 3 before Fe: $3Fe + 4H_2O \rightarrow Fe_3O_4 + 4H_2$
  5. Add state symbols: $3Fe(s) + 4H_2O(g) \rightarrow Fe_3O_4(s) + 4H_2(g)$

4. NCERT Activities / Experiments from this Chapter

ACTIVITY 1.1: BURNING MAGNESIUM RIBBON Procedure: Clean Mg ribbon with sandpaper and burn it in air.
Observation: Dazzling white flame; white ash of magnesium oxide forms.
Equation: $$ 2Mg(s) + O_2(g) \longrightarrow 2MgO(s) $$
Concept: Combination reaction and oxidation.
ACTIVITY 1.2: LEAD NITRATE + POTASSIUM IODIDE Procedure: Mix aqueous lead nitrate and potassium iodide solutions.
Observation: Yellow precipitate of lead iodide forms.
Equation: $$ Pb(NO_3)_2(aq) + 2KI(aq) \longrightarrow PbI_2(s)\downarrow + 2KNO_3(aq) $$
Concept: Double displacement and precipitation reaction.
ACTIVITY 1.3: SODIUM SULPHATE + BARIUM CHLORIDE Procedure: Mix sodium sulphate solution with barium chloride solution.
Observation: White precipitate of barium sulphate forms.
Equation: $$ Na_2SO_4(aq) + BaCl_2(aq) \longrightarrow BaSO_4(s)\downarrow + 2NaCl(aq) $$
Concept: Double displacement and precipitation reaction.
ACTIVITY 1.4: HEATING FERROUS SULPHATE CRYSTALS Procedure: Heat green crystals of ferrous sulphate in a dry test tube.
Observation: Colour changes from green to brown; gases with burning sulphur smell evolve.
Equation: $$ 2FeSO_4(s) \xrightarrow{\Delta} Fe_2O_3(s) + SO_2(g) + SO_3(g) $$
Concept: Thermal decomposition reaction.
ACTIVITY 1.5: HEATING LEAD NITRATE Procedure: Heat lead nitrate crystals in a test tube.
Observation: Brown fumes of nitrogen dioxide evolve.
Equation: $$ 2Pb(NO_3)_2(s) \xrightarrow{\Delta} 2PbO(s) + 4NO_2(g) + O_2(g) $$
Concept: Thermal decomposition reaction.
ACTIVITY 1.6: ELECTROLYSIS OF WATER Procedure: Pass electricity through acidified water using electrodes.
Observation: Gases are collected at both electrodes; volume of hydrogen is double that of oxygen.
Equation: $$ 2H_2O(l) \xrightarrow{\text{Electricity}} 2H_2(g) + O_2(g) $$
Concept: Electrolytic decomposition.
ACTIVITY 1.7: SUNLIGHT ON SILVER CHLORIDE / SILVER BROMIDE Procedure: Keep AgCl/AgBr in sunlight.
Observation: White AgCl (or pale yellow AgBr) turns grey due to formation of silver metal.
Equations: $$ 2AgCl(s) \xrightarrow{\text{Sunlight}} 2Ag(s) + Cl_2(g) $$ $$ 2AgBr(s) \xrightarrow{\text{Sunlight}} 2Ag(s) + Br_2(g) $$
Concept: Photochemical decomposition; used in black-and-white photography.
ACTIVITY 1.8: IRON NAIL IN COPPER SULPHATE SOLUTION Procedure: Place clean iron nail in blue copper sulphate solution for some time.
Observation: Blue solution becomes light green; reddish-brown copper deposits on nail.
Equation: $$ Fe(s) + CuSO_4(aq) \longrightarrow FeSO_4(aq) + Cu(s) $$
Concept: Displacement reaction.

5. Types of Chemical Reactions (Board-Focused)

A. Combination Reaction

Two or more reactants combine to form one product.

$CaO(s) + H_2O(l) \rightarrow Ca(OH)_2(aq) + \text{Heat}$ (exothermic)

Whitewashing connection: $Ca(OH)_2 + CO_2 \rightarrow CaCO_3 + H_2O$ (shiny white finish)

B. Decomposition Reaction

One compound breaks into two or more simpler substances.

  • Thermal decomposition: by heat ($FeSO_4$, $Pb(NO_3)_2$ examples)
  • Electrolytic decomposition: by electricity ($H_2O$)
  • Photochemical decomposition: by sunlight ($AgCl$, $AgBr$)

C. Displacement Reaction

More reactive element displaces less reactive element from its compound.

$Fe + CuSO_4 \rightarrow FeSO_4 + Cu$

D. Double Displacement Reaction

Exchange of ions between two compounds to form new compounds.

$Na_2SO_4 + BaCl_2 \rightarrow BaSO_4\downarrow + 2NaCl$

6. Oxidation and Reduction (Redox)

Oxidation: Addition of oxygen or removal of hydrogen.

Reduction: Removal of oxygen or addition of hydrogen.

Example: $$ CuO + H_2 \rightarrow Cu + H_2O $$

  • $CuO \rightarrow Cu$: reduction (oxygen removed)
  • $H_2 \rightarrow H_2O$: oxidation (oxygen added)

When oxidation and reduction occur together, it is called a redox reaction.

7. Exothermic and Endothermic Reactions

EXOTHERMIC

Heat is released to surroundings.

Examples: Burning of natural gas, respiration, quick lime + water.

$$ C_6H_{12}O_6(aq) + 6O_2(g) \longrightarrow 6CO_2(g) + 6H_2O(l) + \text{Energy} $$
ENDOTHERMIC

Heat is absorbed from surroundings.

Examples: Most decomposition reactions (thermal/electrolytic/photolytic).

8. Effects of Oxidation in Everyday Life

CORROSION
  • Iron: rusting, $Fe_2O_3 \cdot xH_2O$ (reddish-brown)
  • Silver: tarnishing, $Ag_2S$ (black)
  • Copper: green coating of basic copper carbonate, $CuCO_3 \cdot Cu(OH)_2$

Prevention: painting, oiling/greasing, galvanisation, alloying, electroplating.

RANCIDITY

Oxidation of fats and oils in food causes bad smell and taste.

Prevention: antioxidants, airtight packing, refrigeration, nitrogen flushing in packets.

9. Last-Minute Board Exam Pointers

  • Always write balanced equations with state symbols.
  • Learn observations from all NCERT activities (colour change, precipitate, gas, smell, temperature).
  • Differentiate clearly: displacement vs double displacement.
  • Do not confuse exothermic/endothermic and oxidation/reduction.
  • Frequently asked applications: whitewashing, corrosion, rancidity, respiration.
