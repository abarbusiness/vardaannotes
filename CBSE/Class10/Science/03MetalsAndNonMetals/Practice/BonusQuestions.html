Directions (Q11-Q14): In the following questions, a statement of assertion (A) is followed by a statement of reason (R). Mark the correct choice as:
  1. Both A and R are true and R is the correct explanation of A.
  2. Both A and R are true but R is not the correct explanation of A.
  3. A is true but R is false.

11. Assertion (A): Zinc oxide is an amphoteric oxide.
Reason (R): Amphoteric oxides react with both acids and bases to produce salt and water.
12. Assertion (A): Gold and Platinum are found in the native state in nature.
Reason (R): They are highly reactive metals.
13. Assertion (A): Ionic compounds have high melting and boiling points.

14. Assertion (A): Metals produce basic oxides.
Reason (R): Non-metals produce acidic oxides.

SECTION D: CASE STUDY (4 Marks)
23. Read the following text and answer the questions:
Elements can be classified as metals and non-metals on the basis of their physical and chemical properties. Metals are lustrous, malleable, ductile and are good conductors of heat and electricity. Non-metals do not have these properties. Chemical properties involve reaction with air, water, acids etc.

A student took samples of four metals A, B, C and D and added them to the following solutions one by one. The results were:
- Metal A: No reaction with Iron(II) sulphate.
- Metal B: Displacement with Iron(II) sulphate.
- Metal C: No reaction with Iron(II) sulphate, Displacement with Copper(II) sulphate.
- Metal D: No reaction with Iron(II) sulphate, No reaction with Copper(II) sulphate.

(i) Which is the most reactive metal? (1 Mark)
(ii) Arrange the metals in decreasing order of reactivity. (2 Marks)
(iii) Which metal from A, B, C or D is likely to be Silver? (1 Mark)
24. Read the following text and answer the questions:
Corrosion is a natural process that converts a refined metal into a more chemically-stable form such as oxide, hydroxide, or sulfide. It is the gradual destruction of materials (usually a metal) by chemical and/or electrochemical reaction with their environment. Rusting of iron is the most common example of corrosion.

(i) What is the chemical formula of rust? (1 Mark)
(ii) State two methods to prevent rusting of iron. (1 Mark)
(iii) Silver articles become black after some time when exposed to air. Why? Write the chemical name of the black substance formed. (2 Marks)
