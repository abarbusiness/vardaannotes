1. Physical Properties & Exceptions

Metals: Solid, Hard, Lustrous, Conductors. EXCEPT:

  • Liquid: Mercury (Hg).
  • Soft (Knife Cut): Sodium (Na), Potassium (K), Lithium (Li).
  • Low MP (Melt on Palm): Gallium (Ga), Caesium (Cs).

Non-Metals: Brittle, Insulators. EXCEPT:

  • Lustrous: Iodine.
  • Liquid: Bromine.
  • Hardest: Diamond (Carbon).
  • Conductor: Graphite (Carbon).

2. Chemical Properties

A. Reactivity with Oxygen

Metal + Oxygen $\to$ Metal Oxide (Basic).

$$ 4Al + 3O_2 \to 2Al_2O_3 $$
AMPHOTERIC OXIDES React with BOTH Acid and Base to give Salt + Water.
Example: $Al_2O_3$ and $ZnO$.
Reaction with Base: $Al_2O_3 + 2NaOH \to 2NaAlO_2 \text{ (Sodium Aluminate)} + H_2O$.

Anodising

Process of forming a thick oxide layer on Aluminum. Clean Al is made the Anode in Dilute $H_2SO_4$. Oxygen evolved reacts with Al to form a protective layer that prevents further corrosion.

B. Reactivity with Water

  • Cold Water: Na, K (Violent/Exothermic), Ca (Floats).
  • Hot Water: Mg (Floats).
  • Steam: Al, Fe, Zn. ($3Fe + 4H_2O(g) \to Fe_3O_4 + 4H_2$).
  • No Reaction: Pb, Cu, Ag, Au.

C. Reaction with Acids (HNO$_3$ Exception)

Generally, Metal + Dil Acid $\to$ Salt + $H_2$.

NITRIC ACID EXCEPTION $HNO_3$ is a strong Oxidizing Agent. It oxidizes evolved $H_2$ to $H_2O$ and itself gets reduced to Nitrogen Oxides ($NO_2, N_2O$).
Exception: Magnesium ($Mg$) and Manganese ($Mn$) react with very dilute $HNO_3$ to evolve $H_2$ gas.

Aqua Regia (Royal Water)

Freshly prepared mixture of Conc. HCl and Conc. HNO$_3$ in ratio 3:1. Highly corrosive, can dissolve Gold and Platinum.

3. Ionic Compounds

Transfer of electrons from Metal $\to$ Non-Metal.

FORMATION OF NaCl $Na (2,8,1) \xrightarrow{-1e^-} Na^+ (2,8)$
$Cl (2,8,7) \xrightarrow{+1e^-} Cl^- (2,8,8)$
Attracted by strong Electrostatic Forces.

Properties: High MP/BP, Soluble in water, Conduct electricity in Molten/Aq state (not Solid).

4. Metallurgy (Extraction)

Enrichment Method

Roasting (Sulphide Ores): $2ZnS + 3O_2 \xrightarrow{\Delta} 2ZnO + 2SO_2$ (Excess Air)

Calcination (Carbonate Ores): $ZnCO_3 \xrightarrow{\Delta} ZnO + CO_2$ (Limited Air)

THERMITE REACTION $Fe_2O_3(s) + 2Al(s) \to 2Fe(l) + Al_2O_3(s) + Heat$.
Used to join railway tracks. Fe is produced in molten state due to high heat.

Electrolytic Refining (Copper)

  • Anode: Impure Cu (thick).
  • Cathode: Pure Cu (thin).
  • Electrolyte: Acidified $CuSO_4$.
  • Process: Pure Cu deposits on Cathode. Impurities settled as Anode Mud.

5. Alloys & Corrosion

ALLOY LIST
  • Brass: Cu + Zn
  • Bronze: Cu + Sn
  • Solder: Pb + Sn (Low MP, for welding)
  • Stainless Steel: Fe + Ni + Cr (Hard, rust-free)
  • Amalgam: Metal + Mercury (Hg)

24 Carat vs 22 Carat Gold: 24k is pure but very soft. 22k contains 2 parts Cu or Ag to make it hard enough for jewelry.
