Chapter Practice Sheet: Areas Related to Circles

Class: 10 (CBSE) Subject: Mathematics Max. Marks: 25
SECTION A: OBJECTIVE TYPE QUESTIONS (1 Mark Each)
1. If the sum of the areas of two circles with radii $R_1$ and $R_2$ is equal to the area of a circle of radius R, then:
  1. $R_1 + R_2 = R$
  2. $R_1^2 + R_2^2 = R^2$
  3. $R_1 + R_2 < R$
  4. $R_1^2 + R_2^2 < R^2$
[1]
2. Area of a sector of angle p (in degrees) of a circle with radius R is:
  1. $\frac{p}{180} \times 2\pi R$
  2. $\frac{p}{180} \times \pi R^2$
  3. $\frac{p}{360} \times 2\pi R$
  4. $\frac{p}{720} \times 2\pi R^2$
[1]
3. The length of the minute hand of a clock is 14 cm. The area swept by the minute hand in 5 minutes is:
  1. $154/3$ cm$^2$
  2. $44/3$ cm$^2$
  3. $154$ cm$^2$
  4. $77$ cm$^2$
[1]
SECTION B: SHORT ANSWER TYPE QUESTIONS (2 Marks Each)
4. Find the area of a quadrant of a circle whose circumference is 22 cm.
[2]
5. A chord of a circle of radius 10 cm subtends a right angle at the centre. Find the area of the minor segment. (Use $\pi = 3.14$)
[2]
6. In a circle of radius 21 cm, an arc subtends an angle of $60^{\circ}$ at the center. Find the length of the arc.
[2]
SECTION C: LONG ANSWER TYPE QUESTIONS (3 Marks Each)
7. Find the area of the shaded region in the figure, if ABCD is a square of side 14 cm and APD and BPC are semicircles.
[3]
8. Calculate the area of the designed region common between the two quadrants of circle of radius 8 cm each.
[3]
9. A round table cover has six equal designs. If the radius of the cover is 28 cm, find the cost of making the designs at the rate of Rs. 0.35 per cm$^2$. (Use $\sqrt{3} = 1.7$)
[3]
SECTION D: CASE STUDY (4 Marks)
10. Case Study: Car Wipers
A car has two wipers which do not overlap. Each wiper has a blade of length 25 cm sweeping through an angle of $115^{\circ}$.

(i) Calculate the total area cleaned at each sweep of the blades. (2 Marks)
(ii) What is the area of the sector formed by one wiper? (1 Mark)
(iii) If the angle was $90^{\circ}$, what would be the area swept by one blade? (1 Mark)
[4]
