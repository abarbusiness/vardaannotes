1. Key Concepts

Line of Sight: The line drawn from the eye of an observer to the point in the object viewed by the observer.

Angle of Elevation: Angle formed by the line of sight with the horizontal when examining a point above the horizontal level (Looking Up).

Angle of Depression: Angle formed by the line of sight with the horizontal when examining a point below the horizontal level (Looking Down).

2. Important Case Studies (PYQ Models)

Case 1: The Broken Tree

Problem: A tree breaks due to storm and the broken part bends so that the top of the tree touches the ground making an angle of $30^\circ$ with it. The distance between the foot of the tree to the point where the top touches the ground is 8 m.

AI IMAGE PROMPT:
Generate a diagram of a "Broken Tree" on a white background.
Subject: A vertical tree stump (AB) standing on ground. The broken upper part (AC) is leaning down touching the ground at point C.
Labels: Mark the angle at ground C as $30^\circ$. Mark distance from base B to C as "8m".
Concept: Total Height of tree = Standing Part + Broken Leaning Part.

Solution Logic:

  • Height of standing part ($P$) $\rightarrow$ Use $\tan 30^\circ$.
  • Length of broken part ($H$) $\rightarrow$ Use $\cos 30^\circ$.
  • Total Height = $P + H$.

Case 2: Flagstaff on a Tower (One Above Other)

Problem: From a point on the ground, the angles of elevation of the bottom and the top of a transmission tower fixed at the top of a 20m high building are $45^\circ$ and $60^\circ$ respectively.

AI IMAGE PROMPT:
Generate a diagram showing a Building with a Tower on top of it.
Details:
- Building labeled AB (height 20m).
- Tower labeled BC (on top of it).
- Observer at point P on ground.
- Draw two lines of sight: P to B (Bottom of tower) and P to C (Top of tower).
- Mark angles $45^\circ$ (smaller) and $60^\circ$ (larger).

Solution Logic:

  • Identify Common Base method. The distance of observer from building is same for both triangles.
  • Triangle 1 (Small): $\tan 45^\circ = \frac{20}{Base} \Rightarrow Base = 20$.
  • Triangle 2 (Big): $\tan 60^\circ = \frac{20+h}{Base} \Rightarrow \sqrt{3} = \frac{20+h}{20}$.
  • Solve for $h$.

Case 3: Two Poles on Opposite Sides

Problem: Two poles of equal heights are standing opposite each other on either side of the road, which is 80 m wide.

AI IMAGE PROMPT:
Generate a diagram of two vertical poles AB and CD on opposite ends of a horizontal line BD (the road).
Details: A point P is on the road between them. Draw lines of sight to the tops of both poles. Mark angles $60^\circ$ and $30^\circ$.
Labels: Road width = 80m. If one part is 'x', other is '80-x'.

Solution Logic:

  • Let height be $h$. Let distance of point from one pole be $x$, then other is $80-x$.
  • Eq 1: $\tan 60 = h/x \Rightarrow h = x\sqrt{3}$.
  • Eq 2: $\tan 30 = h/(80-x) \Rightarrow h = (80-x)/\sqrt{3}$.
  • Equate 'h' to find 'x'.
