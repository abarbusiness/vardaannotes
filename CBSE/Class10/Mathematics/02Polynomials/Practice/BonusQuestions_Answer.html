Master Notes: Polynomials

Class: 10 (CBSE) Subject: Mathematics Worksheet Solutions
FORMULA Relationship between Zeroes and Coefficients:
For a quadratic polynomial $ax^2 + bx + c$: $$ \text{Sum of zeroes } (\alpha + \beta) = -\frac{b}{a} $$ $$ \text{Product of zeroes } (\alpha\beta) = \frac{c}{a} $$
SECTION A: OBJECTIVE TYPE (Answers)
1. Number of zeroes for 3 intersections:
Ans: (d) 3
Reason: The number of zeroes of a polynomial $p(x)$ corresponds to the number of points where the graph intersects the X-axis.
2. Value of $\alpha + \beta$ for $x^2 - 5x + 6$:
Ans: (a) 5
Here $a=1, b=-5, c=6$. $\text{Sum} = -\frac{b}{a} = -\frac{(-5)}{1} = 5$.
3. Quadratic polynomial with zeroes -3 and 4:
Ans: (c) $x^2 - x - 12$
Sum $S = -3 + 4 = 1$. Product $P = (-3)(4) = -12$. Formula: $k[x^2 - Sx + P]$ $\Rightarrow$ $x^2 - (1)x + (-12)$ $\Rightarrow$ $x^2 - x - 12$.
4. Value of k if one zero is 2 for $x^2 + 3x + k$:
Ans: (b) -10
Substitute $x=2$: $(2)^2 + 3(2) + k = 0$. $4 + 6 + k = 0$ $\Rightarrow$ $10 + k = 0$ $\Rightarrow$ $k = -10$.
5. Shape of the graph of a quadratic polynomial:
Ans: (c) Parabola
6. Values of a and b if zeroes are 2 and -3:
Ans: (d) a = 0, b = -6
Sum $= 2 + (-3) = -1$ $\Rightarrow$ $-(a+1) = -1$ $\Rightarrow$ $a = 0$. Product $= 2(-3) = -6$ $\Rightarrow$ $b = -6$.
SECTION B: SHORT ANSWER (Solutions)
7. Find zeroes of $6x^2 - 3 - 7x$ and verify.
Standard form: $6x^2 - 7x - 3$. Factorization: $6x^2 - 9x + 2x - 3$ $= 3x(2x - 3) + 1(2x - 3)$ $= (3x + 1)(2x - 3)$. Zeroes: $x = -1/3$ and $x = 3/2$.
Master Tip Always arrange the polynomial in descending order of powers ($ax^2 + bx + c$) before identifying coefficients $a, b, c$.
Verification: Sum: $(-1/3) + (3/2) = 7/6$. Formula: $-(-7)/6 = 7/6$. (Matches) Product: $(-1/3)(3/2) = -1/2$. Formula: $-3/6 = -1/2$. (Matches)
8. Quadratic polynomial with sum 1/4 and product -1.
Formula: $k[x^2 - (\text{Sum})x + \text{Product}]$. $k[x^2 - \frac{1}{4}x - 1]$. Take $k=4$ to remove fraction. Answer: $4x^2 - x - 4$.
9. Zeroes are double of $2x^2 - 5x - 3$. Find p, q.
Zeroes of $2x^2 - 5x - 3$ are $-1/2$ and $3$. New Zeroes: $2(-1/2) = -1$ and $2(3) = 6$. New Poly: $x^2 - (-1+6)x + (-1)(6)$ $= x^2 - 5x - 6$. Comparing with $x^2 + px + q$: $p = -5, q = -6$.
10. One zero of $(k - 1)x^2 + kx + 1$ is -3. Find k.
Put $x = -3$: $(k - 1)(-3)^2 + k(-3) + 1 = 0$. $9(k - 1) - 3k + 1 = 0$ $\Rightarrow$ $9k - 9 - 3k + 1 = 0$. $6k - 8 = 0$ $\Rightarrow$ $k = 8/6 = 4/3$.
SECTION C: LONG ANSWER (Solutions)
11. $f(x) = x^2 - 5x + k$ and $\alpha - \beta = 1$. Find k.
Given: $\alpha + \beta = 5$ and $\alpha\beta = k$.
IDENTITY $$ (\alpha - \beta)^2 = (\alpha + \beta)^2 - 4\alpha\beta $$
Substitute values: $(1)^2 = (5)^2 - 4(k)$. $1 = 25 - 4k$ $\Rightarrow$ $4k = 24$ $\Rightarrow$ $k = 6$.
12. New polynomial with zeroes $1/\alpha, 1/\beta$.
Original $2x^2 - 4x + 5$: $\alpha+\beta = 2$, $\alpha\beta = 5/2$. New Sum $S' = \frac{1}{\alpha} + \frac{1}{\beta}$ $= \frac{\alpha+\beta}{\alpha\beta}$ $= \frac{2}{5/2}$ $= \frac{4}{5}$. New Product $P' = \frac{1}{\alpha\beta}$ $= \frac{1}{5/2}$ $= \frac{2}{5}$. Required Poly: $5[x^2 - \frac{4}{5}x + \frac{2}{5}]$ $\Rightarrow$ $5x^2 - 4x + 2$.
13. Squared difference of zeroes is 144 for $x^2 + px + 45$.
$(\alpha - \beta)^2 = 144$. Sum $=-p$, Prod $=45$. $(-p)^2 - 4(45) = 144$ $\Rightarrow$ $p^2 - 180 = 144$. $p^2 = 324$ $\Rightarrow$ $p = \pm 18$.
14. Find a, b for $2x^2 - (a + 3)x + (2b - 1)$ if Sum=4, Prod=3.
Sum: $\frac{a+3}{2} = 4$ $\Rightarrow$ $a+3=8$ $\Rightarrow$ $a=5$. Product: $\frac{2b-1}{2} = 3$ $\Rightarrow$ $2b-1=6$ $\Rightarrow$ $2b=7$ $\Rightarrow$ $b=3.5$.
SECTION D: CASE STUDY (Solutions)
15. Case Study: $P(x) = -x^2 + 2x + 8$
(i) Downwards. Coeff of $x^2$ is negative (-1). (ii) Zeroes: $-x^2 + 2x + 8 = 0$ $\Rightarrow$ $x^2 - 2x - 8 = 0$ $\Rightarrow$ $(x-4)(x+2)=0$. Zeroes are $4, -2$. (iii) At $x=0$, $P(0) = 8$. (iv) Verify $x=4$: $-(4)^2 + 2(4) + 8$ $= -16 + 8 + 8 = 0$. Yes.
