Master Notes: Coordinate Geometry

Class: 10 (CBSE) Subject: Mathematics Worksheet Solutions
SECTION A: OBJECTIVE TYPE QUESTIONS
1. The distance of the point P(2, 3) from the x-axis is:
Ans: (b) 3
Distance from x-axis is the magnitude of y-coordinate. |3| = 3.
2. The points (-1, -2), (1, 0), (-1, 2), (-3, 0) form...
Ans: (a) Square
All sides equal length $\sqrt{4+4}=\sqrt{8}$.
Diagonals $\sqrt{16}=4$ and $\sqrt{16}=4$. Equal diagonals.
Hence, Square.
3. If the distance between the points (4, p) and (1, 0) is 5...
Ans: (b) $\pm 4$
$\sqrt{(4-1)^2 + (p-0)^2} = 5$.
$9 + p^2 = 25 \Rightarrow p^2 = 16 \Rightarrow p = \pm 4$.
4. The mid-point of the line segment joining A(-2, 8) and B(-6, -4)...
Ans: (a) (-4, 2)
$x = (-2-6)/2 = -4$. $y = (8-4)/2 = 2$.
5. The point which divides the line segment joining (7, -6) and (3, 4) in the ratio 1:2...
Ans: (iv) IV quadrant
$x = (1(3)+2(7))/3 = 17/3$.
$y = (1(4)+2(-6))/3 = -8/3$.
x positive, y negative $\rightarrow$ IV quadrant.
6. If A(1, 2), B(0, 0) and C(a, b) are collinear...
Ans: (c) 2a = b
Slope AB = (0-2)/(0-1) = 2.
Slope BC = (b-0)/(a-0) = b/a.
$2 = b/a \Rightarrow 2a = b$.
SECTION B: SHORT ANSWER TYPE QUESTIONS
7. Find relations between x and y... equidistant from (7, 1) and (3, 5).
Ans: x - y = 2
$(x-7)^2 + (y-1)^2 = (x-3)^2 + (y-5)^2$.
$-14x + 49 - 2y + 1 = -6x + 9 - 10y + 25$.
$-8x + 8y + 16 = 0 \Rightarrow x - y - 2 = 0 \Rightarrow x-y=2$.
8. Find ratio in which y-axis divides... Also intersection.
Ans: Ratio 5:1, Point (0, -13/3)
Let ratio be k:1. on y-axis, x=0.
$0 = (k(-1) + 1(5))/(k+1) \Rightarrow k = 5$. Ratio 5:1.
$y = (5(-4) + 1(-6))/6 = -26/6 = -13/3$.
9. Find coords of A where AB is diameter, Centre (2, -3), B(1, 4).
Ans: A(3, -10)
$(x+1)/2 = 2 \Rightarrow x=3$.
$(y+4)/2 = -3 \Rightarrow y=-10$.
10. Check whether (5, -2), (6, 4) and (7, -2) form an isosceles triangle.
Ans: Yes
$AB = \sqrt{1^2 + 6^2} = \sqrt{37}$.
$BC = \sqrt{1^2 + (-6)^2} = \sqrt{37}$.
$AC = \sqrt{2^2 + 0} = 2$.
Since $AB=BC$, it is isosceles.
SECTION C: LONG ANSWER TYPE QUESTIONS
11. Find area of rhombus (3, 0), (4, 5), (-1, 4), (-2, -1).
Ans: 24 sq units
$d1 = AC = \sqrt{(-1-3)^2 + (4-0)^2}$
$= \sqrt{32} = 4\sqrt{2}$.
$d2 = BD = \sqrt{(-2-4)^2 + (-1-5)^2}$
$= \sqrt{72} = 6\sqrt{2}$.
Area = $1/2 \times d1 \times d2 = 1/2 \times 4\sqrt{2} \times 6\sqrt{2} = 24$.
12. Points of trisection of (4, -1) and (-2, -3).
Ans: (2, -5/3) and (0, -7/3)
P (1:2): $x=(1(-2)+2(4))/3 = 2$,
$y=(1(-3)+2(-1))/3 = -5/3$.
Q (2:1): $x=(2(-2)+1(4))/3 = 0$,
$y=(2(-3)+1(-1))/3 = -7/3$.
13. A(6, 1), B(8, 2), C(9, 4), D(p, 3) are vertices of a parallelogram... Find p.
Ans: p = 7
Midpoint of AC = Midpoint of BD.
$(6+9)/2 = (8+p)/2 \Rightarrow 15 = 8+p \Rightarrow p=7$.
14. Find centre of circle passing through (6, -6), (3, -7) and (3, 3).
Ans: (3, -2)
Centre (x, y) equidistant from all 3.
Using OA=OB and OB=OC.
Solving equations leads to x=3, y=-2.
SECTION D: CASE STUDY
15. Case Study: Classroom Seating
Detailed Solution:
(i) $AB = \sqrt{(6-3)^2 + (7-4)^2} = \sqrt{9+9} = 3\sqrt{2}$.
(ii) $AC = \sqrt{(9-3)^2 + (4-4)^2} = \sqrt{36} = 6$.
(iii) All sides $3\sqrt{2}$. Diagonals $AC=6$, $BD=6$. Thus, Square.
