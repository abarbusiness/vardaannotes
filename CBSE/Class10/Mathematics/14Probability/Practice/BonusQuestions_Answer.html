Master Notes: Probability

Class: 10 (CBSE) Subject: Mathematics Worksheet Solutions
SECTION A: OBJECTIVE TYPE QUESTIONS
1. The probability of an impossible event is:
Ans: (b) 0
2. If $P(E) = 0.05$, then the probability of 'not E' is:
Ans: (b) 0.95
Explanation: $P(\text{not } E) = 1 - P(E) = 1 - 0.05 = 0.95$.
3. A card is drawn from a well-shuffled deck of 52 cards. The probability that it is a face card is:
Ans: (b) 3/13
Explanation: Total face cards = 12. $P = \frac{12}{52} = \frac{3}{13}$.
4. Which of the following cannot be the probability of an event?
Ans: (b) -1.5
Explanation: Probability cannot be negative.
5. The probability of getting a prime number in a single throw of a die is:
Ans: (a) 1/2
Explanation: Prime numbers on a die are 2, 3, 5. So, 3 outcomes. $P = \frac{3}{6} = \frac{1}{2}$.
6. Two coins are tossed strictly. The probability of getting at most one head is:
Ans: (c) 3/4
Explanation: Outcomes: HH, HT, TH, TT. At most one head: HT, TH, TT (3 cases). $P = \frac{3}{4}$.
SECTION B: SHORT ANSWER TYPE QUESTIONS
7. A bag contains 3 red balls and 5 black balls. A ball is drawn at random from the bag. What is the probability that the ball drawn is (i) red? (ii) not red?
Ans: (i) 3/8 (ii) 5/8
Total balls = 3 + 5 = 8.
(i) $P(\text{Red}) = \frac{3}{8}$
(ii) $P(\text{Not Red}) = 1 - P(\text{Red})$
$= 1 - \frac{3}{8} = \frac{5}{8}$.
8. A die is thrown once. Find the probability of getting (i) a number lying between 2 and 6 (ii) an odd number.
Ans: (i) 1/2 (ii) 1/2
(i) Numbers between 2 and 6 are 3, 4, 5. So, 3 outcomes. $P = \frac{3}{6} = \frac{1}{2}$.
(ii) Odd numbers are 1, 3, 5. So, 3 outcomes. $P = \frac{3}{6} = \frac{1}{2}$.
9. One card is drawn from a well-shuffled deck of 52 cards. Find the probability of getting (i) a king of red colour (ii) a spade.
Ans: (i) 1/26 (ii) 1/4
(i) Red Kings: King of Hearts, King of Diamonds (2 cards). $P = \frac{2}{52} = \frac{1}{26}$.
(ii) Spades = 13 cards. $P = \frac{13}{52} = \frac{1}{4}$.
10. A letter is chosen at random from the word "ASSASSINATION". Find the probability that the letter chosen is a vowel.
Ans: 6/13
Total letters = 13. Vowels (A, A, A, I, A, I, O) = 6. $P = \frac{6}{13}$.
SECTION C: LONG ANSWER TYPE QUESTIONS
11. A box contains 90 discs which are numbered from 1 to 90. If one disc is drawn at random from the box, find the probability that it bears (i) a two-digit number (ii) a perfect square number (iii) a number divisible by 5.
Ans: (i) 9/10 (ii) 1/10 (iii) 1/5
Total outcomes = 90.
(i) Two-digit numbers (10 to 90) = $90 - 9 = 81$. $P = \frac{81}{90} = \frac{9}{10}$.
(ii) Perfect squares (1, 4, 9, 16, 25, 36, 49, 64, 81) = 9. $P = \frac{9}{90} = \frac{1}{10}$.
(iii) Divisible by 5 (5, 10, ..., 90) = 18. $P = \frac{18}{90} = \frac{1}{5}$.
12. A piggy bank contains hundred 50p coins, fifty ₹1 coins, twenty ₹2 coins and ten ₹5 coins. ...
Ans: (i) 5/9 (ii) 17/18
Total coins = $100 + 50 + 20 + 10 = 180$.
(i) 50p coins = 100. $P = \frac{100}{180} = \frac{5}{9}$.
(ii) ₹5 coins = 10. NOT ₹5 coin = $180 - 10 = 170$.
$P = \frac{170}{180} = \frac{17}{18}$.
13. Two dice are thrown at the same time. Find the probability that the sum of the two numbers appearing on the top of the dice is (i) 8 (ii) 13 (iii) less than or equal to 12.
Ans: (i) 5/36 (ii) 0 (iii) 1
Total outcomes = 36.
(i) Sum 8: (2,6), (3,5), (4,4), (5,3), (6,2) = 5 outcomes. $P = \frac{5}{36}$.
(ii) Sum 13: Impossible (max sum is 12). $P = 0$.
(iii) Sum <= 12: All outcomes satisfy this. $P=1$ (Sure event).
14. Cards Problem...
Ans: (i) 1/5 (ii)(a) 1/4 (ii)(b) 0
Cards: 10, J, Q, K, A (Diamonds). Total = 5.
(i) One Queen. $P = \frac{1}{5}$.
(ii) Queen removed. Total = 4 (10, J, K, A).
(a) Ace: 1 in 4. $P = \frac{1}{4}$.
(b) Queen: 0 remaining. $P = 0$.
SECTION D: CASE STUDY
15. Case Study: The Game of Choice
(i) Sample Space (12 items):
{1R, 1B, 1G, 1Y, 2R, 2B,
2G, 2Y, 3R, 3B, 3G, 3Y}

(ii) Even number (2) and Red: {2R} (1 outcome).
$P = \frac{1}{12}$.

(iii) Number < 3 (1, 2) and Not Yellow (R, B, G).
Outcomes: {1R, 1B, 1G, 2R, 2B, 2G} (6 outcomes).
$P = \frac{6}{12} = \frac{1}{2}$.

(iv) Odd (1, 3) and Primary (R, B, Y).
Outcomes: {1R, 1B, 1Y, 3R, 3B, 3Y} (6 outcomes).
$P = \frac{6}{12} = \frac{1}{2}$.
