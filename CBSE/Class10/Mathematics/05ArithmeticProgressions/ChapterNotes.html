Arithmetic Progressions

Class 10 Mathematics • Chapter 05 (Rationalized Syllabus)

1. Introduction

Arithmetic Progression (AP): An AP is a list of numbers in which each term is obtained by adding a fixed number to the preceding term except the first term.

  • The fixed number is called the Common Difference ($d$).
  • The first term is denoted by $a$.

Important: The common difference $d$ can be positive, negative, or zero.

General Form: $a, a+d, a+2d, a+3d, ...$

2. nth Term of an AP

GENERAL TERM
$$ a_n = a + (n-1)d $$

Where $a_n$ is the nth term, $a$ is the first term, $d$ is common difference, and $n$ is the position.

Quick Example

Q: Find the 10th term of AP: 2, 7, 12, ...

Sol:

  • $a = 2, d = 7 - 2 = 5, n = 10$.
  • $a_{10} = 2 + (10-1)5 = 2 + 9(5) = 2 + 45 = 47$.
EXAM TRICK: FINDING nth TERM FROM END To find the $n$th term from the last term ($l$) towards the first term:
Term from end = $l - (n-1)d$

Harder Example: First Negative Term

Q: Which term of the AP: 121, 117, 113, ... is its first negative term?

Sol:

  1. $a = 121, d = 117 - 121 = -4$.
  2. We want $a_n < 0$.
  3. $121 + (n-1)(-4) < 0$.
  4. $121 - 4n + 4 < 0 \Rightarrow 125 - 4n < 0$.
  5. $125 < 4n \Rightarrow n> 31.25$.
  6. So, $n = 32$. The 32nd term is the first negative term.

3. Sum of First n Terms

SUM FORMULA
$$ S_n = \frac{n}{2} [2a + (n-1)d] $$

OR

$$ S_n = \frac{n}{2} [a + l] $$

(Use when last term $l$ is known)

Example: Sum of First 1000 Positive Integers

Sol:

  • AP: 1, 2, 3, ..., 1000.
  • $a = 1, l = 1000, n = 1000$.
  • $S_{1000} = \frac{1000}{2} [1 + 1000] = 500 \times 1001 = 500500$.
CONCEPT LINK If sum $S_n$ is given, nth term can be found by: $$ a_n = S_n - S_{n-1} $$

4. Important Case Studies (Word Problems)

AP is the most popular chapter for Case Study questions. Here are the classic scenarios.

Case 1: The Savings Problem

Question: Ramkali saved ₹5 in the first week of a year and then increased her weekly savings by ₹1.75. If in the nth week, her weekly savings become ₹20.75, find n.

Solution:

  1. This is an AP: 5, 6.75, 8.50, ...
  2. $a = 5, d = 1.75, a_n = 20.75$.
  3. $a_n = a + (n-1)d$.
  4. $20.75 = 5 + (n-1)(1.75)$.
  5. $15.75 = (n-1)(1.75)$.
  6. $n-1 = \frac{15.75}{1.75} = \frac{1575}{175} = 9$.
  7. $n = 10$. In the 10th week, her savings become ₹20.75.

Case 2: The Flower Bed Problem

Question: In a flower bed, there are 23 rose plants in the first row, 21 in the second, 19 in the third, and so on. There are 5 rose plants in the last row. How many rows are there?

Solution:

  1. AP: 23, 21, 19, ..., 5.
  2. $a = 23, d = 21 - 23 = -2, l (a_n) = 5$.
  3. $5 = 23 + (n-1)(-2)$.
  4. $-18 = (n-1)(-2) \Rightarrow n-1 = 9 \Rightarrow n = 10$.
  5. There are 10 rows.

Case 3: Divisibility Problems

Question: How many multiples of 4 lie between 10 and 250?

Solution:

  1. First multiple of 4 > 10 is 12.
  2. Last multiple of 4 < 250 is 248.
  3. AP: 12, 16, 20, ..., 248.
  4. $a = 12, d = 4, a_n = 248$.
  5. $248 = 12 + (n-1)4 \Rightarrow 236 = 4(n-1)$.
  6. $n-1 = 59 \Rightarrow n = 60$.
  7. There are 60 multiples of 4.
