Chapter Practice Sheet: Arithmetic Progressions

Class: 10 (CBSE) Subject: Mathematics Max. Marks: 30
SECTION A: OBJECTIVE TYPE QUESTIONS (1 Mark Each)
1. The nth term of the AP: 5, 2, -1, -4, ... is:
  1. 2n - 5
  2. 2n + 5
  3. 8 - 3n
  4. 3n - 8
[1]
2. If the common difference of an AP is 3, then $a_{20} - a_{15}$ is:
  1. 5
  2. 3
  3. 15
  4. 20
[1]
3. The first four terms of an AP whose first term is -2 and the common difference is -2 are:
  1. -2, 0, 2, 4
  2. -2, -4, -8, -16
  3. -2, -4, -6, -8
  4. -2, -2, -2, -2
[1]
4. The sum of the first five multiples of 3 is:
  1. 45
  2. 55
  3. 65
  4. 75
[1]
5. If k, 2k-1 and 2k+1 are three consecutive terms of an AP, the value of k is:
  1. 2
  2. -2
  3. 3
  4. -3
[1]
6. The 10th term from the end of the AP: 4, 9, 14, ..., 254 is:
  1. 209
  2. 205
  3. 214
  4. 213
[1]
SECTION B: SHORT ANSWER TYPE QUESTIONS (2 Marks Each)
7. Find the sum of the first 22 terms of the AP: 8, 3, -2, ...
[2]
8. Which term of the AP: 3, 15, 27, 39, ... will be 132 more than its 54th term?
[2]
9. Find the number of terms in the AP: 7, 13, 19, ..., 205.
[2]
10. If the sum of the first n terms of an AP is $4n - n^2$, what is the 10th term?
[2]
SECTION C: LONG ANSWER TYPE QUESTIONS (3 Marks Each)
11. The sum of the 4th and 8th terms of an AP is 24 and the sum of the 6th and 10th terms is 44. Find the first three terms of the AP.
[3]
12. How many two-digit numbers are divisible by 3?
[3]
13. Find the sum of all odd integers between 2 and 100 which are divisible by 3.
[3]
14. If m times the mth term of an AP is equal to n times its nth term and $m \ne n$, show that the $(m+n)$th term of the AP is zero.
[3]
SECTION D: CASE STUDY (4 Marks)
15. Case Study: Savings Plan
A person saves ₹100 in the first month, ₹150 in the second month, ₹200 in the third month, and so on. The monthly savings form an Arithmetic Progression.

(i) What will be his saving in the 10th month? (1 Mark)
(ii) What will be his total savings in 10 months? (1 Mark)
(iii) In which month will his saving be ₹500? (2 Marks)
[4]
