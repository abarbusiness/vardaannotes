Quadratic Equations

Class 10 Mathematics • Chapter 04 (Rationalized Syllabus)

1. Introduction

Standard Form: A quadratic equation in the variable $x$ is an equation of the form:

$$ax^2 + bx + c = 0$$

Where $a, b, c$ are real numbers and $a \neq 0$.

The degree of a quadratic equation is always 2.

2. Methods of Solving

Method 1: Factorization (Splitting Middle Term)

Steps:

  1. Find two numbers $p$ and $q$ such that $p + q = b$ and $pq = ac$.
  2. Rewrite $bx$ as $px + qx$.
  3. Factor by grouping.

Example: Solve $6x^2 - x - 2 = 0$.

Here $ac = 6 \times (-2) = -12$ and $b = -1$.

Pairs of factors of -12 are: (1, -12), (2, -6), (3, -4)... Wait! $3 + (-4) = -1$.

So, split $-x$ as $+3x - 4x$.

$6x^2 + 3x - 4x - 2 = 0$

$3x(2x + 1) - 2(2x + 1) = 0$

$(3x - 2)(2x + 1) = 0$

Roots: $x = 2/3$ or $x = -1/2$.

Method 2: Quadratic Formula (Sridharacharya Formula)

This is a universal method that works even when factorization is difficult.

MASTER FORMULA
$$ x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a} $$

3. Nature of Roots

The term $D = b^2 - 4ac$ is called the Discriminant. It decides the nature of the roots.

Value of D Nature of Roots
$D > 0$ Two distinct real roots
$D = 0$ Two equal real roots (Coincident)
$D < 0$ No real roots (Imaginary)
EXAM TRICK: FINDING 'k' Q: Find $k$ if $2x^2 + kx + 3 = 0$ has two equal roots.
Sol: For equal roots, $D = 0 \Rightarrow b^2 - 4ac = 0$.
Here $a=2, b=k, c=3$.
$k^2 - 4(2)(3) = 0 \Rightarrow k^2 - 24 = 0 \Rightarrow k^2 = 24 \Rightarrow k = \pm 2\sqrt{6}$.

4. The Heart of the Chapter: Word Problems

Quadratic Equations are heavily used to solve real-world problems. Here are the most critical types for exams.

Type 1: Train & Speed Problems (Very Important)

Question: A train travels 360 km at a uniform speed. If the speed had been 5 km/h more, it would have taken 1 hour less for the same journey. Find the speed of the train.

Solution:

  1. Let usual speed = $x$ km/h. Distance = 360 km.
  2. Usual Time $T_1 = \frac{360}{x}$.
  3. New Speed = $(x+5)$ km/h. New Time $T_2 = \frac{360}{x+5}$.
  4. Logic: Faster speed $\rightarrow$ Less time. So, $T_1 - T_2 = 1$.
  5. Equation: $\frac{360}{x} - \frac{360}{x+5} = 1$.
  6. $360 [\frac{1}{x} - \frac{1}{x+5}] = 1 \Rightarrow 360 [\frac{x+5-x}{x(x+5)}] = 1$.
  7. $360(5) = x^2 + 5x \Rightarrow x^2 + 5x - 1800 = 0$.
  8. Solving: $x^2 + 45x - 40x - 1800 = 0 \Rightarrow (x+45)(x-40) = 0$.
  9. Since speed cannot be negative ($x \neq -45$), Speed = 40 km/h.

Type 2: Time & Work (Pipes/Cisterns)

Question: Two water taps together can fill a tank in $9 \frac{3}{8}$ hours. The tap of larger diameter takes 10 hours less than the smaller one to fill the tank separately. Find the time in which each tap can separately fill the tank.

Solution:

If a tap fills a tank in $x$ hours, in 1 hour it fills $(1/x)$ part of tank.
  1. Let smaller tap take $x$ hours. Larger tap takes $(x-10)$ hours.
  2. Combined time = $75/8$ hours. Combined rate = $8/75$ tank per hour.
  3. Equation: $\frac{1}{x} + \frac{1}{x-10} = \frac{8}{75}$.
  4. Simplify: $\frac{x-10+x}{x(x-10)} = \frac{8}{75} \Rightarrow \frac{2x-10}{x^2-10x} = \frac{8}{75}$.
  5. $75(2x-10) = 8(x^2-10x) \Rightarrow 150x - 750 = 8x^2 - 80x$.
  6. $8x^2 - 230x + 750 = 0 \Rightarrow 4x^2 - 115x + 375 = 0$.
  7. Solving gives $x = 25$ or $x = 3.75$.
  8. If $x=3.75$, larger tap time is $3.75 - 10$ (Negative, Impossible).
  9. So, Smaller tap = 25 hours, Larger tap = 15 hours.

Type 3: Geometry & Areas

Question: The altitude of a right triangle is 7 cm less than its base. If the hypotenuse is 13 cm, find the other two sides.

Solution:

  1. Let Base = $x$ cm. Altitude = $(x-7)$ cm. Hypotenuse = 13 cm.
  2. By Pythagoras Theorem: $Base^2 + Altitude^2 = Hyp^2$.
  3. $x^2 + (x-7)^2 = 13^2$.
  4. $x^2 + x^2 - 14x + 49 = 169 \Rightarrow 2x^2 - 14x - 120 = 0$.
  5. $x^2 - 7x - 60 = 0$.
  6. $(x-12)(x+5) = 0$. Since side $> 0$, $x = 12$.
  7. Base = 12 cm, Altitude = 5 cm.

Type 4: Upstream - Downstream Revisited

Question: A motor boat whose speed is 18 km/h in still water takes 1 hour more to go 24 km upstream than to return downstream to the same spot. Find the speed of the stream.

Solution:

Upstream Speed ($S_U$) = Boat - Stream ($18 - x$).
Downstream Speed ($S_D$) = Boat + Stream ($18 + x$).
  1. Let speed of stream = $x$ km/h. Distance = 24 km.
  2. Time Upstream $T_U = \frac{24}{18-x}$. Time Downstream $T_D = \frac{24}{18+x}$.
  3. Given: Upstream takes MORE time. So $T_U - T_D = 1$.
  4. $\frac{24}{18-x} - \frac{24}{18+x} = 1$.
  5. $24 [\frac{18+x - (18-x)}{(18-x)(18+x)}] = 1 \Rightarrow 24 [\frac{2x}{324 - x^2}] = 1$.
  6. $48x = 324 - x^2 \Rightarrow x^2 + 48x - 324 = 0$.
  7. Factors of 324 whose diff is 48: 54 and 6.
  8. $(x+54)(x-6) = 0$. Speed cannot be negative ($x \neq -54$).
  9. Speed of stream = 6 km/h.

5. Number Problems

Question: The sum of the reciprocals of Rehman's ages (in years) 3 years ago and 5 years from now is $1/3$. Find his present age.

Solution:

  • Let present age = $x$.
  • 3 years ago = $x-3$. 5 years hence = $x+5$.
  • Equation: $\frac{1}{x-3} + \frac{1}{x+5} = \frac{1}{3}$.
  • Result: $\frac{x+5+x-3}{(x-3)(x+5)} = \frac{1}{3} \Rightarrow \frac{2x+2}{x^2+2x-15} = \frac{1}{3}$.
  • $3(2x+2) = x^2+2x-15 \Rightarrow 6x+6 = x^2+2x-15$.
  • $x^2 - 4x - 21 = 0 \Rightarrow (x-7)(x+3) = 0$.
  • Age cannot be negative. Present age = 7 years.
