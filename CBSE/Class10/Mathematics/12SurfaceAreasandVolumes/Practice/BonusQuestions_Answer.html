Master Notes: Surface Areas and Volumes

Class: 10 (CBSE) Subject: Mathematics Worksheet Solutions
SECTION A: OBJECTIVE TYPE ANSWERS
1. Two cubes (vol 64) joined. Surface Area is:
Ans: (b) 160 cm$^2$
$a^3 = 64 \Rightarrow a = 4$ cm. Cuboid $L=8$, $B=4$, $H=4$.
$TSA = 2(LB+BH+HL)$
$= 2(32+16+32) = 2(80) = 160$ cm$^2$.
2. Volume of Cone + Hemisphere (r=1, h=1):
Ans: (a) $\pi$ cm$^3$
Vol = $\frac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h + \frac{2}{3}\pi r^3$.
Vol = $\frac{1}{3}\pi (1)(1) + \frac{2}{3}\pi (1)$
$= \frac{1}{3}\pi + \frac{2}{3}\pi = \pi$.
3. Recast Sphere (4.2cm) to Cylinder (6cm). Find h:
Ans: (a) 2.74 cm
$\frac{4}{3}\pi R^3 = \pi r^2 h$
$\Rightarrow \frac{4}{3}(4.2)^3 = 6^2 h$.
$h = \frac{4 \times 4.2 \times 4.2 \times 4.2}{3 \times 36} = 2.744$ cm.
SECTION B: SHORT ANSWER TYPE ANSWERS
4. Capsule Surface Area (Cylinder + 2 Hemispheres).
Ans: 220 mm$^2$
Radius $r = 5/2 = 2.5$ mm. Hemi length = 2.5 mm.
Cylinder length $h = 14 - (2.5+2.5) = 9$ mm.
SA = $2\pi rh + 2(2\pi r^2) = 2\pi r(h+2r)$
$= 2 \times \frac{22}{7} \times 2.5 (9+5)$
$= \frac{110}{7} \times 14 = 220$ mm$^2$.
5. Tent Canvas Area (Cylinder + Cone).
Ans: 44 m$^2$
$r=2$ m, $h=2.1$ m, $l=2.8$ m.
Area = $2\pi rh + \pi rl = \pi r(2h+l)$
$= \frac{22}{7} \times 2 (4.2 + 2.8)$
$= \frac{44}{7} \times 7 = 44$ m$^2$.
6. TSA of remaining solid (Cylinder - Cone).
Ans: 18 cm$^2$
$r=0.7$, $h=2.4$. $l = \sqrt{2.4^2 + 0.7^2} = 2.5$.
TSA = CSA Cyl + CSA Cone + Base Area
$= 2\pi rh + \pi rl + \pi r^2$.
$= \pi r(2h+l+r)$
$= \frac{22}{7} \times 0.7 (4.8+2.5+0.7)$
$= 2.2 \times 8 = 17.6 \approx 18$ cm$^2$.
SECTION C: LONG ANSWER TYPE ANSWERS
7. Wooden article (Cylinder - 2 Hemispheres). TS Area.
Ans: 374 cm$^2$
TSA = CSA Cyl + 2 CSA Hemi
$= 2\pi rh + 2(2\pi r^2) = 2\pi r(h+2r)$.
$= 2 \times \frac{22}{7} \times 3.5 (10+7)$
$= 22 \times 17 = 374$ cm$^2$.
8. Number of lead shots dropped.
Ans: 100
Vol of Water = Vol of Cone = $\frac{1}{3}\pi (5)^2(8) = \frac{200}{3}\pi$.
Water flowed out = $\frac{1}{4} \times \frac{200}{3}\pi = \frac{50}{3}\pi$.
Vol of 1 shot = $\frac{4}{3}\pi (0.5)^3 = \frac{4}{3}\pi (1/8) = \frac{\pi}{6}$.
Number of shots = $\frac{50\pi/3}{\pi/6} = \frac{50}{3} \times 6 = 100$.
9. Mass of solid iron pole.
Ans: 892.26 kg
Vol = Vol Cyl 1 + Vol Cyl 2
$= \pi (12)^2(220) + \pi (8)^2(60)$.
$= 3.14 (31680 + 3840)$
$= 3.14 \times 35520 = 111532.8$ cm$^3$.
Mass = $111532.8 \times 8$ g
$= 892262.4$ g $= 892.26$ kg.
SECTION D: CASE STUDY ANSWERS
10. Case Study: Top (Lattu)
(i) $r = 3.5/2 = 1.75$. Height of cone $h = 5 - 1.75 = 3.25$ cm.
(ii) $l = \sqrt{h^2 + r^2} = \sqrt{3.25^2 + 1.75^2}$
$= \sqrt{10.5625 + 3.0625} = \sqrt{13.625} \approx 3.7$ cm.
(iii) TSA = CSA Hemi + CSA Cone = $2\pi r^2 + \pi rl$.
$= \pi r(2r+l)$.
$= \frac{22}{7} \times 1.75 (3.5 + 3.7)$
$= 5.5 \times 7.2 = 39.6$ cm$^2$.
