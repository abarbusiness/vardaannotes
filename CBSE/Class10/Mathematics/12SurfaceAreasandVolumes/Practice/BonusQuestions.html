Chapter Practice Sheet: Surface Areas and Volumes

Class: 10 (CBSE) Subject: Mathematics Max. Marks: 25
SECTION A: OBJECTIVE TYPE QUESTIONS (1 Mark Each)
1. Two cubes each of volume 64 cm$^3$ are joined end to end. The surface area of the resulting cuboid is:
  1. 128 cm$^2$
  2. 160 cm$^2$
  3. 140 cm$^2$
  4. 180 cm$^2$
[1]
2. A solid is in the form of a cone standing on a hemisphere with both their radii being equal to 1 cm and the height of the cone is equal to its radius. Find the volume of the solid in terms of $\pi$.
  1. $\pi$ cm$^3$
  2. $2\pi$ cm$^3$
  3. $\frac{4}{3}\pi$ cm$^3$
  4. $\frac{2}{3}\pi$ cm$^3$
[1]
3. A metallic sphere of radius 4.2 cm is melted and recast into the shape of a cylinder of radius 6 cm. Find the height of the cylinder.
  1. 2.74 cm
  2. 2.84 cm
  3. 2.64 cm
  4. 2.54 cm
[1]
SECTION B: SHORT ANSWER TYPE QUESTIONS (2 Marks Each)
4. A medicine capsule is in the shape of a cylinder with two hemispheres stuck to each of its ends. The length of the entire capsule is 14 mm and the diameter of the capsule is 5 mm. Find its surface area.
[2]
5. A tent is in the shape of a cylinder surmounted by a conical top. If the height and diameter of the cylindrical part are 2.1 m and 4 m respectively, and the slant height of the top is 2.8 m, find the area of the canvas used for making the tent.
[2]
6. From a solid cylinder whose height is 2.4 cm and diameter 1.4 cm, a conical cavity of the same height and same diameter is hollowed out. Find the total surface area of the remaining solid to the nearest cm$^2$.
[2]
SECTION C: LONG ANSWER TYPE QUESTIONS (3 Marks Each)
7. A wooden article was made by scooping out a hemisphere from each end of a solid cylinder. If the height of the cylinder is 10 cm, and its base is of radius 3.5 cm, find the total surface area of the article.
[3]
8. A vessel is in the form of an inverted cone. Its height is 8 cm and the radius of its top, which is open, is 5 cm. It is filled with water up to the brim. When lead shots, each of which is a sphere of radius 0.5 cm are dropped into the vessel, one-fourth of the water flows out. Find the number of lead shots dropped in the vessel.
[3]
9. A solid iron pole consists of a cylinder of height 220 cm and base diameter 24 cm, which is surmounted by another cylinder of height 60 cm and radius 8 cm. Find the mass of the pole, given that 1 cm$^3$ of iron has approximately 8g mass. (Use $\pi = 3.14$)
[3]
SECTION D: CASE STUDY (4 Marks)
10. Case Study: Construction Block
Rasheed got a playing top (lattu) as his birthday present, which surprisingly had no colour on it. He wanted to colour it with his crayons. The top is shaped like a cone surmounted by a hemisphere. The entire top is 5 cm in height and the diameter of the top is 3.5 cm.

(i) Find the height of the conical part. (1 Mark)
(ii) Calculate the slant height of the cone. (2 Marks)
(iii) Find the Total Surface Area of the toy to be colored. (1 Mark)
[4]
