Q19. Assertion (A): In the given figure, a toy is in the form of a cylinder surmounted by a hemisphere... Volume is \(81\pi\). (r=3, h=7).
Reason (R): Volume is sum of volumes.
  • (A) Both A and R are true and R is correct explanation of A
  • (B) Both A and R are true but R is not correct explanation of A
  • (C) A is true but R is false
  • (D) A is false but R is true
Cylinder: \(r=3, h=7\). Vol = \(\pi(3)^2(7) = 63\pi\).
Hemisphere: \(r=3\). Vol = \(\frac{2}{3}\pi(3)^3 = 18\pi\).
Total = \(63\pi + 18\pi = 81\pi\).
Assertion is True. Reason is True and Explains A.
Answer: (A)
5 Marks 30-S
Q34. (a) From a solid cylinder of height 2.4 cm and radius 0.7 cm, a conical cavity of the same height and same radius is hollowed out. Find the volume and total surface area of the remaining solid.
OR
Q34. (b)
A carpenter is making a wooden toy (lattu) which is conical in shape and surmounted by a hemisphere. Ratio of height of hemisphere and cone is 3:4. Radius 2.1 cm. Find volume and area to be painted.
(a) Vol = Vol Cyl - Vol Cone = \(\pi r^2h - \frac{1}{3}\pi r^2h = \frac{2}{3}\pi r^2h\).
Vol = \(\frac{2}{3} \frac{22}{7} (0.7)^2(2.4) = 1.232 \text{ cm}^3\).
TSA = CSA Cyl + CSA Cone + Area Base.
Slant height \(l = \sqrt{2.4^2 + 0.7^2} = \sqrt{5.76 + 0.49} = \sqrt{6.25} = 2.5\).
TSA = \(2\pi rh + \pi rl + \pi r^2 = \pi r(2h + l + r)\).
TSA = \(\frac{22}{7}(0.7)(4.8 + 2.5 + 0.7) = 2.2(8) = 17.6 \text{ cm}^2\).

(b) \(h_{hemisphere} = r = 2.1\).
Ratio \(h_{hem} : h_{cone} = 3 : 4 \Rightarrow 2.1 : h_{cone} = 3 : 4 \Rightarrow h_{cone} = 2.8\).
Vol = Vol Cone + Vol Hem = \(\frac{1}{3}\pi r^2h + \frac{2}{3}\pi r^3\).
Area = CSA Cone + CSA Hem.

Q19. Assertion (A): Volume of a cone of height h and radius r is \(\frac{1}{3} \pi r^2 h\).
Reason (R): Volume of a cone is 3 times the volume of a cylinder of same height and radius.
[Image: 2025-30-S-Question19]
  • (A) Both Assertion (A) and Reason (R) are true and Reason (R) is the correct explanation of Assertion (A).
  • (B) Both Assertion (A) and Reason (R) are true, but Reason (R) is not the correct explanation of Assertion (A).
  • (C) Assertion (A) is true, but Reason (R) is false.
  • (D) Assertion (A) is false, but Reason (R) is true.
Assertion is standard formula, hence True.
Reason: Volume of cone = \(\frac{1}{3}\) Volume of Cylinder. So cone is 1/3, not 3 times. Reason is False.
Answer: (C)

Q34. A tent is in the shape of a cylinder surmounted by a conical top. If the height and diameter of the cylindrical part are 2.1 m and 4 m respectively, and the slant height of the top is 2.8 m, find the area of the canvas used for making the tent. Also, find the cost of the canvas of the tent at the rate of ₹ 500 per m\({}^2\). (Note that the base of the tent will not be covered with canvas.)
[Image: 2025-30-S-Question34]
Cylinder: \(h = 2.1\) m, \(d = 4\) m \(\Rightarrow r = 2\) m.
Cone: \(l = 2.8\) m, \(r = 2\) m.
Canvas Area = CSA(Cylinder) + CSA(Cone).
\(= 2\pi r h + \pi r l = \pi r (2h + l)\).
\(= \frac{22}{7} \times 2 \times (2(2.1) + 2.8) = \frac{44}{7} \times (4.2 + 2.8) = \frac{44}{7} \times 7 = 44\) m\({}^2\).
Cost = Area \(\times\) Rate = \(44 \times 500 = \) ₹ 22,000.

Q29. A room is in the form of a cylinder surmounted by a hemispherical dome. The base radius of the hemisphere is one-half the height of the cylindrical part. Find the height of the cylindrical part if the room contains \(\frac{1408}{21} \text{ m}^3\) of air.
Let radius be \(r\) and height of cylinder be \(h_{cyl}\).
Given \(r = h_{cyl}/2 \Rightarrow h_{cyl} = 2r\).
Volume = Vol Cyl + Vol Hemi = \(\pi r^2(2r) + \frac{2}{3}\pi r^3 = 2\pi r^3 + \frac{2}{3}\pi r^3 = \frac{8}{3}\pi r^3\).
\(\frac{8}{3} \times \frac{22}{7} \times r^3 = \frac{1408}{21} \Rightarrow \frac{176}{21}r^3 = \frac{1408}{21}\).
\(176r^3 = 1408 \Rightarrow r^3 = 8 \Rightarrow r = 2\).
Height of cylindrical part = \(2r = 4\) m.

Q36. Case Study - 1: A juice seller serves his customers using glasses as shown in figure. The inner diameter of the cylindrical glass was 6.4 cm but the bottom of the glass had a conical raised portion which reduced the capacity of the glass. The height of the glass was 10 cm and the height of the conical portion was 3 cm.
[Image: 2025-30-2-Question36]
(i) Find the Apparent Capacity of the glass.
(ii) Find the Actual Capacity of the glass.
(iii) (a) Find the ratio of Apparent Capacity to Actual Capacity.
OR
(iii) (b) If the juice seller sells the juice at the rate of ₹ 100 per litre, find the cost of 100 glasses of juice. (\(\pi = 3.14\)).
Given: Diameter \(d = 6.4\) cm \(\Rightarrow r = 3.2\) cm.
Height of glass \(H = 10\) cm. Height of cone \(h = 3\) cm.

(i) Apparent Capacity (Volume of Cylinder):
\(V_{cyl} = \pi r^2 H = 3.14 \times (3.2)^2 \times 10\).
\(= 3.14 \times 10.24 \times 10 = 321.536\) cm\(^3\).

(ii) Actual Capacity:
Actual Vol = \(V_{cyl} - V_{cone}\).
\(V_{cone} = \frac{1}{3} \pi r^2 h = \frac{1}{3} \times 3.14 \times (3.2)^2 \times 3\).
\(= 3.14 \times 10.24 = 32.1536\) cm\(^3\).
Actual Vol = \(321.536 - 32.1536 = 289.3824\) cm\(^3\) (approx \(289.38\) cm\(^3\)).

(iii) (a):
Ratio = \(321.536 : 289.3824 = 10 : 9\).
(Since \(V_{cone} = \frac{1}{3} \pi r^2 (3) = \pi r^2\). \(V_{cyl} = 10 \pi r^2\). Ratio \(10:9\) is accurate).

(iii) (b):
Total Vol for 100 glasses = \(100 \times 289.3824 = 28938.24\) cm\(^3\).
In Litres: \(28938.24 / 1000 = 28.938\) Litres.
Cost = \(28.938 \times 100\) = ₹ \(2893.8\).

Q32. A solid toy is in the form of a hemisphere surmounted by a right circular cone. Height of cone 2 cm, diameter 4 cm. Find volume and surface area. (\(\pi = 3.14\))
[Image: 2025-30-03-32]
\(r = 2, h = 2\). Slant height \(l = \sqrt{4 + 4} = 2\sqrt{2} \approx 2.828\).
Volume = \(\frac{1}{3}\pi r^2h + \frac{2}{3}\pi r^3 = \frac{1}{3}\pi(4)(2) + \frac{2}{3}\pi(8) = \frac{8\pi}{3} + \frac{16\pi}{3} = 8\pi \approx 25.12\) cm³.
Surface Area = \(\pi rl + 2\pi r^2 = \pi(2)(2.828) + 2\pi(4) = 5.656\pi + 8\pi = 13.656 \times 3.14 \approx 42.88\) cm².

Q33 (a). From one face of a solid wooden cube of side 14 cm, maximum number of hemispheres of diameter 1.4 cm are scooped out. Find the total number of hemispheres and the total surface area of the remaining solid.
OR
Q33 (b). From a solid cylinder of height 24 cm and radius 5 cm, two cones of height 12 cm and radius 5 cm are hollowed out. Find the volume and surface area of the remaining solid.
(a): Diameter = 1.4 cm. Side = 14 cm. Hemispheres along one side = \(14/1.4 = 10\).
Total hemispheres = \(10 \times 10 = 100\).
SA = TSA of Cube - Area of 100 circles + CSA of 100 hemispheres.
= \(6(196) - 100(\pi r^2) + 100(2\pi r^2) = 6(196) + 100(\pi r^2)\).
= \(1176 + 100(22/7)(0.49) = 1176 + 154 = 1330\) cm².

(b): Volume = \(\pi r^2 H - 2(\frac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h) = \pi(25)(24) - \frac{2}{3}\pi(25)(12) = 600\pi - 200\pi = 400\pi \approx 1256\) cm³.
SA = \(2\pi rH + 2(\pi rl) = 2\pi(5)(24) + 2\pi(5)(13) = 240\pi + 130\pi = 370\pi \approx 1162\) cm².

Q5. If a cone of greatest volume is hollowed from a cylinder, ratio of remaining wood to cone is
  • (A) 1 : 1
  • (B) 1 : 3
  • (C) 2 : 1
  • (D) 3 : 1
Vol Cylinder = \(\pi r^2 h\).
Vol Cone (greatest) = \(\frac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h\).
Vol Remaining = \(\pi r^2 h - \frac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h = \frac{2}{3}\pi r^2 h\).
Ratio = \(\frac{2}{3}\pi r^2 h : \frac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h = 2 : 1\).
Correct Option: (C)

Q33. Fermentation tanks are designed in the form of cylinder mounted on a cone as shown below:
The total height of the tank is 3.3 m and height of conical part is 1.2 m. The diameter of the cylindrical as well as conical part is 1 m.
Find the capacity of the tank. If the level of liquid in the tank is 0.7 m from the top, find the surface area of the tank in contact with liquid.
[Image: 30-6-3_Mathematics Standard.pdf - Question 33]
Radius \(r = 0.5\) m. Total H = 3.3 m. Cone H \((h_c)\) = 1.2 m.
Cylinder H \((h_{cyl})\) = \(3.3 - 1.2 = 2.1\) m.

1. Capacity:
\(V = V_{cyl} + V_{cone} = \pi r^2 h_{cyl} + \frac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h_c\)
\(= \pi(0.5)^2(2.1) + \frac{1}{3}\pi(0.5)^2(1.2)\)
\(= 0.25\pi(2.1 + 0.4) = 0.25\pi(2.5) = 0.625\pi \approx 1.96\) m³.

2. Surface Area in contact with liquid:
Liquid level is 0.7 m from top. So liquid height in cylinder = \(2.1 - 0.7 = 1.4\) m.
Liquid fills entire cone and 1.4 m of cylinder.
Area = CSA Cone + CSA Cylinder (height 1.4 m).
Slant height \(l = \sqrt{1.2^2 + 0.5^2} = \sqrt{1.44 + 0.25} = \sqrt{1.69} = 1.3\) m.
CSA Cone = \(\pi r l = \pi(0.5)(1.3) = 0.65\pi\).
CSA Cyl = \(2\pi r h = 2\pi(0.5)(1.4) = 1.4\pi\).
Total Area = \(2.05\pi \approx 6.44\) m².
