Chapter 1: Real Numbers

[1 Mark]
Q10. \(\sqrt{0.4}\) is a/an
  • (A) natural number
  • (B) integer
  • (C) rational number
  • (D) irrational number
Solution: We have \(\sqrt{0.4} = \sqrt{\frac{4}{10}} = \frac{2}{\sqrt{10}}\). Since \(\sqrt{10}\) is irrational, \(\sqrt{0.4}\) is irrational.
Correct Option: (D)
[1 Mark]
Q11. Which of the following cannot be the unit digit of \(8^n\)?
  • (A) 4
  • (B) 2
  • (C) 0
  • (D) 6
Solution: Powers of 8 cycle {8, 4, 2, 6}. 0 is never a unit digit.
Correct Option: (C)
[1 Mark]
Q20. Assertion (A): \(LCM(2x, 4y) = 4xy\) for odd primes x, y.
Reason (R): LCM is multiple of HCF.
  • (B) Both true but R not explanation.
Solution: LCM of \(2x, 4y\) is \(4xy\). Assertion True. Reason True but distinct logic.
Correct Option: (B)
[3 Marks]
Q30 (a). Prove \(\sqrt{5}\) is irrational.
Solution: Proof by contradiction method assuming \(\sqrt{5} = a/b\). Shows 5 divides both a and b. Contradiction.
OR
Q30 (b). Check \(pqr + q\) composite. Examples.
Solution: \(q(pr+1)\) is composite. Examples: \(3 \cdot 5 \cdot 7 + 1 = 106\) (Composite), \(2 \cdot 3 \cdot 5 + 1 = 31\) (Prime).

Chapter 2: Polynomials

[1 Mark]
Q13. Zeroes of \(ax^2 + bx + 2a/b\) are reciprocal. Value of b?
Solution: Product = 1. \(1 = \frac{2a/b}{a} \Rightarrow b=2\).
Correct Option: (A)
[3 Marks]
Q31. Find zeroes of \(r(x) = 4x^2 + 3x - 1\). New poly with reciprocal zeroes.
Solution: Zeroes \(1/4, -1\). New zeroes \(4, -1\). Poly \(x^2 - 3x - 4\).
