1. Congruence vs Similarity

Congruent Figures: Same Shape AND Same Size (e.g., two identical coins).

Similar Figures: Same Shape but Size may vary (e.g., a large circle and a small circle).

Conditions for Similarity of Polygons:

  1. Corresponding angles are equal.
  2. Corresponding sides are in the same ratio (or proportion).

2. Basic Proportionality Theorem (BPT)

Also known as Thales Theorem. This is the most important theorem for the exam.

THEOREM 6.1

Statement: If a line is drawn parallel to one side of a triangle to intersect the other two sides in distinct points, the other two sides are divided in the same ratio.

Given: In $\triangle ABC$, $DE \parallel BC$.

To Prove: $\frac{AD}{DB} = \frac{AE}{EC}$

Proof of BPT

Construction: Join $BE$ and $CD$. Draw $DM \perp AC$ and $EN \perp AB$.

AI IMAGE PROMPT:
Generate a professional, high-definition educational geometry diagram in landscape orientation on a pure white background.
Subject: A large, clean Triangle labeled ABC at the vertices (Top A, Bottom Left B, Bottom Right C). Draw a horizontal line DE cutting sides AB and AC, where D lies on AB and E lies on AC. DE should be parallel to BC.
Construction Details: Draw dashed/dotted lines connecting B to E and C to D. Draw a perpendicular dropped from D to side AC, labeled M (so DM $\perp$ AC). Draw a perpendicular dropped from E to side AB, labeled N (so EN $\perp$ AB).
Style: Minimalist black stroke lines, no shading.
Labels: Use a serif font like 'Computer Modern' or 'Times New Roman' for vertices A, B, C, D, E, M, N. Text should be crisp black.
  1. Area($\triangle ADE$) = $\frac{1}{2} \times AD \times EN$.
  2. Area($\triangle BDE$) = $\frac{1}{2} \times DB \times EN$.
  3. $\frac{Area(ADE)}{Area(BDE)} = \frac{\frac{1}{2} \cdot AD \cdot EN}{\frac{1}{2} \cdot DB \cdot EN} = \frac{AD}{DB}$ ... (i)
  4. Similarly, $\frac{Area(ADE)}{Area(DEC)} = \frac{\frac{1}{2} \cdot AE \cdot DM}{\frac{1}{2} \cdot EC \cdot DM} = \frac{AE}{EC}$ ... (ii)
  5. Since $\triangle BDE$ and $\triangle DEC$ are on the same base $DE$ and between same parallel lines $BC$ and $DE$, their areas are equal.
  6. From (i) and (ii), $\frac{AD}{DB} = \frac{AE}{EC}$. **Hence Proved.**
COROLLARY (Useful for sums) From BPT, we can also say: $$\frac{AB}{DB} = \frac{AC}{EC} \quad \text{and} \quad \frac{AD}{AB} = \frac{AE}{AC}$$

Converse of BPT (Theorem 6.2)

If a line divides any two sides of a triangle in the same ratio, then the line is parallel to the third side.

3. Criteria for Similarity of Triangles

For two triangles $\triangle ABC$ and $\triangle PQR$ to be similar, we use specific criteria.

AAA (Angle-Angle-Angle) / AA Similarity

If in two triangles, corresponding angles are equal, then their corresponding sides are in the same ratio and hence the two triangles are similar.

AI IMAGE PROMPT:
Generate a professional geometry diagram on a pure white background in landscape.
Subject: Two triangles side-by-side: A larger triangle labeled ABC and a smaller triangle labeled PQR.
Markings: Mark Angle A and Angle P with a single arc (indicating equality). Mark Angle B and Angle Q with a double arc. Mark Angle C and Angle R with a triple arc.
Style: Clean black lines. Serif font for labels. Ensure clear visual distinction in size between the two triangles to demonstrate Similarity (same shape, different size).
AA Similarity: If two angles of one triangle are equal to two angles of another, the third angle is automatically equal (Angle Sum Property). So, finding 2 equal pairs is enough to prove similarity.

SSS (Side-Side-Side) Similarity

If in two triangles, sides of one triangle are proportional to (i.e., in the same ratio of) the sides of the other triangle, then their corresponding angles are equal and the triangles are similar.

Condition: $\frac{AB}{PQ} = \frac{BC}{QR} = \frac{CA}{RP}$

SAS (Side-Angle-Side) Similarity

If one angle of a triangle is equal to one angle of the other triangle and the sides including these angles are proportional, then the two triangles are similar.

Condition: $\angle A = \angle P$ and $\frac{AB}{PQ} = \frac{AC}{PR}$

4. Important Exam Problems (PYQ Trends)

Type 1: The Trapezium Problem

Q: ABCD is a trapezium with $AB \parallel DC$. Diagonals AC and BD intersect at O. Show that $\frac{AO}{BO} = \frac{CO}{DO}$.

Solution:

AI IMAGE PROMPT:
Generate a professional geometry diagram on a pure white background in landscape.
Subject: A single Trapezium labeled ABCD. The top side AB should be shorter than the bottom side DC. AB is parallel to DC.
Details: Draw two diagonals: AC and BD. Mark their point of intersection as 'O'.
Style: High-contrast black lines, white background. Serif font for labels A, B, C, D, O.
  1. In $\triangle AOB$ and $\triangle DOC$:
  2. $\angle AOB = \angle DOC$ (Vertically opposite angles).
  3. $\angle OAB = \angle OCD$ (Alternate interior angles as $AB \parallel DC$).
  4. By AA Similarity, $\triangle AOB \sim \triangle COD$.
  5. Therefore, $\frac{AO}{CO} = \frac{OB}{OD}$.
  6. Rearranging: $\frac{AO}{BO} = \frac{CO}{DO}$.

Type 2: Shadow/Height Problem

Q: A vertical pole of length 6m casts a shadow 4m long on the ground and at the same time a tower casts a shadow 28m long. Find the height of the tower.

Solution:

AI IMAGE PROMPT:
Generate a professional geometry diagram on a pure white background in landscape showing two separate figures side-by-side.
Figure 1 (Left): A vertical line segment labeled AB representing a 'Pole', casting a horizontal shadow labeled BC on the ground. A dotted line connects the top A to the tip of shadow C (representing sun ray).
Figure 2 (Right): A larger vertical line segment labeled PQ representing a 'Tower', casting a longer horizontal shadow labeled QR. A dotted line connects top P to tip R.
Style: The dotted 'sun ray' lines should appear parallel in both figures to imply same time of day. Clean black lines, Serif font labels.
  1. Let $AB$ be the pole and $BC$ be its shadow. Let $PQ$ be the tower and $QR$ be its shadow.
  2. At the same time, the angle of elevation of the sun is same for both.
  3. $\angle C = \angle R$ (Sun's elevation).
  4. $\angle B = \angle Q = 90^\circ$.
  5. By AA Similarity, $\triangle ABC \sim \triangle PQR$.
  6. $\frac{AB}{PQ} = \frac{BC}{QR} \Rightarrow \frac{6}{h} = \frac{4}{28}$.
  7. $\frac{6}{h} = \frac{1}{7} \Rightarrow h = 42$ m.
  8. Height of the tower is 42 m.
