Master Notes: Triangles

Class: 10 (CBSE) Subject: Mathematics Worksheet Solutions
SECTION A: OBJECTIVE TYPE QUESTIONS
1. If $\triangle ABC \sim \triangle PQR$... then PQ is:
Ans: (a) 12 cm
Area(ABC)/Area(PQR) = $(AB/PQ)^2$.
$81/144 = (9/PQ)^2$ $\Rightarrow$ $9/12 = 9/PQ$
$\Rightarrow PQ = 12$ cm.
2. In $\triangle ABC$, DE || BC... then EC is:
Ans: (c) 2 cm
By BPT: $AD/DB = AE/EC$.
$1.5/3 = 1/EC$ $\Rightarrow$ $1/2 = 1/EC$
$\Rightarrow EC = 2$ cm.
3. The lengths of the diagonals of a rhombus are 24 cm and 32 cm. The side is:
Ans: (c) 20 cm
Diagonals bisect at 90 deg. Half diag: 12, 16.
Side = $\sqrt{12^2 + 16^2}$
$= \sqrt{144+256}$
$= \sqrt{400}$ $= 20$ cm.
4. Two poles of height 6 m and 11 m... Distance between tops is:
Ans: (a) 13 m
Difference in height = $11-6 = 5$ m. Base = 12 m.
Hypotenuse = $\sqrt{5^2 + 12^2}$
$= \sqrt{25+144}$
$= \sqrt{169}$ $= 13$ m.
5. A vertical pole of length 6 m casts a shadow 4 m... Height of tower?
Ans: (b) 42 m
Similar triangles. $H/28 = 6/4$.
$H = (6/4) \times 28 = 1.5 \times 28 = 42$ m.
6. Two isosceles triangles have equal angles... Ratio of corresponding heights:
Ans: (a) 4:5
Ratio of areas = Square of ratio of corresponding heights.
$h_1^2/h_2^2 = 16/25 \Rightarrow h_1/h_2 = 4/5$.
SECTION B: SHORT ANSWER TYPE QUESTIONS
7. Ratio of the area of $\triangle PQR$ to area of $\triangle ABC$.
Ans: 1:4
Line joining midpoints divides triangle into 4 congruent triangles.
Area(PQR) = 1/4 Area(ABC).
8. In a trapezium ABCD... Show that $AO/BO = CO/DO$.
$\triangle AOB \sim \triangle COD$ (AA similarity).
$AO/CO = BO/DO \Rightarrow AO/BO = CO/DO$.
9. Perimeters of ABC and PQR are 60 and 48... Find AB.
Ans: 10 cm
Ratio of perimeters = Ratio of sides.
$60/48 = AB/8 \Rightarrow 5/4 = AB/8 \Rightarrow AB = 10$.
10. If $\triangle ABE \cong \triangle ACD$, show that $\triangle ADE \sim \triangle ABC$.
$AB=AC$ and $AE=AD$ (CPCT).
$AD/AB = AE/AC$. Angle A is common.
By SAS similarity, $\triangle ADE \sim \triangle ABC$.
SECTION C: LONG ANSWER TYPE QUESTIONS
11. Show that $CA^2 = CB \cdot CD$.
In $\triangle ADC$ and $\triangle BAC$:
$\angle ADC = \angle BAC$ (Given), $\angle C = \angle C$ (Common).
So $\triangle ADC \sim \triangle BAC$.
$CA/CB = CD/CA \Rightarrow CA^2 = CB \cdot CD$.
12. Show that $\triangle ABC \sim \triangle PQR$.
Given $AB/PQ = BC/QR = AD/PM$.
$BC/QR = 2BD/2QM = BD/QM$.
So $AB/PQ = BD/QM = AD/PM \Rightarrow \triangle ABD \sim \triangle PQM$ (SSS).
Therefore $\angle B = \angle Q$.
In $\triangle ABC$ and $\triangle PQR$, $AB/PQ = BC/QR$ and $\angle B = \angle Q$.
So $\triangle ABC \sim \triangle PQR$ (SAS).
13. Prove that ratio of areas... equal to square of ratio of corresponding medians.
We know $\triangle ABC \sim \triangle PQR \Rightarrow Area(ABC)/Area(PQR) = (AB/PQ)^2$.
Also $\triangle ABD \sim \triangle PQM$ (as shown in Q12 logic) $\Rightarrow$ $AB/PQ = AD/PM$.
Hence $Area(ABC)/Area(PQR) = (AD/PM)^2$.
14. Prove that $4(BL^2 + CM^2) = 5 BC^2$.
In $\triangle ABL$, $BL^2 = AB^2 + AL^2 = AB^2 + (AC/2)^2$.
In $\triangle ACM$, $CM^2 = AC^2 + AM^2 = AC^2 + (AB/2)^2$.
$BL^2 + CM^2 = AB^2 + AC^2/4 + AC^2 + AB^2/4$
$= 5/4(AB^2 + AC^2)$.
$4(BL^2 + CM^2) = 5(BC^2)$. (Since $AB^2+AC^2 = BC^2$).
SECTION D: CASE STUDY
15. Case Study: Light House
(i) [Sketch: Vertical pole AB=3.6m, Boy CD=0.9m, Shadow DE=x].
(ii) Let shadow length be x. In 4 sec, dist covered BD = $1.2 \times 4 = 4.8$ m.
$\triangle ABE \sim \triangle CDE$. $AB/CD = BE/DE$.
$3.6/0.9 = (4.8+x)/x \Rightarrow 4 = (4.8+x)/x \Rightarrow 4x = 4.8 + x \Rightarrow 3x = 4.8$
$\Rightarrow x = 1.6$ m.
(iii) AA Similarity.
