Pair of Linear Equations in Two Variables

Class 10 Mathematics • Chapter 03 (Rationalized Syllabus)

1. Introduction

General Form: The general form of a pair of linear equations in two variables $x$ and $y$ is:

  • $a_1x + b_1y + c_1 = 0$
  • $a_2x + b_2y + c_2 = 0$

Where $a_1, b_1, c_1, a_2, b_2, c_2$ are real numbers and $a_1^2 + b_1^2 \neq 0$, $a_2^2 + b_2^2 \neq 0$.

2. Graphical Method & Solvability

When we draw two lines on a plane, three possibilities exist:

Case 1: Intersecting Lines

The lines intersect at exactly one point. The system has a Unique Solution and is Consistent.

$$ \frac{a_1}{a_2} \neq \frac{b_1}{b_2} $$

Case 2: Coincident Lines (Overlapping)

The lines are identical (one on top of another). The system has Infinitely Many Solutions and is Consistent (Dependent).

$$ \frac{a_1}{a_2} = \frac{b_1}{b_2} = \frac{c_1}{c_2} $$

Case 3: Parallel Lines

The lines never meet. The system has No Solution and is Inconsistent.

$$ \frac{a_1}{a_2} = \frac{b_1}{b_2} \neq \frac{c_1}{c_2} $$

3. Algebraic Methods

Method 1: Substitution Method

Steps:

  1. Pick one equation and express one variable (say $y$) in terms of the other ($x$).
  2. Substitute this value of $y$ into the other equation. This reduces it to a linear equation in one variable.
  3. Solve for $x$.
  4. Substitute $x$ back into Step 1 to find $y$.

Example: Solve $x + y = 14$ and $x - y = 4$.

  1. From (2): $x = 4 + y$.
  2. Substitute in (1): $(4 + y) + y = 14 \Rightarrow 4 + 2y = 14 \Rightarrow 2y = 10 \Rightarrow y = 5$.
  3. Put $y=5$ in Step 1: $x = 4 + 5 = 9$.
  4. Solution: $x=9, y=5$.

Method 2: Elimination Method (Recommended)

Steps:

  1. Multiply equations by constants so that the coefficient of one variable (x or y) becomes numerically equal.
  2. Add or subtract the equations to eliminate that variable.
  3. Solve for the remaining variable.
  4. Substitute back to find the first variable.

Example: $9x - 4y = 2000$ and $7x - 3y = 2000$.

  1. Multiply eq(1) by 3 and eq(2) by 4 to equalize $y$ coefficients to 12.
  2. $27x - 12y = 6000$
    $28x - 12y = 8000$
  3. Subtract: $(28x - 27x) = 8000 - 6000 \Rightarrow x = 2000$.
  4. Substitute $x$ in eq(1): $9(2000) - 4y = 2000 \Rightarrow 18000 - 2000 = 4y \Rightarrow 16000 = 4y \Rightarrow y = 4000$.

4. Important Exam Word Problems (Trends)

Word problems are the heart of this chapter. Here are the most frequent types.

Type 1: Age Problems

Question: Five years hence, the age of Jacob will be three times that of his son. Five years ago, Jacob's age was seven times that of his son. Find their present ages.

Solution:

  1. Let Jacob's present age be $x$ and son's age be $y$.
  2. Case 1 (5 years hence): $(x+5) = 3(y+5) \Rightarrow x - 3y = 10$ ...(i)
  3. Case 2 (5 years ago): $(x-5) = 7(y-5) \Rightarrow x - 7y = -30$ ...(ii)
  4. Subtract (ii) from (i): $(x-3y) - (x-7y) = 10 - (-30) \Rightarrow 4y = 40 \Rightarrow y = 10$.
  5. Put $y$ in (i): $x - 30 = 10 \Rightarrow x = 40$.
  6. Answer: Jacob is 40 years old, Son is 10 years old.

Type 2: Fraction Problems

Question: A fraction becomes $9/11$ if 2 is added to both numerator and denominator. If 3 is added to both, it becomes $5/6$. Find the fraction.

Solution:

  1. Let the fraction be $x/y$.
  2. Case 1: $\frac{x+2}{y+2} = \frac{9}{11} \Rightarrow 11x + 22 = 9y + 18 \Rightarrow 11x - 9y = -4$ ...(i)
  3. Case 2: $\frac{x+3}{y+3} = \frac{5}{6} \Rightarrow 6x + 18 = 5y + 15 \Rightarrow 6x - 5y = -3$ ...(ii)
  4. Using Substitution or Elimination, we get $x = 7$ and $y = 9$.
  5. Answer: The fraction is $7/9$.

Type 3: Upstream & Downstream (Critical)

Question: A boat goes 30 km upstream and 44 km downstream in 10 hours. In 13 hours, it can go 40 km upstream and 55 km downstream. Determine the speed of the stream and that of the boat in still water.

CONCEPT TRICK Speed Downstream = $(x + y)$ km/h (Flow helps).
Speed Upstream = $(x - y)$ km/h (Flow opposes).
Time = Distance / Speed.

Solution:

  1. Let speed of boat = $x$ km/h, speed of stream = $y$ km/h.
  2. Case 1: $\frac{30}{x-y} + \frac{44}{x+y} = 10$.
  3. Case 2: $\frac{40}{x-y} + \frac{55}{x+y} = 13$.
  4. Put $\frac{1}{x-y} = u$ and $\frac{1}{x+y} = v$.
  5. Equations become:
    • $30u + 44v = 10$ ...(i)
    • $40u + 55v = 13$ ...(ii)
  6. Solving linear equations for $u$ and $v$: We get $u = 1/5$ and $v = 1/11$.
  7. So, $x - y = 5$ and $x + y = 11$.
  8. Adding both: $2x = 16 \Rightarrow x = 8$. Subtracting: $2y = 6 \Rightarrow y = 3$.
  9. Answer: Speed of boat = 8 km/h, Speed of stream = 3 km/h.

Type 4: Digits Problems

Question: The sum of digits of a two-digit number is 9. Also, nine times this number is twice the number obtained by reversing the order of the digits.

Solution:

Number = $10x + y$ (where $x$ is tens digit, $y$ is units digit).
Reversed Number = $10y + x$.
  1. $x + y = 9$ ...(i)
  2. $9(10x + y) = 2(10y + x)$.
  3. $90x + 9y = 20y + 2x \Rightarrow 88x - 11y = 0 \Rightarrow 8x = y$.
  4. Substitute $y=8x$ in eq(i): $x + 8x = 9 \Rightarrow 9x = 9 \Rightarrow x = 1$.
  5. Then $y = 8(1) = 8$.
  6. Answer: The number is 18.
