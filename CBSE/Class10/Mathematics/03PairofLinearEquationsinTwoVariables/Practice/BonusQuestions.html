Chapter Practice Sheet: Pair of Linear Equations

Class: 10 (CBSE) Subject: Mathematics Max. Marks: 30
SECTION A: OBJECTIVE TYPE QUESTIONS (1 Mark Each)
1. The pair of equations $x+2y-5=0$ and $-3x-6y+15=0$ have:
  1. A unique solution
  2. Exactly two solutions
  3. Infinitely many solutions
  4. No solution
[1]
2. If the lines given by $3x + 2ky = 2$ and $2x + 5y = 1$ are parallel, then the value of k is:
  1. -5/4
  2. 2/5
  3. 15/4
  4. 3/2
[1]
3. The value of k for which the system of equations $x + 2y = 3$ and $5x + ky + 7 = 0$ has no solution is:
  1. 10
  2. 6
  3. 3
  4. 1
[1]
4. The solution of the equations $x-y=2$ and $x+y=4$ is:
  1. 3, 1
  2. 4, 0
  3. 1, 3
  4. 2, 2
[1]
5. If a pair of linear equations is consistent, then the lines will be:
  1. Parallel
  2. Always coincident
  3. Intersecting or coincident
  4. Always intersecting
[1]
6. The sum of the digits of a two-digit number is 9. If 27 is added to it, the digits get reversed. The number is:
  1. 27
  2. 72
  3. 45
  4. 36
[1]
SECTION B: SHORT ANSWER TYPE QUESTIONS (2 Marks Each)
7. Solve for x and y: $2x + 3y = 11$ and $2x - 4y = -24$.
[2]
8. For what value of k will the following system of linear equations have infinite solutions?
$kx + 3y = (k-3)$, $12x + ky = k$
[2]
9. Find the values of $\alpha$ and $\beta$ for which the following pair of linear equations has infinitely many solutions:
$2x + 3y = 7$, $2\alpha x + (\alpha + \beta)y = 28$
[2]
10. Solve for x and y: $\frac{2}{x} + \frac{3}{y} = 13$, $\frac{5}{x} - \frac{4}{y} = -2$.
[2]
SECTION C: LONG ANSWER TYPE QUESTIONS (3 Marks Each)
11. A fraction becomes 9/11 if 2 is added to both the numerator and the denominator. If 3 is added to both the numerator and the denominator it becomes 5/6. Find the fraction.
[3]
12. Five years hence, the age of Jacob will be three times that of his son. Five years ago, Jacob's age was seven times that of his son. What are their present ages?
[3]
13. Yarn A costs 4 rupees per meter and Yarn B costs 5 rupees per meter. A total of 100 meters of yarn was purchased for 440 rupees. How much of each yarn was purchased?
[3]
14. Solve graphically the system of linear equations: $x - y + 1 = 0$ and $3x + 2y - 12 = 0$. Determine the coordinates of the vertices of the triangle formed by these lines and the x-axis.
[3]
SECTION D: CASE STUDY (4 Marks)
15. Case Study: Taxi Charges
A taxi charge in a city consists of a fixed charge together with the charge for the distance covered. For a distance of 10 km, the charge paid is ₹105 and for a journey of 15 km, the charge paid is ₹155.

(i) Form the linear equations representing the situation. (1 Mark)
(ii) Find the fixed charge. (1 Mark)
(iii) Find the charge per kilometer. (1 Mark)
(iv) How much does a person have to pay for travelling a distance of 25 km? (1 Mark)
[4]
