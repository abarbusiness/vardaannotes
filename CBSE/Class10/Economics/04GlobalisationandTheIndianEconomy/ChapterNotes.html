Globalization and the Indian Economy

Class 10 Economics • Chapter 04

1. Production Across Countries

Earlier, only raw materials, food stuff and finished products crossed boundaries. Now, MNCs play a major role.

Multinational Corporations (MNCs)

A company that owns or controls production in more than one nation.
Why? They set up offices/factories where they get cheap labour and other resources (to maximize profit).
Spreading Production: China (Manufacturing cheap), India (Engineers, English speaking youth for BPOs).

2. Interlinking Production

Investment: Money spent to buy assets like land, building, machines.
Foreign Investment: Investment made by MNCs.

MNCs interlink production in 3 ways:

  1. Setting up partnerships with local companies (MNC provides money + tech).
  2. Buying up local companies (Most common route). E.g., Cargill Foods bought Parakh Foods.
  3. Placing orders with small producers (Garments/Footwear).

3. Factors Enabled Globalization

1. Technology

  • Transportation: Containers for shipping reduced costs. Air travel expedited delivery.
  • ICT: Computers, Internet, Email, Voice mail made communication instant and cheap. Satellite communication devices.

2. Liberalization of Foreign Trade

Removing barriers or restrictions set by govt.
Post-1947: India put barriers (Tax on imports) to protect domestic producers from foreign competition.
New Economic Policy (1991): Govt realized time had come for Indian producers to compete globally to improve quality. Barriers removed.

4. World Trade Organization (WTO)

Aim: Liberalize international trade. Establish rules for fair trade.
Criticism: Developed countries (US/EU) have unfairly retained trade barriers (Subsidies to farmers) while forcing developing countries to remove them.

5. Impact of Globalization in India

Positive Impact

  • Consumers: Greater choice, lower prices, better quality. Standard of living improved.
  • Jobs: New jobs in IT, data entry, accounting.
  • Indian MNCs: Tata Motors, Infosys, Ranbaxy, Asian Paints became global.

Negative Impact (Challenges)

  • Small Producers: Hit hard by competition. Batteries/Toys/Tyres manufacturing units closed.
  • Labour Laws: Flexibilisation of labour laws. Workers hired on temporary basis (no job security) to cut costs.

6. The Struggle for Fair Globalization

Globalization is a reality. Question is how to make it 'Fair'.
Govt Role:

  • Ensure labour laws are implemented.
  • Support small producers to improve performance.
  • Use trade barriers if necessary to protect interests.
  • Align with other developing countries to fight domination of developed countries in WTO.

SEZ (Special Economic Zones): Industrial zones with world class facilities (Electricity, Water, Roads). Exempted from taxes for initial 5 years to attract foreign investment.
