Development

Class 10 Economics • Chapter 01

1. What Development Promises?

Values concerning development are subjective. Different people have different developmental goals.

  • Different Goals: Landless labourers want more days of work and better wages. Prosperous farmers want high support prices.
  • Conflicting Goals: A dam may be development for industrialist (electricity) but disaster for tribals living in that area (submersion of land).
KEY TAKEAWAY Thinking about development involves the question of how to achieve these goals with minimal conflict.

2. Income and Other Goals

People want more income. But they also seek:

  • Equal Treatment
  • Freedom
  • Security
  • Respect of others

Example: A job in a far-off place (with high pay) may offer less time for family and less security, so one might prefer a lower paying job closer to home (better quality of life).

3. National Development (Comparing Countries)

How to compare? We use averages.

Average Income (Per Capita Income)

It is the total income of the country divided by its total population.

$$ \text{Per Capita Income} = \frac{\text{Total Income of Country}}{\text{Total Population}} $$

World Bank Criterion (WDR):

  • Rich Countries: PCI > US$ 49,300 per annum (2019).
  • Low Income Countries: PCI < US$ 2500 per annum.
  • India: Lower Middle Income Country (PCI approx US$ 6700).

Limitation of Average: Hides disparities. A country may have high average but very rich few and very poor masses.

4. Income and Other Criteria (HDI)

Money in pocket cannot buy all goods and services (e.g., Pollution free environment, Protection from diseases).
UNDP (United Nations Development Programme) publishes Human Development Report (HDR).

Human Development Index (HDI) Components:

  1. Per Capita Income: Standard of living.
  2. Life Expectancy at Birth: Health status.
  3. Mean Years of Schooling: Educational levels.

India's Rank: 132 (out of 191 countries in 2021-22). Sri Lanka is ahead of India.

Body Mass Index (BMI)

$$ \text{BMI} = \frac{\text{Weight (kg)}}{\text{Height}^2 (\text{m}^2)} $$
  • If BMI < 18.5: Undernourished.
  • If BMI > 25: Overweight.

5. Sustainability of Development

We have not inherited the world from our forefathers, we have borrowed it from our children.

  • Groundwater Overuse: In Punjab/Haryana, water table has fallen drastically. Renewable resource but can be exhausted if used faster than rain replenishment.
  • Exhaustion of Natural Resources: Crude Oil (Non-renewable). We may run out of stock in 50 years.

Solution: Focus on renewable energy (Solar/Wind) and sustainable practices.
