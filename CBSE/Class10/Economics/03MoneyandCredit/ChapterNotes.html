Money and Credit

Class 10 Economics • Chapter 03

1. Money as a Medium of Exchange

Barter System: Direct exchange of goods without money. Requires Double Coincidence of Wants (Both parties must want what other sells).
Money: Eliminates need for double coincidence of wants. Acts as intermediate in exchange process.

2. Modern Forms of Money

Currency

Paper notes and coins. No intrinsic value (unlike Gold/Silver coins).
Why accepted? Authorized by government.
RBI: Issues currency on behalf of Central Govt. No one else can issue it.

Deposits with Banks (Demand Deposits)

People deposit surplus money in bank. Bank pays interest. Money can be withdrawn on demand.
Check: Paper instructing bank to pay specific amount.

3. Loan Activities of Banks

Banks keep only a small portion (15%) of deposits as cash (Cash Reserve Ratio) to pay depositors. Rest is used to extend loans.
Income of Bank: Difference between interest charged on borrowers and interest paid to depositors.

4. Two Different Credit Situations

1. Festive Season (Salim)

Salim takes loan to meet working capital needs for shoe order. Completes order, pays back loan, earns profit. (Credit played positive role).

2. Crop Failure (Swapna)

Swapna takes loan for cultivation. Crop fails. Takes another loan to repay. falls into Debt Trap. (Credit played negative role).

5. Terms of Credit

Every loan agreement has terms:

  • Interest Rate: Specified.
  • Collateral (Security): Asset (land, building, vehicle, deposits) that borrower owns and uses as guarantee.
  • Documentation Required: Papers.
  • Mode of Repayment: Cash/Installments.

6. Formal vs Informal Sector Loans

Formal Sector (Banks, Cooperatives)

  • Supervised by RBI (Reserve Bank of India).
  • Fixed/Low interest rates.
  • RBI monitors if banks lend to small cultivators/small industries, not just rich.

Informal Sector (Moneylenders, Traders, Friends)

  • NO organization supervises them.
  • High interest rates.
  • Unfair means to get money back.
  • Result: Debt trap. Cost of borrowing increases.

Status: Rich households use Formal sector (90%). Poor households use Informal sector (85%).

NEED FOR REFORM Banks need to expand in rural areas so dependence on informal sector reduces. Cheap and affordable credit is crucial for country's development.

7. Self Help Groups (SHGs) for Poor

Typical SHG has 15-20 women members who save regularly (Rs 25-100).

  • Internal Loans: Members take small loans from group at low interest.
  • Bank Linkage: After 1-2 years of regular savings, growing SHG is eligible for bank loan (without collateral).
  • Social Impact: Women become self-reliant. Platform to discuss health, nutrition, domestic violence.
  • Grameen Bank: Success story of Bangladesh (Prof. Muhammad Yunus).
