Sectors of the Indian Economy

Class 10 Economics • Chapter 02

1. Classification by Nature of Activity

1. Primary Sector (Agriculture & Related)

Use of natural resources directly. E.g., Cultivation of cotton/wheat, Dairy, Fishing, Mining.
Why Primary? Because it forms the base for all other products.

2. Secondary Sector (Industrial)

Natural products changed into other forms through manufacturing.
E.g., Cotton fibre to Cloth, Sugarcane to Sugar, Earth to Bricks.

3. Tertiary Sector (Service)

Activities that help in the development of Primary and Secondary sectors. Do not produce goods but offer services.
E.g., Transport, Storage, Banking, Communication, Teachers, Doctors, Software.

2. Comparing the Sectors (GDP)

Gross Domestic Product (GDP): The value of all final goods and services produced within a country during a particular year. (Sum of Primary + Secondary + Tertiary).

Note: We only count FINAL goods (e.g., Biscuit). We don't count intermediate goods (Flour) separately because their value is already included in final good.

3. Rising Importance of Tertiary Sector

In 1973-74, Primary was largest. In 2013-14, Tertiary became largest sector in India.

  • Basic Services: Hospitals, Schools, Police, Courts are responsibility of Govt. Demand increased.
  • Development of Ag/Ind: Transport, trade, storage developed because of growth in primary/secondary.
  • Income Rise: People started demanding eating out, tourism, shopping.
  • ICT Boom: Information and Communication Technology services grew rapidly.
THE PARADOX While Tertiary sector contributes most to GDP, Primary sector (Agriculture) still employs the most people (Disguised Unemployment).

Disguised Unemployment (Underemployment): More people working than required. If you remove few, production won't fall. (Common in agriculture).

4. How to Create Employment?

  • Irrigation: Can create jobs in farm.
  • Infrastructure: Road/Transport/Storage.
  • Credit: Cheap loans for seeds/fertilizers.
  • Industries in semi-rural areas: Cold storage, Honey collection, Dal mills.
  • NREGA 2005 (MGNREGA): Right to Work. 100 days of guaranteed employment in rural areas. If govt fails, it gives unemployment allowance.

5. Division by Condition of Work

Organized Sector

  • Terms of employment are regular and registered by Govt.
  • Follows Factory Act, Minimum Wages Act.
  • Job security, paid leave, PF, medical benefits.

Unorganized Sector

  • Small scattered units outside govt control.
  • Low paid, irregular. No job security.
  • No paid holidays. Can be asked to leave anytime.
  • Need protection (Landless labourers, rag pickers).

6. Division by Ownership

  • Public Sector: Govt owns assets and provides services. Motive: Public Welfare. (Albeit it may earn profit). E.g., Railways, Post Office.
  • Private Sector: Ownership in hands of private individuals. Motive: Profit. E.g., TISCO, RIL.
