Consumer Rights

Class 10 Economics • Chapter 05

1. The Consumer in the Marketplace

Market is where goods and services are exchanged. Consumer is the king, but often exploited.

  • Forms of Exploitation: Adulteration (Mixing cheap things), False weights, Substandard goods, Hoarding/Black marketing.

2. Consumer Movement

Arose out of dissatisfaction of consumers due to unfair trade practices. Rampant food shortages/hoarding in 1960s gave birth to movement.
COPRA 1986: Consumer Protection Act 1986. A major step by Indian Govt.

National Consumers Day: December 24.

3. Rights of Consumers (Under COPRA)

1. Right to Safety

Right to be protected against marketing of goods which are hazardous to life and property.
E.g., Pressure cooker with defective safety valve can be fatal.

2. Right to be Informed

Consumers have right to be informed about particulars of goods (Ingredients, Price, Batch No, Expiry date, MRP).
RTI 2005: Right to Information Act covers govt services too.

3. Right to Choose

Right to choose from a variety of goods at competitive prices. No one can force you to buy a specific brand.

4. Right to Seek Redressal

Right to get compensation against unfair trade practices.
Three Tier Quasi-Judicial Machinery:

  • District Forum: Claims up to Rs 1 Crore.
  • State Commission: Claims 1 Cr to 10 Cr.
  • National Commission: Claims above 10 Cr.

5. Right to Represent

Right to represent in consumer courts.

6. Right to Consumer Education

Right to acquire knowledge and skill to be an informed consumer.

4. Learning to become a Well-Informed Consumer

When we buy, we must check for Quality Standards marks:

  • ISI Mark: Industrial goods (Switches, Electricals).
  • Agmark: Agriculture products (Honey, Oil, Spices).
  • Hallmark: Gold jewelry.
JAGO GRAHAK JAGO (Awake Consumer Awake). A govt campaign to spread awareness.
Responsibilities: Ask for Cash Memo, Check Expiry Date, Be aware of rights.

5. Taking the Consumer Movement Forward

India has been observing 24 Dec as National Consumers Day.
Problems: Process is slow, expensive and time consuming. Lawyer fees are high. Cash memos not often issued (so evidence is weak).
