Subject-Verb Concord (Agreement)

üîë The Fundamentals

Singular Subject = Singular Verb (Verb usually ends in 's', like is, was, has, plays).
Plural Subject = Plural Verb (Verb without 's', like are, were, have, play).
Exception: I (mixes rules) & You (always plural verb).

üö´ The "Uncountable" Trap (Most Repeated!)

These words are ALWAYS Singular. Never adding 's' or using plural verbs:

  • Furniture (Not Furnitures) -> The furniture is old.
  • Information (Not Informations) -> The information is correct.
  • News (Looks plural but is Singular) -> The news is shocking.
  • Advice, Scenery, Hair, Equipment.

The 10 Golden Rules

1. The "And" Rule

Two subjects joined by "and" usually take a Plural verb.

‚úÖ Ram and Shyam are playing.

Exception: If they represent one idea, use Singular.

‚úÖ Bread and butter is a wholesome breakfast.

2. Neither/Nor & Either/Or Rule (Proximity Rule)

The verb agrees with the subject closest to it.

‚úÖ Neither the teacher nor the students were present.

‚úÖ Neither the students nor the teacher was present.

3. Indefinite Pronouns

Words like Everyone, Someone, Anybody, Nobody, Each, Every are always Singular.

‚úÖ Everyone knows the answer. (Not know)

‚úÖ Each of the boys has a prize. (Not have)

4. Collective Nouns

Group words (Team, Class, Jury, Committee) are usually Singular if acting as a unit.

‚úÖ The team is winning.

Exception: If members are disagreeing, use Plural.

‚úÖ The jury were divided in their opinion.

5. Distances, Money, Time

Amounts of money, periods of time, and distances are seen as a single unit = Singular.

‚úÖ Ten kilometers is a long way to walk.

‚úÖ Five hundred rupees is not enough.

6. Names ending in 'S'

Books, countries, or diseases ending in 's' are still Singular.

‚úÖ Mathematics is my favorite subject.

‚úÖ The Arabian Nights is an interesting book.

7. "One of the..." Rule

One of the [Plural Noun] + [Singular Verb].

‚úÖ One of my friends is a doctor. (One is the subject, not friends).

8. "Along with", "As well as"

The verb agrees with the First Subject. Ignore the middle part.

‚úÖ The politician, along with his followers, was arrested.

9. "Here" and "There"

The subject comes after the verb.

‚úÖ There are many apples in the basket.

‚úÖ Here is the report.

10. "A number of" vs "The number of"

A number of = Plural (Many).

The number of = Singular (A specific number).

‚úÖ A number of students are absent.

‚úÖ The number of students is 50.

üìù Practice Questions

  • Neither of the two candidates ___________ (has/have) applied.
    Answer: has (Neither is singular).
  • The quality of the mangoes ___________ (was/were) good.
    Answer: was (Subject is Quality, not mangoes).
  • Time and tide ___________ (wait/waits) for none.
    Answer: wait (Compound subject, Plural idea in modern grammar, though arguably singular in proverbs. CBSE accepts Plural).

üî• Teacher's Secret: The "Tricky Three"

These 3 rules account for 80% of Board Exam errors!

  • 1. The "One of" Trap: Always use a Plural Noun but a Singular Verb.
    One of the boys is... (Not are).
  • 2. The "Long Distance" Trap: Don't get distracted by words in the middle.
    The quality (of the mangoes) is good. (Ignore 'of the mangoes').
  • 3. The "As well as" Trap: Ignore the second person!
    The captain (as well as the players) was present.

üéì Board Exam Corner (2024-2023)

Q1 (2023): Many a student was present at the award ceremony. (Corrected from 'were')
Rule: "Many a" takes a Singular Verb.

Q2 (2024): The study examines the association between exposure to sunlight and vitamin D. (Corrected from 'among')
Rule: "Between" for two distinct things/groups.
