Chapter Overview

This chapter covers the fundamental concepts of Oscillations. Ensure you understand the definitions and derivations thoroughly.

13.1 Periodic & Oscillatory Motions

  • Periodic Motion: Motion that repeats itself at regular intervals of time.
  • Oscillatory Motion: To and fro motion about a mean position. (Every oscillatory motion is periodic, but not vice versa).
  • Period (T): Time to complete one oscillation.
  • Frequency ($\nu$): Number of oscillations per second. $\nu = 1/T$. Unit: Hertz (Hz).

13.2 Simple Harmonic Motion (SHM)

Definition: Motion where restoring force is directly proportional to displacement from mean position and acts opposite to it.

$$ F = -k x $$

Displacement Equation:

$$ x(t) = A \cos(\omega t + \phi) $$
  • $A$: Amplitude (Max displacement).
  • $\omega$: Angular Frequency ($2\pi/T$).
  • $\phi$: Phase Constant (depends on initial conditions).

13.3 Velocity & Acceleration in SHM

Figure 13.1: Phase relationship between Displacement, Velocity, and Acceleration

  • Velocity: $v = \frac{dx}{dt} = -\omega A \sin(\omega t + \phi)$. Max $v_{max} = \omega A$.
  • Acceleration: $a = \frac{dv}{dt} = -\omega^2 A \cos(\omega t + \phi) = -\omega^2 x$. Max $a_{max} = \omega^2 A$.
  • Phase Difference: $v$ leads $x$ by $\pi/2$. $a$ leads $x$ by $\pi$.
Practice Problem 1

Q: A particle executes SHM with amplitude $5 \text{ cm}$ and period $0.2 \pi \text{ s}$. Find velocity at mean position and acceleration at extreme position.

Ans: $A = 0.05 \text{ m}$. $\omega = \frac{2\pi}{T} = \frac{2\pi}{0.2\pi} = 10 \text{ rad/s}$.
$v_{max}$ (at mean) = $\omega A = 10 \times 0.05 = \mathbf{0.5 \text{ m/s}}$.
$a_{max}$ (at extreme) = $\omega^2 A = 100 \times 0.05 = \mathbf{5 \text{ m/s}^2}$.

13.4 Force Law & Springs

Restoring Force: $F(t) = -k x(t)$.

From Newton's Law: $F = ma = -m \omega^2 x$.

Comparing: $k = m \omega^2 \Rightarrow \omega = \sqrt{k/m}$.

Time Period of Spring-Mass: $T = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{m}{k}}$.

Spring Combinations:

  • Series: $\frac{1}{k_{eq}} = \frac{1}{k_1} + \frac{1}{k_2}$.
  • Parallel: $k_{eq} = k_1 + k_2$.

13.5 Energy in SHM

Figure 13.2: Variation of Kinetic and Potential Energy with Displacement

  • Potential Energy ($U$): $\frac{1}{2} k x^2 = \frac{1}{2} m \omega^2 A^2 \cos^2(\omega t + \phi)$.
  • Kinetic Energy ($K$): $\frac{1}{2} m v^2 = \frac{1}{2} m \omega^2 A^2 \sin^2(\omega t + \phi)$.
  • Total Energy ($E$): $K + U = \frac{1}{2} m \omega^2 A^2 (\sin^2 \theta + \cos^2 \theta) = \frac{1}{2} k A^2$.

Total mechanical energy is conserved in SHM.

Practice Problem 2

Q: At what displacement from the mean position is the kinetic energy equal to potential energy?

Ans: Set $K = U$.
$\frac{1}{2} k (A^2 - x^2) = \frac{1}{2} k x^2$.
$A^2 - x^2 = x^2 \Rightarrow 2x^2 = A^2 \Rightarrow x = \pm \frac{A}{\sqrt{2}}$.
$x \approx \pm 0.707 A$.

13.6 The Simple Pendulum

Figure 13.3: Forces acting on a Simple Pendulum

Restoring Force: $F = -mg \sin \theta$.

For small $\theta$, $\sin \theta \approx \theta = x/L$.

$F \approx -mg(x/L)$. Comparing with $F = -kx$, we get $k = mg/L$.

$$ T = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{m}{k}} = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}} $$

Time period is independent of mass $m$.

13.7 Damped & Forced Oscillations

  • Damped: Amplitude decreases with time due to dissipative forces (friction/drag). $x(t) = A e^{-bt/2m} \cos(\omega' t + \phi)$.
  • Forced: Body oscillates under external periodic force.
  • Resonance: When driving frequency equals natural frequency ($\omega_d = \omega_n$), amplitude becomes maximum.

Practice Problem 3

Q: A simple pendulum has a time period of 2s. If its length is increased 4 times, find the new time period.

Ans: $T \propto \sqrt{L}$.
$\frac{T_2}{T_1} = \sqrt{\frac{L_2}{L_1}} = \sqrt{\frac{4L}{L}} = 2$.
$T_2 = 2 T_1 = 2(2) = \mathbf{4 \text{ s}}$.
