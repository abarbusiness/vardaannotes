Chapter Overview

This chapter covers the fundamental concepts of Waves. Ensure you understand the definitions and derivations thoroughly.

14.1 Introduction to Waves

  • Wave Motion: Disturbance that propagates through a medium, transferring energy and momentum without transport of matter.
  • Transverse Wave: Particles oscillate perpendicular to direction of wave propagation (e.g., Light, String waves).
  • Longitudinal Wave: Particles oscillate parallel to direction of wave propagation (e.g., Sound).

14.3 Progressive Wave Equation

Displacement Relation: For a plane progressive harmonic wave traveling in +x direction:

$$ y(x, t) = A \sin(kx - \omega t + \phi) $$
  • $y$: Displacement of particle at position $x$ at time $t$.
  • $A$: Amplitude.
  • $\omega$: Angular Frequency ($2\pi\nu$).
  • $k$: Wave Number ($2\pi/\lambda$).
  • $\phi$: Initial Phase.

Phase Difference ($\Delta \phi$): $\Delta \phi = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda} \Delta x = k \Delta x$.

14.4 Speed of a Traveling Wave

Wave Velocity ($v$): $v = \frac{\omega}{k} = \nu \lambda$.

  • Speed on Stretched String: $v = \sqrt{\frac{T}{\mu}}$ (where $\mu$ is mass per unit length).
  • Speed of Sound (Newton-Laplace): $v = \sqrt{\frac{\gamma P}{\rho}}$ (where $\gamma$ is adiabatic ratio).
Practice Problem 1

Q: A wave is represented by $y = 0.05 \sin(80x - 3t)$ (SI units). Find amplitude, wavelength, frequency, and velocity.

Ans: Compare with $y = A \sin(kx - \omega t)$.
$A = \mathbf{0.05 \text{ m}}$.
$k = 80 \Rightarrow \frac{2\pi}{\lambda} = 80 \Rightarrow \lambda = \frac{\pi}{40} \approx \mathbf{0.078 \text{ m}}$.
$\omega = 3 \Rightarrow 2\pi\nu = 3 \Rightarrow \nu = \frac{3}{2\pi} \approx \mathbf{0.48 \text{ Hz}}$.
$v = \frac{\omega}{k} = \frac{3}{80} = \mathbf{0.0375 \text{ m/s}}$.

14.5 Principle of Superposition

Statement: When two or more waves overlap, the resultant displacement at any point is the vector sum of individual displacements.

$$ y = y_1 + y_2 $$

14.6 Reflection & Standing Waves

Reflection from Rigid Boundary: Phase change of $\pi$ ($180^\circ$). Pulse inverts.

Reflection from Open Boundary: No phase change. Pulse does not invert.

Standing (Stationary) Waves: Formed by superposition of two identical waves traveling in opposite directions.

$y_1 = A \sin(kx - \omega t)$, $y_2 = A \sin(kx + \omega t)$.

$y = y_1 + y_2 = 2A \sin kx \cos \omega t$.

  • Nodes: Points of zero amplitude ($\sin kx = 0 \Rightarrow x = n\lambda/2$).
  • Antinodes: Points of max amplitude ($\sin kx = \pm 1 \Rightarrow x = (n + \frac{1}{2})\lambda/2$).

Practice Problem 2 (Harmonics)

Q: A pipe closed at one end is 20 cm long. Find fundamental frequency if speed of sound is 340 m/s.

Ans: Closed Pipe Fundamental: $L = \lambda/4 \Rightarrow \lambda = 4L$.
$\nu = \frac{v}{\lambda} = \frac{v}{4L}$.
$\nu = \frac{340}{4 \times 0.2} = \frac{340}{0.8} = \frac{3400}{8} = \mathbf{425 \text{ Hz}}$.

14.7 Beats

Phenomenon: Periodic variation in intensity of sound when two waves of slightly different frequencies superimpose.

$$ \nu_{beat} = |\nu_1 - \nu_2| $$

Used in tuning musical instruments.

14.8 Doppler Effect

Definition: Apparent change in frequency of wave due to relative motion between source and observer.

$$ \nu' = \nu \left( \frac{v \pm v_o}{v \mp v_s} \right) $$
  • $v$: Speed of sound (~340 m/s).
  • $v_o$: Speed of Observer (+ if moving towards source).
  • $v_s$: Speed of Source (- if moving towards observer).

Practice Problem 3 (Doppler)

Q: A train whistling at 400 Hz approaches a station at 10 m/s. What is the frequency heard by a stationary observer on the platform? ($v = 340$ m/s).

Ans: Observer stationary ($v_o = 0$). Source approaching ($v_s$ is -ve in denominator).
$\nu' = \nu \left( \frac{v}{v - v_s} \right)$.
$\nu' = 400 \left( \frac{340}{340 - 10} \right) = 400 \left( \frac{340}{330} \right)$.
$\nu' = 400 \times 1.03 = \mathbf{412 \text{ Hz}}$. (Frequency increases).
