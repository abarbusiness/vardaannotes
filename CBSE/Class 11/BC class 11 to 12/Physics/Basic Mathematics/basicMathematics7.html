VECTORS FINAL PART

WHY THIS MATTERS

The real world is 3-dimensional. A force isn't just a number — it has magnitude AND direction. A vector is the mathematical object that captures both. Velocity, force, momentum — every important quantity in physics is a vector. Understanding vectors means understanding physics itself.

§1. Scalars vs. Vectors

Scalar Quantities: Only have magnitude. Examples: Mass, Time, Energy, Distance, Speed.
Vector Quantities: Have both magnitude and direction. Examples: Displacement, Velocity, Force, Momentum.
NOTATION

Vectors are written with an arrow: $\vec{A}$ or in bold: $\mathbf{A}$. Magnitude of $\vec{A}$ is written as $|\vec{A}|$ or simply $A$.

§2. Types of Vectors

Unit Vector ($\hat{A}$): Magnitude exactly 1. $\hat{A} = \vec{A}/|\vec{A}|$. Standard: $\hat{i}, \hat{j}, \hat{k}$.
Null Vector: Magnitude = 0. Direction undefined.
Equal Vectors: Same magnitude AND direction.
Negative Vector: Same magnitude, opposite direction.

§3. Vector Addition

Triangle Law & Parallelogram Law

If two vectors $\vec{A}$ and $\vec{B}$ act at an angle $\theta$, their resultant $\vec{R} = \vec{A} + \vec{B}$ is:

MAGNITUDE & DIRECTION
$$|\vec{R}| = \sqrt{A^2 + B^2 + 2AB\cos\theta}$$ $$\tan\alpha = \frac{B\sin\theta}{A + B\cos\theta}$$

§4. Resolution of a Vector (Components)

RESOLUTION

$A_x = A\cos\theta \qquad A_y = A\sin\theta$

$\vec{A} = A_x\hat{i} + A_y\hat{j} = (A\cos\theta)\hat{i} + (A\sin\theta)\hat{j}$

Example: Force of 50 N acts at 37° above horizontal. Find components.

$F_x = 50\cos 37° = 50(0.8) = \mathbf{40\text{ N}}$

$F_y = 50\sin 37° = 50(0.6) = \mathbf{30\text{ N}}$

§5. Dot Product & Cross Product

Dot Product (Scalar):
$\vec{A}\cdot\vec{B} = AB\cos\theta$
Physics: Work, Power, Flux.
Cross Product (Vector):
$|\vec{A}\times\vec{B}| = AB\sin\theta$
Physics: Torque, Angular Momentum.

Practice Questions

DRILL 1 — BASIC VECTORS
  • 1. Find the magnitude of $\vec{A} = 3\hat{i} - 4\hat{j}$.
  • 2. Two forces of 6 N and 8 N act at 90°. Find the resultant.
  • 3. Find the unit vector along $\vec{B} = 6\hat{i} + 2\hat{j} + 3\hat{k}$.
DRILL 2 — PRODUCTS
  • 4. Find $\vec{A}\cdot\vec{B}$ if $\vec{A}=2\hat{i}+3\hat{j}$ and $\vec{B}=\hat{i}-2\hat{j}$.
  • 5. Find work done by $\vec{F}=5\hat{i}+3\hat{j}$ N in displacement $\vec{d}=2\hat{i}+4\hat{j}$ m.
  • 6. The dot product of two vectors is zero. What is the angle between them?
