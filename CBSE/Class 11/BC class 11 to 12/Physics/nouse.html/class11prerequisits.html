EXHAUSTIVE CLASS 11 PREREQUISITES FOR CLASS 12 JEE

This is the definitive, exhaustive manual designed for students with a weak Class 11 base. It contains the precise micro-topics, formulas, and bridging concepts required to master the Class 12 JEE syllabus. If it is not in this document, you do not need it to start Class 12.

1. PHYSICS: The Mechanics & Mathematical Bridge

Class 12 Physics relies heavily on invisible fields (Electric, Magnetic). Because you cannot see them, you must rely entirely on the mathematics of Vectors, Kinematics, and NLM to predict particle behavior.

CRITICAL MATH

1.1 Basic Mathematics & Approximations

Physics derivations in Class 12 often require you to approximate complex formulas when dealing with tiny distances (like dipoles).

  • Binomial Approximation: If $x \ll 1$ (x is very small compared to 1), then: $$ (1 \pm x)^n \approx 1 \pm nx $$ Application: Finding the electric field of a dipole at a large distance, finding the variation of 'g' with height, and deriving the magnetic field of a short bar magnet.
  • Small Angle Approximations: If $\theta$ is very small (in radians): $$ \sin\theta \approx \theta \quad ; \quad \tan\theta \approx \theta \quad ; \quad \cos\theta \approx 1 $$ Application: Used extensively in Ray Optics (paraxial rays) and Wave Optics (Young's Double Slit).

1.2 Vectors (The absolute backbone)

  • Resolution in 2D and 3D: $\vec{A} = A_x\hat{i} + A_y\hat{j} + A_z\hat{k}$. Finding magnitude: $|\vec{A}| = \sqrt{A_x^2 + A_y^2 + A_z^2}$.
    Application: Finding net force/field from multiple charges in a 3D coordinate system.
  • Dot Product (Scalar): $\vec{A} \cdot \vec{B} = |\vec{A}||\vec{B}| \cos\theta = A_xB_x + A_yB_y + A_zB_z$.
    Application: Electric Flux $\Phi = \vec{E} \cdot \vec{A}$, Work $W = \vec{F} \cdot \vec{s}$, Potential Energy of Dipole $U = -\vec{p} \cdot \vec{E}$.
  • Cross Product (Vector): $\vec{A} \times \vec{B} = |\vec{A}||\vec{B}| \sin\theta \ \hat{n}$. Calculate using the Determinant method.
    Application: Magnetic Force $\vec{F} = q(\vec{v} \times \vec{B})$, Torque $\vec{\tau} = \vec{p} \times \vec{E}$. You must know the Right-Hand Rule.

1.3 Kinematics & Projectile Motion

  • 1D Motion Equations: $v = u + at$, $s = ut + \frac{1}{2}at^2$, $v^2 = u^2 + 2as$.
  • Projectile Motion: Trajectory equation $y = x \tan\theta - \frac{gx^2}{2u^2\cos^2\theta}$. Time of flight $T = \frac{2u\sin\theta}{g}$.
    Application: If an electron enters a uniform electric field perpendicularly, it traces a parabolic path exactly like a projectile under gravity.

1.4 Newton's Laws & Friction

  • FBDs and Equilibrium: $\sum F_x = 0$ and $\sum F_y = 0$.
  • Friction: $f_s \le \mu_s N$ (Static) and $f_k = \mu_k N$ (Kinetic).
    Application: Finding the minimum electric field required to move a charged block resting on a rough surface.

1.5 Work, Energy, Power & Center of Mass

  • Work-Energy Theorem: $W_{\text{net}} = \Delta K$.
  • Conservation of Energy: $K_i + U_i = K_f + U_f$.
  • Conservation of Linear Momentum: If external force is zero, $\vec{P}_{\text{initial}} = \vec{P}_{\text{final}}$. $$ m_1\vec{u_1} + m_2\vec{u_2} = m_1\vec{v_1} + m_2\vec{v_2} $$
    Application: Crucial for Modern Physics. When a photon hits an atom, or a radioactive nucleus decays, momentum is perfectly conserved. You must know elastic vs inelastic collisions.

1.6 Rotational Motion & Gravitation

  • Torque ($\vec{\tau}$): $\vec{\tau} = \vec{r} \times \vec{F}$.
  • Angular Momentum ($L$): $L = mvr$. Conserved when net torque is zero.
    Application: Bohr's Model of the Atom (Class 12 Modern Physics) relies entirely on $mvr = \frac{nh}{2\pi}$.
  • Gravitation: Recognize the exact analogy between $F_g = \frac{G m_1 m_2}{r^2}$ and $F_e = \frac{k q_1 q_2}{r^2}$.

2. CHEMISTRY: The Foundational Trinity & Beyond

Class 12 Chemistry combines Physical, Inorganic, and Organic concepts deeply. Here are the precise tools you need.

Crucial for 12th

2.1 Atomic Structure (NEW REQUIREMENT)

You cannot study Class 12 Modern Physics or d & f block elements without this chapter.

  • Bohr's Model: Memorize the formulas for radius, velocity, and energy of the $n^{th}$ orbit: $$ r_n = 0.529 \frac{n^2}{Z} \text{ \AA} \quad ; \quad v_n = 2.18 \times 10^6 \frac{Z}{n} \text{ m/s} $$ $$ E_n = -13.6 \frac{Z^2}{n^2} \text{ eV} $$
  • Quantum Numbers: Principal ($n$), Azimuthal ($l$), Magnetic ($m_l$), Spin ($m_s$).
  • Electronic Configuration: Aufbau principle, Hund's Rule of Maximum Multiplicity, Pauli Exclusion Principle. You must know how to write configurations for transition metals like $Cu$ ($[Ar] 4s^1 3d^{10}$) and $Cr$.

2.2 States of Matter & Gaseous State

  • Ideal Gas Equation: $PV = nRT$.
  • Dalton's Law of Partial Pressure: $P_{\text{total}} = P_1 + P_2 + P_3 \dots$ where $P_1 = x_1 P_{\text{total}}$ ($x_1$ is mole fraction).
    Application: Required for Class 12 Solutions chapter (Raoult's Law and Henry's Law depend on partial pressures) and Chemical Kinetics for gaseous reactions.

2.3 Mole Concept & Thermodynamics

  • Concentration Terms: Molarity ($M$), Molality ($m$), Mole Fraction ($x$). Required for Solutions.
  • Gibbs Free Energy: $\Delta G = \Delta H - T\Delta S$.
    Application: In Electrochemistry, $\Delta G = -nFE_{\text{cell}}$. If $E_{\text{cell}}$ is positive, $\Delta G$ is negative, so the battery works spontaneously.

2.4 Chemical Bonding

  • VSEPR & Hybridization: Finding shape using Steric Number $= \frac{1}{2} (V+M-C+A)$. Essential for $p$-block structures.
  • Molecular Orbital Theory (MOT): Understand Paramagnetism (presence of unpaired electrons) and Diamagnetism (all paired).
    Application: Required in Class 12 Coordination Compounds (Crystal Field Theory depends on these magnetic concepts).
Make or Break

2.5 General Organic Chemistry (GOC) & Isomerism

Class 12 Organic Chemistry is just GOC applied to functional groups. Study these fiercely:

  • Electronic Effects: Inductive Effect ($+I, -I$), Resonance ($+R, -R$), and Hyperconjugation. Know the stability order of Carbocations ($3^{\circ} > 2^{\circ} > 1^{\circ}$) and Radicals.
  • Isomerism (Optical Isomerism is CRITICAL):
    • Chiral Carbon: A carbon attached to 4 different groups.
    • Enantiomers & Diastereomers: Non-superimposable mirror images.
    • Retention vs Inversion:
      Why? The entire Class 12 chapter on Haloalkanes asks you to determine if an $SN_1$ or $SN_2$ reaction causes Walden Inversion or Racemization. You must understand 3D stereochemistry!

3. MATHEMATICS: The Calculus & Probability Bridge

Class 12 Maths introduces Integration, Differential Equations, and Probability. Your 11th-grade Algebra and Trigonometry are your only weapons to solve them.

CRITICAL FOR PROBABILITY

3.1 Permutations & Combinations (P&C)

Class 12 Probability relies 100% on Class 11 P&C. Without this, you will score zero in Probability.

  • Fundamental Principle of Counting: Multiplication (AND) vs Addition (OR) rules.
  • Factorials & Combinations: $$ {}^nC_r = \frac{n!}{r!(n-r)!} $$ You must know how to select $r$ objects from $n$ distinct objects.
  • Permutations: ${}^nP_r = \frac{n!}{(n-r)!}$. Arranging items in a row.

3.2 Algebra Basics (Series & Binomial)

  • Infinite Geometric Progression (GP): $$ S_{\infty} = \frac{a}{1 - r} \quad (\text{where } |r| < 1) $$ Application: Used in Physics to find the equivalent resistance or capacitance of an infinite ladder network.
  • Binomial Theorem: Know the general term $T_{r+1} = {}^nC_r x^{n-r} y^r$.
  • Wavy Curve Method: Essential for finding Domains and Ranges.

3.3 Functions & Trigonometry

  • Special Functions: Modulus $|x|$, Greatest Integer $[x]$, Fractional Part $\{x\}$, Signum $\text{sgn}(x)$. You must know where these functions "break" (become discontinuous).
  • Trigonometric Identities (MEMORIZE):
    • $\sin(2x) = 2\sin x\cos x \ ; \ \cos(2x) = 2\cos^2x - 1 = 1 - 2\sin^2x$
    • Rearrangements for Integration: $\sin^2x = \frac{1-\cos(2x)}{2}$ and $\cos^2x = \frac{1+\cos(2x)}{2}$.
    • $\sin(3x) = 3\sin x - 4\sin^3x \ ; \ \cos(3x) = 4\cos^3x - 3\cos x$
  • General Solutions: If $\sin\theta = \sin\alpha$, then $\theta = n\pi + (-1)^n\alpha$. Used in finding integration bounds.

3.4 Coordinate Geometry

  • Straight Lines: $y - y_1 = m(x - x_1)$. Slopes of perpendicular lines $m_1 m_2 = -1$. Used in Class 12 AOD (Tangents and Normals).
  • Conic Sections: Standard formulas for Parabola ($y^2=4ax, x^2=4ay$), Ellipse, and Circle. Used heavily in the Class 12 Area Under Curve (Integration) chapter to sketch graphs.

3.5 Limits, Derivatives & Basic Integration

  • Limits: Standard limits like $\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin x}{x} = 1$. L'Hopital's rule. Used to prove Continuity.
  • Differentiation: Chain Rule, Product Rule ($u'v + uv'$), Quotient Rule ($\frac{u'v - uv'}{v^2}$).
  • Basic Integration (Reverse Differentiation): $$ \int x^n dx = \frac{x^{n+1}}{n+1} + C \quad ; \quad \int \sin x dx = -\cos x + C $$ $$ \int \cos x dx = \sin x + C \quad ; \quad \int \frac{1}{x} dx = \ln|x| + C $$ Application: Without these basic formulas, you cannot even begin the Class 12 Integration chapter.
