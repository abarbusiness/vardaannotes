Thermodynamics Comprehensive Practice Set
Student Name: ____________________________________ Class: 11 Subject: Physics
Section A: First Law of Thermodynamics & Internal Energy
1.
A thermodynamic system is supplied with $100 \text{ J}$ of heat, and it performs $40 \text{ J}$ of work on its surroundings. Calculate the change in the internal energy of the system.
2.
In a certain process, $500 \text{ J}$ of heat is removed from a system, and $200 \text{ J}$ of work is done on the system. Determine the change in its internal energy.
3.
An ideal gas is compressed at a constant pressure of $2 \times 10^5 \text{ N/m}^2$ from a volume of $5 \text{ L}$ to $2 \text{ L}$. If $400 \text{ J}$ of heat is extracted from the gas during the process, what is the change in its internal energy?
4.
The internal energy of a monoatomic ideal gas is given by $U = 1.5 PV$. If the gas expands from $V_0$ to $2V_0$ at a constant pressure $P_0$, find the heat supplied to the gas.
5.
A system undergoes a process where its internal energy decreases by $300 \text{ J}$ while it performs $150 \text{ J}$ of work. Calculate the amount of heat exchanged and specify whether it is absorbed or released.
6.
Two moles of an ideal monoatomic gas are heated at constant volume from $27^\circ\text{C}$ to $127^\circ\text{C}$. Calculate the heat added, work done, and change in internal energy. (Take $R = 8.31 \text{ J/mol K}$)
7.
A rigid container holds $14 \text{ g}$ of nitrogen gas at $27^\circ\text{C}$. How much heat must be added to double the rms speed of its molecules?
8.
If $\Delta Q = 0$ for a process, and the system expands, what happens to the internal energy and temperature of the system? Prove mathematically using the First Law.
9.
An ideal gas occupies $2 \text{ L}$ at $1 \text{ atm}$ pressure. It is heated at constant volume until its pressure reaches $3 \text{ atm}$. Then it is allowed to expand isothermally until its pressure is $1 \text{ atm}$. Find the total heat supplied.
10.
The volume of a gas expands by $0.25 \text{ m}^3$ at a constant pressure of $10^3 \text{ N/m}^2$. The heat supplied is $500 \text{ J}$. Find the change in internal energy.
11.
A gas is heated such that its pressure and volume both double. If the initial internal energy was $U_0$, what is the final internal energy in terms of $U_0$ for an ideal diatomic gas?
12.
One mole of an ideal gas undergoes a process where $P = \alpha V$. Find the molar heat capacity of the gas for this process in terms of $R$ and $\gamma$.
Section B: Isothermal, Adiabatic, and Polytropic Processes
13.
Calculate the work done when $1 \text{ mole}$ of an ideal gas expands isothermally and reversibly from a volume of $2 \text{ L}$ to $20 \text{ L}$ at $300 \text{ K}$.
14.
An ideal gas ($\gamma = 1.4$) is compressed adiabatically from a volume of $16 \text{ L}$ to $2 \text{ L}$. If the initial pressure is $1 \text{ atm}$, calculate the final pressure.
15.
During an adiabatic process, the pressure of a gas is found to be proportional to the cube of its absolute temperature. Determine the ratio $C_p/C_v$ for the gas.
16.
Two moles of a diatomic gas at $300 \text{ K}$ are compressed adiabatically to one-fourth of their original volume. Calculate the work done on the gas.
17.
An ideal gas at $27^\circ\text{C}$ is suddenly compressed to $1/8^{\text{th}}$ of its original volume. Find the final temperature if $\gamma = 5/3$.
18.
Prove that the slope of an adiabatic curve passing through a point on a PV diagram is $\gamma$ times the slope of the isothermal curve passing through the same point.
19.
A gas undergoes a polytropic process defined by $PV^2 = \text{constant}$. If the initial temperature is $T_1$ and volume $V_1$, find the work done when the volume doubles.
20.
Determine the molar specific heat capacity for a gas undergoing the process $PT = \text{constant}$.
21.
One mole of $O_2$ gas expands isothermally at $300 \text{ K}$ doing $2000 \text{ J}$ of work. Find the ratio of the final volume to the initial volume.
22.
An ideal gas expands against a constant external pressure of $2 \text{ atm}$ from $5 \text{ L}$ to $15 \text{ L}$. Calculate the work done in Joules.
23.
A balloon containing $1 \text{ mole}$ of an ideal gas expands isothermally and reversibly from a pressure of $10 \text{ atm}$ to $1 \text{ atm}$ at $298 \text{ K}$. Calculate $q, w, \Delta U,$ and $\Delta H$.
24.
An ideal gas initially at $P_0, V_0$ undergoes a process where $P = P_0 - \alpha V$. Find the maximum temperature attained by the gas during this process.
Section C: Specific Heat, Gas Mixtures & Degrees of Freedom
25.
Calculate the ratio $C_p/C_v$ for a gaseous mixture consisting of $2 \text{ moles}$ of a monoatomic gas and $3 \text{ moles}$ of a diatomic gas.
26.
The molar specific heat at constant pressure of an ideal gas is $7R/2$. Find the ratio of specific heats at constant pressure to that at constant volume.
27.
A container is divided into two equal compartments. One contains $3 \text{ moles}$ of $\mathrm{He}$ at $T_1$ and the other contains $2 \text{ moles}$ of $\mathrm{N_2}$ at $T_2$. If the partition is removed, find the final equilibrium temperature.
28.
For a gas, the difference between the two specific heats is $4150 \text{ J/kg K}$. What is the specific heat of the gas at constant volume if the ratio of specific heats is $1.4$?
29.
Calculate the internal energy of $10 \text{ g}$ of $\mathrm{H_2}$ gas at $27^\circ\text{C}$. (Assume $\mathrm{H_2}$ is an ideal diatomic gas).
30.
Find the change in internal energy of $2 \text{ moles}$ of a polyatomic gas (having 6 degrees of freedom) if its temperature is raised by $50 \text{ K}$.
31.
A mixture of $n_1$ moles of monoatomic gas and $n_2$ moles of diatomic gas has $\gamma = 1.5$. Find the ratio $n_1/n_2$.
32.
Show that for an ideal gas, $C_p - C_v = R$. State the assumptions made.
33.
The temperature of $100 \text{ g}$ of water is raised from $20^\circ\text{C}$ to $50^\circ\text{C}$. Calculate the change in its internal energy. (Given specific heat of water $= 4184 \text{ J/kg K}$)
34.
What is the specific heat of a gas undergoing an isothermal process? What is it for an adiabatic process? Justify your answers.
35.
When $100 \text{ J}$ of heat is supplied to a gas at constant pressure, it expands and does $30 \text{ J}$ of work. Determine the atomicity of the gas.
36.
Calculate the root mean square velocity of oxygen molecules at $27^\circ\text{C}$.
Section D: PV Diagrams and Cyclic Processes
37.
A thermodynamic system undergoes a cyclic process ABCA. The path AB is isobaric, BC is isochoric, and CA is a straight line on the PV diagram. If $P_A = P_B = 2 \text{ atm}, V_A = 1 \text{ L}, V_B = 3 \text{ L}, P_C = 1 \text{ atm}$, calculate the net work done in the cycle.
38.
In a cyclic process plotted as a circle on a PV diagram with radius $r$, write the expression for the work done in one complete cycle.
39.
An ideal gas undergoes a cyclic process consisting of two isochores and two isobars. The volumes are $V_1$ and $V_2$, and pressures are $P_1$ and $P_2$. Find the efficiency of this cycle if the gas is monoatomic.
40.
A PV diagram shows a rectangle ABCD. The state A is $(P_0, V_0)$, B is $(P_0, 2V_0)$, C is $(2P_0, 2V_0)$, and D is $(2P_0, V_0)$. Find the heat absorbed during the path AB if the gas is diatomic.
41.
For the same rectangle ABCD in Q40, calculate the total work done by the gas in the complete cycle A $\rightarrow$ B $\rightarrow$ C $\rightarrow$ D $\rightarrow$ A.
42.
Draw a PV diagram for a process where a gas expands isothermally, then is compressed isobarically to its initial volume, and finally heated isochorically to its initial pressure. Is the net work done positive or negative?
43.
An ideal gas goes from state $i$ to $f$ via three different paths: 1, 2, and 3. In which path is the change in internal energy maximum? Explain your reasoning.
44.
A gas is taken through a cyclic process ABC. The internal energy at A is $20 \text{ J}$. Heat added in path AB is $50 \text{ J}$ and work done is $20 \text{ J}$. Find the internal energy at state B.
45.
In the cycle from Q44, if the work done in path BC is $-30 \text{ J}$ and no heat is exchanged, what is the internal energy at state C?
46.
Consider a PV indicator diagram consisting of a semi-circle of diameter from $V_1 = 2 \text{ L}$ to $V_2 = 6 \text{ L}$. Find the net work done if the pressure axis scales such that the maximum pressure corresponds to the radius.
47.
In a cyclic process, $\Sigma \Delta Q = 40 \text{ J}$. If the total work done by the system is $W$, find the value of $W$.
48.
Convert a given clockwise circular PV cycle into a PT diagram qualitatively.
Section E: Second Law, Heat Engines, Refrigerators & Carnot Cycle
49.
A Carnot engine operating between temperatures $T_1$ and $T_2$ has an efficiency of $1/6$. When the sink temperature is reduced by $62 \text{ K}$, its efficiency doubles. Find the temperatures $T_1$ and $T_2$.
50.
A refrigerator has a coefficient of performance of 5. If the temperature inside the freezer is $-20^\circ\text{C}$, what is the temperature of the room to which it rejects heat?
51.
A heat engine absorbs $800 \text{ J}$ of heat from a hot reservoir at $500 \text{ K}$ and rejects $600 \text{ J}$ of heat to a cold reservoir at $300 \text{ K}$. Calculate the efficiency of the engine. Is this engine a reversible Carnot engine?
52.
Two Carnot engines A and B are operated in series. Engine A receives heat from a reservoir at $600 \text{ K}$ and rejects heat to a reservoir at temperature $T$. Engine B receives heat rejected by A and rejects it to a sink at $300 \text{ K}$. Find $T$ if the work output of both engines is equal.
53.
For the two Carnot engines in Q52, find the intermediate temperature $T$ if their efficiencies are equal.
54.
A Carnot refrigerator extracts $1000 \text{ J}$ of heat from a freezer at $-10^\circ\text{C}$ and exhausts it to a room at $25^\circ\text{C}$. How much work is required per cycle?
55.
An ideal gas heat engine operates in a Carnot cycle between $227^\circ\text{C}$ and $127^\circ\text{C}$. It absorbs $6 \times 10^4 \text{ J}$ at the higher temperature. Calculate the amount of heat converted into work.
56.
A heat pump is used to heat a building. The outside temperature is $-5^\circ\text{C}$ and the desired indoor temperature is $22^\circ\text{C}$. If the heat pump operates as an ideal Carnot heat pump, calculate its coefficient of performance.
57.
State the Kelvin-Planck and Clausius statements of the Second Law of Thermodynamics and briefly explain how they are equivalent.
58.
Calculate the change in entropy when $10 \text{ g}$ of ice at $0^\circ\text{C}$ is converted into water at the same temperature. (Latent heat of fusion $L = 336 \text{ J/g}$).
59.
An engine working between $300^\circ\text{C}$ and $50^\circ\text{C}$ takes $2000 \text{ J}$ of heat from the source. What is the maximum theoretical work it can do?
60.
Why is it impossible to construct an engine that is 100% efficient? Explain using the concept of entropy and the Carnot cycle.
