# Basic Mathematics for Physics (Class 11) - Complete Syllabus

In order to study physics effectively in Class 11 and 12, a student must have a strong grip on Basic Mathematics. This acts as the fundamental tool for solving problems, deriving formulas, and understanding physical quantities. 

Below is the complete, detailed breakdown of every mathematical concept required, divided into logical parts.

---

## Part 1: Basic Algebra & Arithmetic
This section covers foundational algebraic manipulations used constantly in physics calculations.
*   **Fractions, Indices, and Surds:** Basic calculation rules, laws of exponents.
*   **Quadratic Equations:** Standard form ($ax^2 + bx + c = 0$), quadratic formula (Sridharacharya Formula), finding roots, properties of roots (sum and product).
*   **Binomial Theorem (Approximations):** Expansion of $(1 + x)^n$. Extremely crucial approximation used in Error Analysis and Gravitation: $(1+x)^n ¥approx 1 + nx$ when $x ¥ll 1$.
*   **Progressions:** Arithmetic Progression (AP) basics. Geometric Progression (GP) with a **major focus** on the sum of an infinite GP ($S_¥infty = ¥frac{a}{1-r}$), used in optics and electrostatics.
*   **Componendo and Dividendo:** Basic ratio rules to quickly solve equations without cross-multiplication.

## Part 2: Logarithms & Exponentials
Used heavily in Thermodynamics, Radioactivity (Nuclear Physics), and RC/LR circuits.
*   **Definition of Logarithm:** Understanding the relation between powers and logs.
*   **Types:** Common Logarithm ($¥log_{10} x$) vs Natural Logarithm ($¥ln x$ or $¥log_e x$) and how to convert them ($¥ln x ¥approx 2.303 ¥log_{10} x$).
*   **Properties & Theorems:** 
    *   Product Rule: $¥log(mn) = ¥log m + ¥log n$
    *   Quotient Rule: $¥log(m/n) = ¥log m - ¥log n$
    *   Power Rule: $¥log(m^n) = n ¥log m$
*   **Exponential Functions:** Understanding the number $e$, growth curves ($e^x$), and decay curves ($e^{-x}$).
*   **Antilogarithms:** Using log tables / basic reverse calculations.

## Part 3: Trigonometry
Crucial for resolving vectors, mechanics, waves, and optics.
*   **Angle Measurement:** Conversion between Degrees and Radians ($¥pi ¥text{ radians} = 180^¥circ$).
*   **Trigonometric Ratios:** $¥sin, ¥cos, ¥tan, ¥csc, ¥sec, ¥cot$ in a Right-Angled Triangle.
*   **Standard Values:** Values for $0^¥circ, 30^¥circ, 45^¥circ, 60^¥circ, 90^¥circ, 180^¥circ, 270^¥circ, 360^¥circ$.
*   **The Special Physics Triangle:** Ratios for $37^¥circ$ and $53^¥circ$ (the 3-4-5 triangle).
*   **ASTC Rule (Quadrant Rule):** Determining the sign of trig functions in the 1st, 2nd, 3rd, and 4th quadrants. Allied angle reductions (e.g., $¥sin(180-¥theta)$, $¥cos(90+¥theta)$).
*   **Important Identities:** $¥sin^2¥theta + ¥cos^2¥theta = 1$, etc.
*   **Addition Formulas:** $¥sin(A¥pm B)$, $¥cos(A¥pm B)$, $¥tan(A¥pm B)$.
*   **Double Angle Formulas:** $¥sin(2¥theta), ¥cos(2¥theta)$ (used everywhere in projectile motion).
*   **Small Angle Approximations:** If $¥theta$ is very small and in radians: $¥sin¥theta ¥approx ¥theta$, $¥tan¥theta ¥approx ¥theta$, $¥cos¥theta ¥approx 1$.

## Part 4: Coordinate Geometry & Graphs
Understanding equations to visualize physical movements directly in kinematics and thermodynamics.
*   **Cartesian Coordinate System:** Points in 2D space.
*   **Distance Formula:** Distance between 2 given points.
*   **Straight Lines:** Slope ($m$), Y-intercept ($c$). The universally used equation: $y = mx + c$.
*   **Recognizing Standard Graphs:**
    *   **Parabola:** $y ¥propto x^2$ and $x ¥propto y^2$ (Projectile Trajectories).
    *   **Circle:** $x^2 + y^2 = r^2$.
    *   **Ellipse:** Area of ellipse and understanding eccentricity (Kepler's Planetary Laws).
    *   **Rectangular Hyperbola:** $xy = ¥text{Constant}$ (Isothermal processes, Boyle's Law).
    *   **Sine/Cosine Waves:** Representing Simple Harmonic Motion (SHM) and sound waves.

## Part 5: Calculus - Differentiation
The mathematics of change. Critical for deriving velocity from displacement, acceleration from velocity, etc.
*   **Concept of Rate of Change:** Average vs Instantaneous rate of change.
*   **Geometrical Meaning:** Slope of the tangent to a curve at a single point ($dy/dx$).
*   **Derivatives of Standard Functions:** Constants, $x^n$, $¥sin x$, $¥cos x$, $e^x$, $¥ln x$.
*   **Rules of Differentiation:**
    *   Addition/Subtraction Rule
    *   Constant Multiple Rule
    *   Product Rule ($uv$ rule)
    *   Quotient Rule ($u/v$ rule)
*   **The Chain Rule:** Differentiating composite functions (function inside a function).
*   **Double Differentiation:** Representing acceleration, notation ($¥frac{d^2y}{dx^2}$).
*   **Application - Maxima and Minima:** 
    *   First derivative test (setting $dy/dx = 0$ to find critical points).
    *   Second derivative test (checking if it's a peak/maxima or local valley/minima).

## Part 6: Calculus - Integration
The mathematics of summation. Critical for finding total area, Work Done by variable forces, Center of Mass, etc.
*   **Concept:** The reverse process of differentiation.
*   **Geometrical Meaning:** Calculating the area under a curve.
*   **Indefinite Integration:** Formulas for integrating $x^n, ¥sin x, ¥cos x, e^x, 1/x$.
*   **Constant of Integration:** Why "$+ C$" is added in indefinite integrals.
*   **Rules of Integration:** Addition/Subtraction, multiplying by constants.
*   **Integration by Substitution:** Simplest form using chain rule reversal.
*   **Definite Integration:** Computing exact values using upper limits and lower limits ($¥int_{a}^{b} f(x)dx$).
*   **Physics Applications:** Summing up tiny masses ($dm$) to find the total mass, calculating Work Done equivalent to $¥int F ¥cdot dx$.

## Part 7: Vectors
Often taught at the beginning of physics itself, this acts as the foundational language of multidimensional physics.
*   **Definition:** Scalars vs Vectors, representation with arrows.
*   **Types of Vectors:** Unit vectors ($¥hat{i}, ¥hat{j}, ¥hat{k}$), Null vector, Coplanar, Collinear vectors.
*   **Addition and Subtraction:**
    *   Triangle Law of Vector Addition.
    *   Parallelogram Law of Vector Addition (Magnitude: $¥sqrt{A^2+B^2+2AB¥cos¥theta}$).
    *   Polygon Law for multiple vectors.
*   **Resolution of a Vector:** Breaking a vector into its $X$ and $Y$ (and $z$) mutually perpendicular components (Crucial for 2D motion).
*   **Dot Product (Scalar Product):** $A ¥cdot B = |A||B|¥cos¥theta$. Work done formula. Finding angle between two vectors.
*   **Cross Product (Vector Product):** $A ¥times B = |A||B|¥sin¥theta ¥hat{n}$. Right-Hand Thumb Rule. Used in Torque and Magnetic fields.
