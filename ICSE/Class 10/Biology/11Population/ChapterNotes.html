1. Population Explosion

Population Density: Number of individuals of a species per unit area at a given time.

Reasons for sharp rise in Human Population:

  • Better Healthcare: Reduction in Death Rate (Mortality).
  • Food Security: Availability of food due to improved agriculture.
  • Fewer Wars: Relative peace in the 20th century.
  • Lack of Education: Traditional beliefs desiring more children.

2. Demography

Key Terms:

  • Natality (Birth Rate): Number of live births per 1000 people per year.
  • Mortality (Death Rate): Number of deaths per 1000 people per year.
  • Growth Rate: (Natality - Mortality).
Practice Q1: Definitions

CONCEPT CHECK Define Population Density.

Ans: The number of individuals of a species present per unit area at a given time.

3. Consequences of Overpopulation

  • Resource Depletion: Shortage of food, water, and land.
  • Unemployment: Jobs cannot keep up with population growth.
  • Poverty: Lower standard of living.
  • Pollution: Increased waste, vehicles, and industrial activity leading to environmental degradation.

4. Population Control

A. Surgical Methods (Sterilization)

Permanent method. Prevents gamete transport.

  • Vasectomy (Males): Vas deferens is cut and tied. Sperm cannot reach urethra.
  • Tubectomy (Females): Fallopian tubes are cut and tied. Egg cannot reach uterus.

Practice Q2: Sterilization

BOARD QUESTION Name the surgical method of sterilization in males.

Ans: Vasectomy (Cutting and tying of Vas Deferens).

B. Contraceptive Methods (Temporary)

  • Barrier Methods: Condoms, Diaphragms (Prevent sperm meeting egg).
  • Chemical Methods: Oral Pills (Hormonal, prevent ovulation).
  • IUCD (Intra-Uterine Contraceptive Devices): Copper-T (Prevents implantation). Placed in Uterus.

5. Symbols

Red Triangle: Specifically inverted red triangle is the symbol for Family Welfare in India.
