3. Activity Series of Metals

Decreasing reactivity (top to bottom):

K > Na > Ca > Mg > Al > Zn > Fe > Pb > H > Cu > Hg > Ag > Au

Memory: "Please Send Charlie's Monkey After Zebras In Large Parks Harbouring Cute Harmless Silver Goldfish"

Extraction Method Based on Activity Series:

  • Highly reactive (K, Na, Ca, Mg, Al): Electrolysis of molten ore
  • Moderately reactive (Zn, Fe, Pb): Reduction with carbon/CO
  • Less reactive (Cu, Hg, Ag): Roasting or direct heating

4. Steps in Extraction of Metals

Step 1: Concentration (Dressing) of Ore

Method Used For Principle
Hydraulic Washing Heavy ores Difference in density
Magnetic Separation Magnetic ores (Fe₃O₄) Magnetic property
Froth Flotation Sulphide ores Preferential wetting

Step 2: Conversion to Oxide

Calcination: Heating ore in absence of air (for carbonates)

$ZnCO_3 \xrightarrow{\Delta} ZnO + CO_2$

Roasting: Heating ore in presence of air (for sulphides)

$2ZnS + 3O_2 \xrightarrow{\Delta} 2ZnO + 2SO_2$

Step 3: Reduction to Metal

Reduction with Carbon:

$ZnO + C \xrightarrow{\Delta} Zn + CO$

$Fe_2O_3 + 3CO \xrightarrow{\Delta} 2Fe + 3CO_2$

Electrolytic Reduction: For highly reactive metals (Al)

Step 4: Refining

Purification of metal (e.g., electro-refining for copper)

5. Extraction of Aluminium (Hall-Héroult Process)

Ore: Bauxite (Al₂O₃·2H₂O)

Step 1 - Bayer's Process: Purification of bauxite to pure Al₂O₃

Al₂O₃ + 2NaOH → 2NaAlO₂ + H₂O (dissolution)

NaAlO₂ + 2H₂O → Al(OH)₃ + NaOH (precipitation)

2Al(OH)₃ → Al₂O₃ + 3H₂O (calcination)

Electrolysis of Molten Al₂O₃:

Electrolyte: Al₂O₃ dissolved in molten cryolite (Na₃AlF₆)

Why cryolite? Lowers melting point from 2050°C to 950°C

Cathode: Carbon-lined steel tank

Anode: Graphite rods

At Cathode: Al³⁺ + 3e⁻ → Al

At Anode: 2O²⁻ → O₂ + 4e⁻

Note: Anode gets oxidised by O₂ and needs periodic replacement.

6. Alloys

Alloy: A homogeneous mixture of two or more metals, or a metal with non-metal.

Purpose: To improve properties like hardness, strength, corrosion resistance.

Alloy Composition Uses
Brass Cu + Zn Utensils, decorative items
Bronze Cu + Sn Statues, medals, coins
Stainless Steel Fe + C + Cr + Ni Surgical instruments, kitchenware
Solder Pb + Sn Joining electrical wires
Duralumin Al + Cu + Mg + Mn Aircraft bodies
Fuse Metal Bi + Pb + Sn Electric fuses
