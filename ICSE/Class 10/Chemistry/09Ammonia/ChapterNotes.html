Ammonia (NH₃)

ICSE Class 10 Chemistry • Chapter 09

1. Laboratory Preparation

Method: Heating ammonium chloride with slaked lime (calcium hydroxide)

2NH₄Cl + Ca(OH)₂ → CaCl₂ + 2H₂O + 2NH₃↑

Collection: Downward displacement of air (NH₃ is lighter than air)

Drying Agent: Quick lime (CaO) - NOT P₂O₅ or conc. H₂SO₄ (they react with NH₃)

Other methods:

From Nitrides: Mg₃N₂ + 6H₂O → 3Mg(OH)₂ + 2NH₃↑

From AlN: AlN + 3H₂O → Al(OH)₃ + NH₃↑

2. Industrial Manufacture - Haber's Process

N₂ + 3H₂ ⇌ 2NH₃ + Heat (Exothermic)

Conditions:

  • Temperature: 450-500°C (optimum)
  • Pressure: 200-300 atm (high pressure favours forward reaction)
  • Catalyst: Finely divided iron with Mo or Al₂O₃ as promoter

Why compromise temperature?

Low temp → Higher yield but SLOW reaction

High temp → LOW yield but faster equilibrium

450°C is optimum balance!

3. Physical Properties

Property Description
Colour Colourless
Odour Pungent, characteristic smell
Density Lighter than air (VD = 8.5)
Solubility Extremely soluble in water (Fountain experiment)
Nature Basic (turns red litmus blue)

Fountain Experiment

Observation: Water rises as fountain (turns pink/red with phenolphthalein)

Reason: NH₃ is highly soluble → creates vacuum → water rushes in

1 volume water dissolves 700 volumes of NH₃ at STP

4. Chemical Properties

A. Basic Nature (Weak Base)

Aqueous ammonia: NH₃ + H₂O ⇌ NH₄OH ⇌ NH₄⁺ + OH⁻

Turns red litmus blue, pink with phenolphthalein

B. Reaction with Acids (Neutralisation)

NH₃ + HCl → NH₄Cl (white fumes)

2NH₃ + H₂SO₄ → (NH₄)₂SO₄

NH₃ + HNO₃ → NH₄NO₃

C. Burning in Oxygen

4NH₃ + 3O₂ → 2N₂ + 6H₂O (without catalyst)

With Pt catalyst (catalytic oxidation):

4NH₃ + 5O₂ → 4NO + 6H₂O (Ostwald process step)

D. Reaction with Hot Copper(II) Oxide

3CuO + 2NH₃ → 3Cu + N₂ + 3H₂O

Observation: Black CuO turns reddish-brown (Cu formed)

Shows: NH₃ is a reducing agent

E. Reaction with Chlorine

Excess NH₃: 8NH₃ + 3Cl₂ → N₂ + 6NH₄Cl

Excess Cl₂: NH₃ + 3Cl₂ → NCl₃ + 3HCl (explosive)

7. Uses of Ammonia

  • Manufacture of fertilisers (urea, ammonium sulphate, ammonium nitrate)
  • Manufacture of nitric acid (Ostwald process)
  • Manufacture of explosives (TNT)
  • As a refrigerant (in ice plants)
  • In cleaning agents (household ammonia)
  • Manufacture of sodium carbonate (Solvay process)
