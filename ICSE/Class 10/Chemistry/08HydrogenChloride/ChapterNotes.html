Hydrogen Chloride (HCl)

ICSE Class 10 Chemistry • Chapter 08

1. Laboratory Preparation of HCl Gas

Method: Heating NaCl with conc. H₂SO₄

Below 200°C:

NaCl + H₂SO₄ → NaHSO₄ + HCl↑

Above 200°C:

NaCl + NaHSO₄ → Na₂SO₄ + HCl↑

Why conc. H₂SO₄?

  • Non-volatile acid displaces volatile HCl
  • HNO₃ not used - would oxidise HCl to Cl₂

Collection: By downward displacement of air (HCl is denser than air)

Drying Agent: Conc. H₂SO₄ (NOT CaO, as it reacts with HCl)

2. Properties of HCl Gas

Physical Properties

Property Description
Colour Colourless
Odour Pungent, suffocating
Density Heavier than air (VD = 18.25)
Solubility Highly soluble in water (fountain experiment)
Nature Acidic (turns blue litmus red)

The Fountain Experiment

Setup: A flask of dry HCl inverted over a trough of water with indicator

Observation: Water rushes up as a fountain, turns red (due to dissolved HCl forming acid)

Reason: HCl is extremely soluble → creates vacuum → water rushes in

1 volume of water absorbs 450 volumes of HCl at STP

Chemical Properties

1. Reaction with Ammonia

NH₃ + HCl → NH₄Cl (white fumes)

Use: Detection test for HCl - white fumes form when ammonia is brought near

3. Properties of Hydrochloric Acid (Aqueous HCl)

A. With Metals

Metal + HCl → Salt + H₂↑

Zn + 2HCl → ZnCl₂ + H₂↑

Mg + 2HCl → MgCl₂ + H₂↑

Fe + 2HCl → FeCl₂ + H₂↑

Note: Cu, Ag, Au do not react (below H in activity series)

B. With Metal Oxides

Metal Oxide + HCl → Salt + Water

CuO + 2HCl → CuCl₂ + H₂O

Fe₂O₃ + 6HCl → 2FeCl₃ + 3H₂O

C. With Metal Hydroxides

Metal Hydroxide + HCl → Salt + Water (Neutralisation)

NaOH + HCl → NaCl + H₂O

Ca(OH)₂ + 2HCl → CaCl₂ + 2H₂O

D. With Metal Carbonates & Bicarbonates

Metal Carbonate + HCl → Salt + Water + CO₂↑

CaCO₃ + 2HCl → CaCl₂ + H₂O + CO₂↑

Na₂CO₃ + 2HCl → 2NaCl + H₂O + CO₂↑

NaHCO₃ + HCl → NaCl + H₂O + CO₂↑

Observation: Brisk effervescence (CO₂ released)

E. With Metal Sulphites

Na₂SO₃ + 2HCl → 2NaCl + H₂O + SO₂↑

Observation: Suffocating smell of SO₂

F. With Metal Sulphides

FeS + 2HCl → FeCl₂ + H₂S↑

Observation: Rotten egg smell

4. Tests for HCl/Chloride Ions

Test Observation
Silver Nitrate test White curdy precipitate of AgCl, insoluble in dil. HNO₃, soluble in NH₄OH
Ammonia test Dense white fumes of NH₄Cl
MnO₂ + conc. HCl (heated) Greenish-yellow Cl₂ gas released

Confirmatory Test for Chlorides:

Solution + AgNO₃ → White ppt (AgCl)

AgCl + 2NH₄OH → [Ag(NH₃)₂]Cl + 2H₂O (ppt dissolves)

5. Uses of HCl

  • Manufacture of chlorides (like NH₄Cl, ZnCl₂)
  • Cleaning metals before galvanizing/soldering
  • In medicine (dilute HCl aids digestion)
  • In textile industry
  • As a laboratory reagent
