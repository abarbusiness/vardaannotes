Organic Chemistry

ICSE Class 10 Chemistry • Chapter 12

1. Introduction

Organic Chemistry: Study of carbon compounds (except carbonates, bicarbonates, cyanides, CO, CO₂)

Unique Properties of Carbon:

  • Tetravalency: Carbon has 4 valence electrons → can form 4 bonds
  • Catenation: Carbon atoms can link with each other to form long chains, branched chains, and rings

2. Types of Carbon Bonds

Bond Type Electrons Shared Represented As Example
Single Bond 2 C — C Ethane (C₂H₆)
Double Bond 4 C = C Ethene (C₂H₄)
Triple Bond 6 C ≡ C Ethyne (C₂H₂)

3. Homologous Series

Definition: A series of organic compounds with the same functional group and similar chemical properties, where consecutive members differ by CH₂ unit.

Characteristics:

  • Same general formula
  • Similar chemical properties
  • Gradual change in physical properties
  • Molecular mass differs by 14 u (for CH₂)

4. Hydrocarbons

Hydrocarbons: Organic compounds containing only carbon and hydrogen.

Types:

  • Saturated: Only single bonds (Alkanes)
  • Unsaturated: Contains double/triple bonds (Alkenes, Alkynes)

A. Alkanes (Saturated Hydrocarbons)

General Formula: CₙH₂ₙ₊₂

Suffix: -ane

Bond: Only single bonds (C—C)

Name Formula Structure
Methane CH₄ One C with 4 H
Ethane C₂H₆ CH₃—CH₃
Propane C₃H₈ CH₃—CH₂—CH₃
Butane C₄H₁₀ CH₃—CH₂—CH₂—CH₃

Reactions of Alkanes

Combustion: CH₄ + 2O₂ → CO₂ + 2H₂O + Heat

Substitution (with Cl₂ in sunlight):

CH₄ + Cl₂ → CH₃Cl + HCl (methyl chloride)

CH₃Cl + Cl₂ → CH₂Cl₂ + HCl (dichloromethane)

B. Alkenes (Unsaturated - Double Bond)

General Formula: CₙH₂ₙ

Suffix: -ene

Bond: Contains C=C double bond

Name Formula Structure
Ethene C₂H₄ CH₂=CH₂
Propene C₃H₆ CH₃—CH=CH₂

Reactions of Alkenes

Addition of H₂ (Hydrogenation):

C₂H₄ + H₂ → C₂H₆ (Ni catalyst, 200°C)

Addition of Br₂ (Bromine Test):

C₂H₄ + Br₂ → C₂H₄Br₂ (decolourises brown bromine water)

Addition of HCl:

C₂H₄ + HCl → C₂H₅Cl (chloroethane)

Test for Unsaturation: Compound decolourises bromine water (turns from brown to colourless)

C. Alkynes (Unsaturated - Triple Bond)

General Formula: CₙH₂ₙ₋₂

Suffix: -yne

Bond: Contains C≡C triple bond

Name Formula Structure
Ethyne (Acetylene) C₂H₂ CH≡CH
Propyne C₃H₄ CH₃—C≡CH

Preparation of Ethyne

CaC₂ + 2H₂O → Ca(OH)₂ + C₂H₂↑

(Calcium carbide + Water → Slaked lime + Acetylene)

5. Alcohols

General Formula: CₙH₂ₙ₊₁OH

Functional Group: —OH (Hydroxyl group)

Suffix: -ol

Ethanol (C₂H₅OH)

Common Name: Ethyl alcohol, Grain alcohol

Physical Properties: Colourless liquid, pleasant smell, miscible with water

Preparation of Ethanol

By Hydration of Ethene:

C₂H₄ + H₂O → C₂H₅OH (conc. H₂SO₄, 300°C)

By Fermentation:

C₆H₁₂O₆ → 2C₂H₅OH + 2CO₂ (yeast, 25-30°C)

Chemical Properties of Ethanol

Combustion: C₂H₅OH + 3O₂ → 2CO₂ + 3H₂O

With Na: 2C₂H₅OH + 2Na → 2C₂H₅ONa + H₂↑

Dehydration: C₂H₅OH → C₂H₄ + H₂O (conc. H₂SO₄, 170°C)

Esterification: C₂H₅OH + CH₃COOH → CH₃COOC₂H₅ + H₂O

Denatured Alcohol: Ethanol made unfit for drinking by adding methanol and pyridine.

Methylated Spirit: Ethanol + Methanol (poisonous)

6. Carboxylic Acids

General Formula: CₙH₂ₙ₊₁COOH

Functional Group: —COOH (Carboxyl group)

Suffix: -oic acid

Acetic Acid (CH₃COOH)

Common Name: Ethanoic acid, Vinegar (5-8% solution)

Pure Form: Glacial acetic acid (freezes at 16.6°C)

Smell: Pungent vinegar smell

Chemical Properties

Acidic Nature (turns litmus red):

CH₃COOH + NaOH → CH₃COONa + H₂O

With Carbonates:

2CH₃COOH + Na₂CO₃ → 2CH₃COONa + H₂O + CO₂↑

Esterification:

CH₃COOH + C₂H₅OH → CH₃COOC₂H₅ + H₂O (ethyl acetate - fruity smell)

8. Isomerism

Isomers: Compounds with same molecular formula but different structural arrangements.

Example: Butane (C₄H₁₀) has two isomers: n-butane and iso-butane
