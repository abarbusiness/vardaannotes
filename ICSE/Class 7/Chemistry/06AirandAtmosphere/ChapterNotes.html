6.1 Introduction to the Atmosphere

The atmosphere is the thick blanket of air that seamlessly surrounds the Earth and extends upwards to several hundreds of kilometers into space. It is securely held to the Earth by the powerful force of gravity.

Layers of the Atmosphere

Scientists categorically divide the vast atmosphere into five major structural layers based precisely on temperature variations uniquely found at distinct altitudes:

  1. Troposphere (0 - 15 km): The heavily dense innermost layer closest to the Earth's surface. Virtually all weather phenomena (clouds, heavy rain, fierce storms) uniquely occur here. It intensely contains precisely $75\%$ of the total atmospheric mass purely consisting of essential gases and heavy water vapour.
  2. Stratosphere (15 - 50 km): A perfectly calm, severely clear layer situated immediately above the troposphere. Heavy commercial jets commonly fly strictly in the lower stratosphere specifically to intelligently avoid stormy weather. Most importantly, it tightly contains the Ozone Layer ($O_3$), which acts as a powerful global shield effectively absorbing extremely lethal Ultraviolet (UV) radiation efficiently radiating continuously from the scorching Sun.
  3. Mesosphere (50 - 85 km): The coldest layer in the entire atmosphere securely where temperature dramatically plunges. Meteors strongly entering safely from deep space intensely rigorously burn up fiercely here due to intense violent friction.
  4. Thermosphere (85 - 600 km): Temperatures aggressively rise rapidly. It features the Ionosphere, containing powerfully charged gas particles reflecting correctly radio waves safely back to Earth, assisting global communication efficiently.
  5. Exosphere (Above 600 km): The outermost layer that exceptionally slowly fades entirely into the infinite vacuum of deep space safely quietly.

    6.3 Exact Composition of Air

    Air is fundamentally a highly variable physical mixture, definitively not a pure chemical compound. Its precise composition was historically established famously by Antoine Lavoisier.

    Percentage Composition by Volume

    Gas Component Volume Percentage Nature
    Nitrogen ($N_2$) $78.1\%$ Highly unreactive, acts as a crucial diluent.
    Oxygen ($O_2$) $20.9\%$ Active supporter of combustion and organic life.
    Argon (Noble Gas) $0.9\%$ Completely inert (chemically non-reactive).
    Carbon Dioxide ($CO_2$) $0.03\% - 0.04\%$ Raw material for botanical photosynthesis.
    Water Vapour, Dust, Smoke Highly variable trace amounts Dictates weather patterns, humidity, and pollution levels.

    6.4 Role and Importance of Atmospheric Gases

    1. Nitrogen ($N_2$)

    Being highly chemically inert, its primary crucial role is to solidly dilute the violently reactive Oxygen. If the air was $100\%$ pure Oxygen, even a tiny spark would instantly cause an uncontrollable, unextinguishable massive global fire.

    The Nitrogen Cycle: Nitrogen is an essential building block for strong proteins found densely inside plants and animals. However, complex organisms completely cannot absorb raw Nitrogen gas directly from the air. It seamlessly must be biologically 'fixed' into usable solid Nitrates ($NO_3$) securely in the remote soil by highly specialized Nitrogen-fixing bacteria (like Rhizobium located firmly inside the root nodules of heavy leguminous plants) or naturally through massive atmospheric lightning strikes.

    2. Oxygen ($O_2$)

    Oxygen is strictly essential for aerobic respiration in all living terrestrial and aquatic organisms. Aquatic life perfectly utilizes the tiny amounts of oxygen naturally dissolved in deep water for their mandatory survival.

    3. Carbon Dioxide ($CO_2$)

    Though composing only a tiny $0.03\%$, its role is incredibly fundamental. Green plants absorb atmospheric $CO_2$ and combine it with liquid water in the chemical presence of bright sunlight to synthesize powerful glucose food. $CO_2$ also actively traps reflected infrared heat radiation naturally escaping from the Earth. This natural insulation (Greenhouse Effect) heavily keeps the Earth warm enough to gracefully sustain liquid water and life. (However, extreme excess $CO_2$ rapidly leads to dangerous Global Warming).

    6.5 Air Pollution

    Air Pollution: The contamination of the natural atmosphere by the introduction of undesirable harmful chemical gases, toxic smoke, and dangerous particulate matter in quantities massive enough to severely injure living organisms, destroy delicate crops, and corrode human infrastructure.

    Major Pollutants and Their Severe Effects

    • Carbon Monoxide ($CO$): Highly toxic gas from incomplete combustion of petrol. It aggressively binds heavily with the haemoglobin in our red blood cells $200$ times faster than life-saving oxygen, forcefully preventing oxygen transport and causing fatal suffocation.
    • Sulphur Dioxide ($SO_2$) and Nitrogen Oxides ($NO_x$): Released from burning impure coal. They dissolve in rain clouds to form deadly Acid Rain (Sulphuric acid and Nitric acid), which severely corrodes historical marble monuments (marble cancer) and fatally acidifies freshwater lakes.
    • Suspended Particulate Matter (SPM): Tiny unburnt carbon smoke that rapidly causes severe chronic respiratory illnesses like asthma.
    • Chlorofluorocarbons (CFCs): Once used in old refrigerators, they slowly float up to the Stratosphere and violently destroy the protective Ozone layer, creating an "Ozone Hole" that allows cancer-causing UV radiation to brutally hit the Earth.
    Practice Zone

    Q1. Why do deep sea divers and mountaineers carry specialized compressed oxygen cylinders?

    Ans: As altitude actively increases on tall mountains, the air density severely decreases, resulting in extremely thin air with heavily insufficient oxygen for normal human breathing. Deep underwater, humans cannot possibly comfortably breathe the tiny amounts of dissolved oxygen (like fish do with specialized gills), so highly compressed dense oxygen cylinders are absolutely mandatory for simple survival.

    Q2. Explain how Acid Rain is formed and state one harmful effect.

    Ans: Acid rain is strictly formed when harmful toxic industrial pollutants like Sulphur Dioxide ($SO_2$) and Nitrogen Dioxide ($NO_2$) chemically react heavily with natural atmospheric water vapour inside large clouds to actively form highly corrosive Sulphuric Acid and Nitric Acid. When it falls, it severely forcefully damages the pristine white marble of ancient historical monuments like the Taj Mahal, causing severe 'marble cancer'.
