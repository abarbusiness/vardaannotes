9.1 Crop Production Basics

Crop: When plants of the exact same kind are grown and cultivated on a massive scale at one single place, it is formally called a crop.

In India, crops are broadly classified into two primary seasonal categories based on exactly when they are physically planted and harvested.

  • Kharif Crops (Monsoon): Placed into the rainy soil in June and harvested in September. They essentially require a large amount of water. Examples: Paddy (rice), maize, soybean, and cotton.
  • Rabi Crops (Winter): Sown cleanly in October and harvested in March. They grow well in cool, dry weather. Examples: Wheat, gram, peas, and mustard.

9.2 Important Agricultural Practices

Farmers sequentially perform a specific series of scientific steps over an entire season to ensure a rich, successful harvest.

  1. Preparation of Soil: The soil must be turned deeply and loosened completely using a physical plough. This permits plant roots to easily penetrate deep down and cleanly breathe oxygen trapped inside the loose soil.
  2. Sowing of Seeds: Good quality, healthy, disease-free seeds are physically selected and meticulously planted specifically at the correct depth and distance to prevent harsh competition for sunlight and nutrients.
  3. Adding Manure and Fertilizers: Continuous, endless farming completely drains the natural soil of essential chemical nutrients. Farmers systematically replenish it. Manure is natural decomposing plant waste. Fertilizers are synthetic, factory-made harsh chemicals fundamentally rich in NPK (Nitrogen, Phosphorus, Potassium).
  4. Irrigation: The controlled, artificial supply of essential water to agricultural fields explicitly at regular time intervals. Modern techniques like precise Drip Irrigation completely save water by dripping it distinctly at the specific root zone.
  5. Protecting from Weeds: Weeds are completely unwanted entirely useless wild plants growing naturally alongside the main crop. They steal vital nutrients and sunlight. They are removed manually or chemically killed strictly using weedicides.
  6. Harvesting & Storage: The cutting and formal gathering of the fully mature crop. The grains must be meticulously dried perfectly in the hot sun and cleanly stored securely inside giant metal silos to prevent tragic spoiling by rats, fungi, and dangerous insects.

9.4 Animal Husbandry

Just like crops, specific domestic animals correctly provide essential nutritional food like fresh milk, rich eggs, and protein-heavy meat.

Animal Husbandry: The scientific, highly organized agricultural practice explicitly involving the selective breeding, expert feeding, clean sheltering, and medical caring of livestock animals specifically raised for severe commercial human use.

  • Dairy Farming: Raising milk-yielding animals (Milch breeds) like strong buffaloes and healthy cows.
