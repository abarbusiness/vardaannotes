üåç Introduction

The Gupta Empire (c. 320‚Äì550 CE) is called the "Golden Age of India" because of unprecedented achievements in science, mathematics, astronomy, religion, art, and literature. The empire was founded by Sri Gupta and reached its peak under Chandragupta I, Samudragupta, and Chandragupta II (Vikramaditya).

üìú PART 1: Sources

  • Account of Fa-Hien (Faxian): A Chinese Buddhist monk who visited India during the reign of Chandragupta II (c. 399‚Äì411 CE). His travel account (Fo-kuo-chi) describes the prosperity of the Gupta period ‚Äî well-governed, low taxes, free hospitals, and Buddhist monasteries. He visited Pataliputra, Mathura, and Kapilavastu.
  • Allahabad Pillar Inscription (Prayagprasasti): A eulogy inscribed on one of Ashoka's original pillars at Prayagraj (Allahabad) by the poet Harishena. It records the military conquests of Samudragupta in great detail ‚Äî listing the kings he defeated across India. It is the most important source for Samudragupta's reign.

üèõÔ∏è PART 3: Administration

  • The Gupta king bore grandiose titles ‚Äî Maharajadhiraja (king of kings), Paramabhattaraka (great lord).
  • Administration was more decentralised than the Mauryas ‚Äî provincial governors (uparikas) had significant autonomy. Village headmen (gramapati) managed local affairs.
  • The army relied more on feudal levies from subordinate kings than a permanent standing army.
  • Fa-Hien notes that the Gupta state was mild ‚Äî no rigid controls, taxes were light, and there were no death penalties for ordinary crimes.

üéì PART 4: Contributions to Education and Science

Education ‚Äì Nalanda University

  • Nalanda University (near Patna, Bihar) ‚Äî Founded by Kumaragupta I. One of the world's first great universities. Had thousands of students from across Asia studying Buddhism, logic, medicine, mathematics, and grammar. Fa-Hien and later Xuanzang (Hiuen Tsang) described it.

Science ‚Äì Aryabhatta

  • Aryabhatta (born 476 CE) ‚Äî The greatest mathematician-astronomer of ancient India.
    • Wrote the Aryabhatiya (499 CE) ‚Äî a comprehensive treatise on mathematics and astronomy.
    • Calculated the value of œÄ (pi) as approximately 3.1416.
    • Explained that the Earth rotates on its own axis (heliocentric idea ‚Äî 1,000 years before Copernicus in Europe).
    • Correct explanation of solar and lunar eclipses (caused by shadows).
    • Worked on algebra, trigonometry, and place-value number system.

Medicine ‚Äì Sushruta

  • Sushruta ‚Äî Ancient surgeon; wrote the Sushruta Samhita. Known as the "Father of Surgery." Performed cataract operations, plastic surgery (nasal reconstruction), and described over 300 surgical procedures and 120 surgical instruments. Became prominent during the Gupta period.

üé® PART 5: Art, Literature and Culture

Kalidasa and Gupta Literature

  • Kalidasa ‚Äî The greatest Sanskrit poet and playwright; one of the Navaratnas at Vikramaditya's court.
    • Plays: Shakuntala (Abhijnanashakuntalam ‚Äî translated into European languages in 1789 by William Jones; inspired Goethe), Malavikagnimitra, Vikramorvasiyam.
    • Poems: Meghaduta (Cloud Messenger), Kumarasambhava, Raghuvamsha.

Art ‚Äì Ajanta Caves and Deogarh Temple

  • Ajanta Caves (Aurangabad, Maharashtra) ‚Äî 30 rock-cut caves with magnificent Buddhist paintings and sculptures. Phases 1 and 2 of paintings belong to the Gupta period. The paintings depict Jataka stories and life of the Buddha in breathtaking naturalistic detail.
  • Deogarh Temple (Tikamgarh, MP) ‚Äî The Dasavatara Temple; one of the earliest surviving Gupta-era temples. Notable for high-relief sculptures of Vishnu ‚Äî especially the Anantashayana panel (Vishnu reclining on the cosmic serpent Ananta).
