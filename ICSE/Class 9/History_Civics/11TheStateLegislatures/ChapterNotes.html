üìñ PART 1: Introduction ‚Äì State Legislature

State Legislature is the law-making body at the state level. Every state in India has a State Legislature. Most states have a unicameral legislature (one house: Vidhan Sabha). Some states also have a bicameral legislature (two houses: Vidhan Sabha + Vidhan Parishad).
  • States with Vidhan Parishad (Legislative Council): Uttar Pradesh, Maharashtra, Bihar, Karnataka, Andhra Pradesh, Telangana (6 states). All others have only the Vidhan Sabha.
  • A new Vidhan Parishad can be created or abolished by Parliament on the recommendation of the concerned state's Vidhan Sabha (Art. 169).

üèõÔ∏è PART 2: Vidhan Sabha (State Legislative Assembly)

Composition

  • The Vidhan Sabha is the Lower House / Popular House of the State Legislature.
  • Maximum strength: 500 members; minimum: 60 members (exception: Sikkim has 32, Goa has 40 ‚Äî allowed by constitutional provisions).
  • Members are directly elected by the people of the state through universal adult franchise and the First-Past-The-Post (plurality) system ‚Äî same as Lok Sabha elections.
  • The President can nominate 1 member from the Anglo-Indian community if they feel the community is under-represented (though this provision was removed by the 104th Amendment, 2020).

Qualifications to be a Member of Vidhan Sabha (MLA)

  • Must be a citizen of India.
  • Must be at least 25 years of age.
  • Must be registered as a voter in the state.
  • Must not hold any office of profit under the government.
  • Must not be of unsound mind or an undischarged insolvent (bankrupt).

Duration of Vidhan Sabha

  • Normal term: 5 years from its first sitting (same as Lok Sabha).
  • Can be dissolved earlier by the Governor on the advice of the Chief Minister (or under President's Rule).
  • Can be extended during a national emergency ‚Äî but not beyond 6 months after the emergency ends.

Speaker and Deputy Speaker of Vidhan Sabha

  • The Vidhan Sabha elects its own Speaker and Deputy Speaker from among its members.
  • The Speaker presides over the sessions of the Vidhan Sabha, maintains order, and interprets the rules of procedure. Like the Lok Sabha Speaker, the Speaker casts a decisive vote only in the event of a tie.
  • The Speaker can be removed by a resolution passed by a majority of the then members of the Vidhan Sabha ‚Äî a 14-day notice must be given.

Sessions of Vidhan Sabha

  • The Vidhan Sabha must meet at least twice a year. The gap between two sessions must not exceed 6 months.
  • Sessions are convened by the Governor. Three kinds of sessions: Budget Session, Monsoon Session, Winter Session (like Parliament).

Powers and Functions of Vidhan Sabha

1. Legislative Powers

  • The Vidhan Sabha makes laws on State List subjects (66 subjects ‚Äî e.g., police, public health, agriculture, land) and on Concurrent List subjects (shared with Parliament ‚Äî e.g., education, forests, marriage).
  • An ordinary bill can be introduced in either house (in bicameral states) but it must be passed by both.

2. Financial Powers

  • The State Budget is presented in and passed by the Vidhan Sabha (not the Vidhan Parishad). Money Bills originate only in the Vidhan Sabha.
  • The Vidhan Parishad can delay a Money Bill by a maximum of 14 days but cannot reject it. After 14 days, it is deemed passed.

3. Executive (Control) Powers

  • The Council of Ministers (state cabinet) is collectively responsible to the Vidhan Sabha ‚Äî if the Vidhan Sabha passes a Vote of No Confidence against the Cabinet, the Chief Minister and all ministers must resign.
  • MLAs can question ministers (Question Hour), move adjournment motions, and move resolutions to hold the government accountable.

4. Electoral Powers

  • Elected members of the Vidhan Sabha participate in the election of the President of India (Article 54).
  • They also elect members of the Rajya Sabha from their state (1/3rd of state seats in Rajya Sabha retire every 2 years).

5. Amending Powers

  • When Parliament proposes to amend certain provisions of the Constitution, the amendment must be ratified by at least half the state legislatures. The Vidhan Sabha of each such state votes on ratification.

üèõÔ∏è PART 3: Vidhan Parishad (State Legislative Council)

Composition

  • The Vidhan Parishad is the Upper House of the State Legislature (in bicameral states).
  • Its strength cannot exceed 1/3rd of the total membership of the Vidhan Sabha of that state, and cannot be less than 40 members.
  • The members of Vidhan Parishad are not directly elected by the people. They are chosen through various indirect methods:
    • 1/3rd elected by MLAs (members of the Vidhan Sabha).
    • 1/3rd elected by local bodies (municipalities, district boards).
    • 1/12th elected by registered graduates of at least 3 years' standing in the state.
    • 1/12th elected by teachers (secondary school level and above, at least 3 years' standing).
    • 1/6th nominated by the Governor ‚Äî from persons with special knowledge/experience in literature, science, art, cooperative movement, or social service.

Qualifications for Membership of Vidhan Parishad (MLC)

  • Must be a citizen of India.
  • Must be at least 30 years of age (higher than Vidhan Sabha's 25).
  • Other disqualifications same as Vidhan Sabha.

Duration of Vidhan Parishad

  • The Vidhan Parishad is a permanent body ‚Äî it cannot be dissolved.
  • 1/3rd of its members retire every 2 years (similar to Rajya Sabha ‚Äî ensuring continuity).
  • Each member serves a 6-year term.

Powers of Vidhan Parishad

  • Can delay an ordinary bill by a maximum of 3 months (4 months if sent a second time). If disagreement continues, the Vidhan Sabha's decision prevails.
  • Can delay a Money Bill by only 14 days.
  • Does not control the executive ‚Äî the Council of Ministers is responsible only to the Vidhan Sabha, not the Vidhan Parishad.

üìå Chapter Summary

  • State Legislature makes laws for the state; most states are unicameral (only Vidhan Sabha); 6 states are bicameral (Vidhan Sabha + Vidhan Parishad).
  • Vidhan Sabha: directly elected; 5-year term; controls the executive (CM responsible to it); has final say on Money Bills; presided by Speaker.
  • Vidhan Parishad: permanent house; indirectly elected + gubernatorial nominees; 6-year member terms; can only delay bills; presided by Chairman.
  • Key difference in age: Vidhan Sabha = 25; Vidhan Parishad = 30 (like Rajya Sabha).
