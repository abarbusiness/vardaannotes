üåê PART 2: Shape of the Earth

The Earth is neither a perfect sphere nor a flat disc. It is an oblate spheroid (also called a geoid) ‚Äî slightly flattened at the poles and bulging at the equator.
  • Equatorial radius: ~6,378 km (longest radius ‚Äî at equator).
  • Polar radius: ~6,357 km (shorter ‚Äî at poles).
  • The difference is about 21 km ‚Äî making the Earth about 1/298 flatter at the poles than at the equator.
  • This shape is due to Earth's rotation ‚Äî centrifugal force causes material to bulge outward at the equator.

The Blue Planet

  • Earth is called the "Blue Planet" because 71% of its surface is covered by oceans/water ‚Äî which appear blue from space.
  • Water is essential for all life and for moderating climate through the water cycle.

The Green Planet

  • Earth is also sometimes called the "Green Planet" because of its rich plant life and biosphere ‚Äî forests, grasslands, and vegetation spanning 29% of its land surface.
  • Plants produce oxygen through photosynthesis and form the base of the food chain for all animal life.
